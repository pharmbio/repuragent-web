=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a practical, *experiment-focused* list of 20 repurposable or non‑AML‑approved drugs reported to have antiproliferative activity in AML (with particular relevance to FLT3‑ITD–positive lines like MOLM‑13 when possible). For each, I give:

- Mechanism / rationale  
- Suggested **IC₅₀ assay concentration range** (final concentrations in wells), assuming 72‑hr viability/proliferation assays in MOLM‑13 or similar AML lines.

Use these ranges as **starting points** for titrations, not as definitive potency values; actual IC₅₀s will depend on serum, cell density, exposure time, etc.

---

## General in‑vitro assay guidance (for all below)

- Cell line: MOLM‑13 (FLT3‑ITD, highly proliferative)  
- Assay: 72 h CellTiter‑Glo / MTT / resazurin or similar  
- Initial test range for small molecules: **0.1 nM – 30 µM** in ~8–10 serial 3‑fold dilutions  
- Plate: 96‑ or 384‑well, 3–6k cells/well (96‑well as reference)  
- Vehicle: 0.1% DMSO max in all wells including controls  

Volumes below are given as **concentration ranges to include in your dilution series**.

---

## A. Kinase inhibitors and targeted agents (already on the market but not originally for AML)

### 1. Sorafenib  
- Type: Multikinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR, FLT3)  
- Rationale: Inhibits FLT3‑ITD; known activity in FLT3‑mutant AML including MOLM‑13.  
- Test range: **1 nM – 10 µM** (e.g., 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM).

### 2. Sunitinib  
- Type: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, VEGFR, PDGFR)  
- Rationale: FLT3 inhibition; antiproliferative in FLT3‑ITD AML.  
- Test range: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

### 3. Midostaurin* (PKC/FLT3 inhibitor; FDA‑approved for AML but originally developed as a broad kinase inhibitor)  
- Type: Multikinase (FLT3, PKC, KIT)  
- Rationale: Good reference FLT3 inhibitor in MOLM‑13; use as positive control and repurposing benchmark.  
- Test range: **0.5 nM – 5 µM**.

### 4. Nintedanib  
- Type: VEGFR/FGFR/PDGFR inhibitor; approved for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis and some cancers  
- Rationale: Shown to inhibit AML cells and leukemic stem/progenitor compartments in some preclinical work.  
- Test range: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

### 5. Dasatinib  
- Type: SRC/ABL inhibitor; approved in CML/ALL  
- Rationale: Inhibits SRC family and some KIT/FLT3‑related signaling; reported activity in some AML models.  
- Test range: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

### 6. Imatinib  
- Type: BCR‑ABL/KIT/PDGFR inhibitor (CML/GIST)  
- Rationale: Limited direct AML efficacy, but can inhibit KIT‑dependent AML subtypes; useful as a comparator.  
- Test range: **10 nM – 20 µM**.

### 7. Trametinib  
- Type: MEK1/2 inhibitor (melanoma)  
- Rationale: AML often depends on RAS/RAF/MEK/ERK signaling; MEK inhibition can reduce proliferation and sensitize to other agents.  
- Test range: **0.1 nM – 3 µM** (MOLM‑13 often sensitive in low‑nM range).

### 8. Everolimus  
- Type: mTORC1 inhibitor (solid tumors, transplant)  
- Rationale: PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway active in AML; everolimus inhibits proliferation and enhances apoptosis in AML cell lines.  
- Test range: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

### 9. Bortezomib  
- Type: 26S proteasome inhibitor (multiple myeloma, MCL)  
- Rationale: Induces apoptosis and disrupts NF‑κB signaling in AML cells, including FLT3‑ITD lines.  
- Test range: **0.5 nM – 1 µM** (often low‑nM IC₅₀).

### 10. Carfilzomib  
- Type: Irreversible proteasome inhibitor (myeloma)  
- Rationale: Similar rationale as bortezomib; potent apoptosis induction in AML lines.  
- Test range: **0.5 nM – 1 µM**.

## B. Epigenetic / transcription‑related drugs

### 11. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- Type: HDAC inhibitor (T‑cell lymphoma)  
- Rationale: AML cells are HDAC‑dependent; vorinostat induces differentiation/apoptosis and synergizes with many AML drugs.  
- Test range: **50 nM – 20 µM** (start around 0.05, 0.15, 0.5, 1.5, 5, 15 µM).

### 12. Panobinostat  
- Type: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor (myeloma)  
- Rationale: Very potent HDAC inhibitor; strong antiproliferative effects in AML models.  
- Test range: **0.5 nM – 1 µM**.

### 13. Belinostat  
- Type: HDAC inhibitor (peripheral T‑cell lymphoma)  
- Rationale: Similar to vorinostat/panobinostat; broad anti‑leukemic epigenetic modulation.  
- Test range: **10 nM – 10 µM**.

### 14. Decitabine* (hypomethylating agent; already AML/MDS drug but originally developed as an antimetabolite)  
- Type: DNMT inhibitor (nucleoside analog)  
- Rationale: Gold‑standard epigenetic comparator; used at low micromolar in vitro to induce DNA hypomethylation and growth inhibition.  
- Test range: **0.1 µM – 20 µM** (be mindful of instability; refresh if using >24 h assays).

### 15. Azacitidine* (also an HMA, repurposed from older cytotoxic use)  
- Type: DNMT inhibitor / ribonucleoside analog  
- Rationale: Widely used; reference for epigenetic therapy and combination testing.  
- Test range: **0.5 µM – 50 µM**.

---

## C. Metabolic / signaling modulators

### 16. Metformin  
- Type: Biguanide; AMPK activator / mitochondrial complex I inhibitor (diabetes)  
- Rationale: Targets leukemia cell metabolism, decreases oxidative phosphorylation; often needs higher in‑vitro concentrations than plasma levels.  
- Test range: **0.1 mM – 20 mM** (100 µM – 20,000 µM).

### 17. Ibrutinib  
- Type: BTK inhibitor (CLL, MCL, WM)  
- Rationale: BTK and related kinases contribute to survival signaling in some myeloid leukemias; preclinical AML data exist.  
- Test range: **10 nM – 20 µM**.

### 18. Venetoclax* (BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved for AML now, but originally developed for CLL)  
- Type: BCL‑2 inhibitor  
- Rationale: Strong activity in AML, particularly in combination with HMAs; excellent positive control for apoptosis in MOLM‑13.  
- Test range: **0.1 nM – 3 µM** (often low‑nM IC₅₀).

---

## D. Immunomodulatory / other repurposable drugs

### 19. Thalidomide  
- Type: Immunomodulatory drug (IMiD; multiple myeloma)  
- Rationale: Anti‑angiogenic and immunomodulatory; direct AML cell killing is moderate, but worth profiling in vitro.  
- Test range: **0.5 µM – 50 µM**.

### 20. Lenalidomide  
- Type: IMiD (myeloma, MDS with del(5q))  
- Rationale: Shows variable but sometimes significant anti‑leukemic activity; also modulates microenvironment‑dependent survival.  
- Test range: **0.1 µM – 50 µM**.

---

## Suggested experimental design for an initial MOLM‑13 screen

- Plate MOLM‑13 at 3,000–6,000 cells/well (96‑well), 100 µL RPMI + 10% FBS.  
- Prepare **10‑point 3‑fold serial dilutions** for each compound within the ranges above.  
- Incubate 72 h; read luminescent viability (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo).  
- Include:
  - DMSO control (0.1%)  
  - Positive controls: midostaurin (FLT3), bortezomib, venetoclax  
- Fit dose–response curves (4‑parameter logistic) to obtain IC₅₀ values.
