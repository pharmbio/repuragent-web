=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing, non–AML-approved drugs with published activity against AML cell lines (including FLT3-ITD–dependent MOLM13) and suggested concentration ranges appropriate for generating IC50 curves in a standard cell-proliferation/viability assay. Ranges are chosen to bracket reported potencies while accounting for serum binding and cell-line variability. Use a multi-point serial dilution spanning these ranges; keep DMSO constant across conditions. Notes flag key caveats (e.g., cofactor requirement, solubility).

- Ponatinib (CML/Ph+ ALL; multi-kinase/FLT3 inhibitor)
  Rationale: Potent against FLT3-ITD; strong activity in MOLM13.
  Suggested range: 0.03–300 nM.

- Sorafenib (HCC/RCC; multi-kinase/FLT3 inhibitor)
  Rationale: Inhibits FLT3-ITD signaling; inhibits MOLM13 proliferation.
  Suggested range: 3–3000 nM.

- Sunitinib (RCC; multi-kinase/FLT3 inhibitor)
  Rationale: Reported anti-FLT3 activity; inhibits FLT3-ITD AML cells.
  Suggested range: 10–10,000 nM.

- Cabozantinib (RCC/thyroid; multi-kinase including FLT3)
  Rationale: Inhibits FLT3 and downstream signaling in AML lines.
  Suggested range: 1–1000 nM.

- Regorafenib (CRC/GIST; multi-kinase similar to sorafenib)
  Rationale: Targets FLT3 and related kinases; AML cell activity reported.
  Suggested range: 10–3000 nM.

- Dasatinib (CML/Ph+ ALL; SRC/ABL inhibitor)
  Rationale: Inhibits SRC-family signaling and has activity in AML lines; can blunt FLT3 pathways.
  Suggested range: 3–10,000 nM.

- Fostamatinib (ITP; SYK inhibitor)
  Rationale: SYK contributes to AML survival; activity in FLT3-ITD AML and MOLM13 reported.
  Suggested range: 0.3–30 μM.

- Bortezomib (multiple myeloma; proteasome inhibitor)
  Rationale: Induces apoptosis in AML lines including MOLM13.
  Suggested range: 0.5–100 nM.

- Panobinostat (myeloma; pan-HDAC inhibitor)
  Rationale: Broad transcriptional repression; potent anti-AML activity.
  Suggested range: 0.3–100 nM.

- Vorinostat/SAHA (CTCL; HDAC inhibitor)
  Rationale: Induces differentiation/apoptosis; active in AML lines.
  Suggested range: 0.3–30 μM.

- Tigecycline (antibiotic)
  Rationale: Inhibits mitochondrial translation; selectively impairs AML (including stem-like) cells; activity in MOLM13 reported.
  Suggested range: 0.5–50 μM.

- Auranofin (rheumatoid arthritis; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor)
  Rationale: Induces ROS and apoptosis in AML lines including MOLM13.
  Suggested range: 0.05–5 μM.

- Disulfiram + copper (alcohol aversion; ALDH/proteasome; requires Cu2+)
  Rationale: DSF-Cu complex is cytotoxic to AML including FLT3-ITD; stem-cell targeting reported.
  Suggested range: Disulfiram 0.03–3 μM with 1 μM Cu2+ held constant.

- Niclosamide (anthelmintic; Wnt/STAT3/OXPHOS modulator)
  Rationale: Broad anti-leukemic signaling inhibition; activity in AML lines.
  Suggested range: 0.1–10 μM. Note: low aqueous solubility; ensure adequate solubilization.

- Mebendazole (anthelmintic; microtubule depolymerizer)
  Rationale: Anti-mitotic effects in AML cells; reported MOLM13 sensitivity.
  Suggested range: 0.1–10 μM. Note: solubility/serum binding considerations.

- Thioridazine (antipsychotic; dopamine receptor antagonist)
  Rationale: AML stem/progenitor targeting; induces AML cell death including MOLM13.
  Suggested range: 0.3–30 μM. Note: cardiotoxicity risk clinically.

- Pimozide (antipsychotic; STAT5/ion channels)
  Rationale: Inhibits STAT5 downstream of FLT3-ITD; activity in MOLM13.
  Suggested range: 0.1–10 μM.

- Ciclopirox (antifungal; iron chelation/eIF5A hypusination blockade)
  Rationale: Induces AML apoptosis; clinical-stage repositioning.
  Suggested range: 0.1–10 μM.

- Simvastatin (dyslipidemia; HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor)
  Rationale: Mevalonate-pathway dependence in AML; reported activity in FLT3-ITD cells.
  Suggested range: 0.3–30 μM. Note: use active acid form; serum lipids can reduce free drug.

- Clofazimine (leprosy; redox/Kv1.3/Wnt modulation)
  Rationale: Cytotoxic to AML lines; reported low-μM to sub-μM potency.
  Suggested range: 0.03–3 μM. Note: highly lipophilic; adsorption to plastic/serum binding can affect free levels.

Practical notes (high-level):
- MOLM13 is FLT3-ITD–dependent; FLT3-targeting agents (e.g., ponatinib, sorafenib, sunitinib, cabozantinib, regorafenib) should show low-nanomolar to low-micromolar IC50s.
- Use a broad, multi-log dilution series that spans each suggested range; keep vehicle constant across wells.
- Some agents have solubility or binding constraints (niclosamide, mebendazole, clofazimine, simvastatin). Confirm compound handling and consider serum effects when interpreting apparent potency.
- For disulfiram, include copper as indicated; without copper, activity in AML cells is markedly reduced.

These ranges are intended to capture expected potencies across AML cell models and should be refined based on preliminary runs in your specific assay setup.
