=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 approved drugs (from other indications) with published or commonly reported antiproliferative activity in AML models and/or plausible mechanistic rationale for MOLM-13 (FLT3-ITD) inhibition. For each, a practical concentration range is suggested for determining IC50 in MOLM-13. These ranges bracket typical cellular potencies reported in hematologic malignancy models; adjust higher if no effect is observed at the top of the range.

FLT3/multikinase TKIs (often most potent in MOLM-13 due to FLT3-ITD)
1) Sorafenib (multi-kinase; FLT3)
- Suggested test range: 1–1000 nM (0.001–1 µM)

2) Ponatinib (BCR-ABL/FLT3/VEGFR)
- Suggested test range: 0.03–300 nM

3) Sunitinib (multi-kinase; FLT3)
- Suggested test range: 3–3000 nM (0.003–3 µM)

4) Cabozantinib (MET/VEGFR2; FLT3 activity reported)
- Suggested test range: 0.3–3000 nM (0.0003–3 µM)

5) Regorafenib (multi-kinase; FLT3 activity reported)
- Suggested test range: 3–3000 nM (0.003–3 µM)

6) Axitinib (VEGFR; reported FLT3 activity at low nM–µM)
- Suggested test range: 0.3–300 nM

7) Lenvatinib (multi-kinase)
- Suggested test range: 1–3000 nM (0.001–3 µM)

ABL/SRC family TKIs with AML activity
8) Dasatinib (SRC/ABL)
- Suggested test range: 0.3–300 nM

9) Nilotinib (ABL; reported FLT3 activity)
- Suggested test range: 3–3000 nM (0.003–3 µM)

10) Bosutinib (SRC/ABL)
- Suggested test range: 3–3000 nM (0.003–3 µM)

JAK/MEK/mTOR pathway inhibitors (common AML signaling dependencies)
11) Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2)
- Suggested test range: 10–10,000 nM (0.01–10 µM)

12) Trametinib (MEK1/2)
- Suggested test range: 0.1–100 nM

13) Selumetinib (MEK1/2)
- Suggested test range: 10–10,000 nM (0.01–10 µM)

14) Everolimus (mTORC1)
- Suggested test range: 0.3–300 nM

15) Temsirolimus (mTORC1)
- Suggested test range: 1–1000 nM (0.001–1 µM)

Epigenetic modulators (active in AML lines including MOLM-13)
16) Vorinostat/SAHA (HDAC inhibitor)
- Suggested test range: 100–10,000 nM (0.1–10 µM)

17) Panobinostat (HDAC inhibitor)
- Suggested test range: 1–300 nM

Proteostasis inhibitors (broad AML cytotoxicity)
18) Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor)
- Suggested test range: 1–100 nM

19) Carfilzomib (proteasome inhibitor)
- Suggested test range: 1–100 nM

Nuclear export inhibitor (active in AML, including FLT3-ITD models)
20) Selinexor (XPO1 inhibitor)
- Suggested test range: 10–1000 nM (0.01–1 µM)

Notes
- MOLM-13 carries FLT3-ITD; drugs with FLT3 activity (e.g., sorafenib, ponatinib, sunitinib, cabozantinib, regorafenib) often yield low-nanomolar IC50s in this line.
- Start with a broad range to capture full dose–response; expand upward (into low double-digit µM) if no effect at the top concentration.
- Consider including an AML-targeted FLT3 inhibitor (e.g., quizartinib or gilteritinib) as a positive control even though they are already AML-approved.
