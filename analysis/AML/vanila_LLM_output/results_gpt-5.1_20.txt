=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 existing (approved or late‑stage) drugs with reported activity in AML (often including FLT3‑ITD–positive models like MOLM‑13). For each, I provide:

- Mechanism / rationale for AML  
- A **reasonable IC₅₀ assay concentration range** (final concentrations in culture medium) to bracket likely activity.  
- A **suggested 8‑point serial dilution** (3‑fold steps) you can adapt (e.g., 72‑h CellTiter‑Glo / MTT on MOLM‑13).

These are order‑of‑magnitude suggestions based on published in‑vitro potencies; you should do a short-range pilot first and adjust.

### General IC₅₀ assay notes (for MOLM‑13)

- **Exposure time:** 72 h is typical for TKIs, HDACi, BCL‑2 inhibitors; consider 96 h for hypomethylating agents.  
- **Cell density:** 3–5 × 10⁴ cells/mL (e.g., 5,000–10,000 cells/well in 96‑well plates) to keep cells in log phase.  
- **Vehicle control:** Match highest DMSO level (usually ≤0.1–0.2% v/v).  
- **Replicates:** At least triplicate wells per concentration.  
- **Readout:** ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or MTS/MTT; optional annexin V/PI at selected doses for mechanism.
