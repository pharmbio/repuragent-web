=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing drugs (approved or advanced clinical agents) that have published preclinical activity against acute myeloid leukemia (AML) cell lines (including FLT3‑ITD models such as MOLM13) and that are good candidates for repurposing screens. For each drug I give a short rationale, a suggested experimentally testable IC50 concentration series (top concentration and a practical serial-dilution plan), and an approximate literature-based IC50 range where available. These suggestions are tuned for a standard 72 h viability assay on MOLM13 (see practical assay notes at the end). Numbers are approximate — run pilot curves and check compound solubility/stability before committing to large screens.

Legend for dilution series: recommended format is 10 concentrations in a 3‑fold serial dilution (10 points, 3× serial), unless otherwise noted. “Top conc.” = highest concentration to plate.

Practical assay notes and recommendations
- Standard parameters (suggested): MOLM13 in 96‑well plates, seed 3,000–10,000 cells/well (optimize to be in log phase through assay). Treat 72 hours for most small molecules. For epigenetic drugs (azacitidine, decitabine) use 5–7 day exposure with periodic medium/compound refresh.  
- Viability readout: ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or resazurin; use at least triplicate wells per concentration and include vehicle (DMSO) controls. Keep final DMSO ≤0.5% (ideally 0.1–0.2%).  
- Recommended dilution series format: 10 concentrations, 3× serial dilutions is a robust standard (covers from top conc down ~8 orders of magnitude). If solubility is an issue, use 8 points. For very potent inhibitors (expected single‑digit nM) you can set top conc at 1 µM to avoid waste and nonspecific effects. For weak agents or compounds with high protein binding, start at 10 µM.  
- Positive control: include a known cytotoxic (staurosporine or a drug with known activity in MOLM13 such as gilteritinib or venetoclax if tolerated) to confirm assay performance.  
- Data fitting: fit a 4‑parameter logistic curve to derive IC50. Report IC50 with 95% CI and check Hill slope; for biphasic curves consider alternative fits.  
- Compound handling: verify compound purity and solubility; some compounds (e.g., azacitidine) are unstable in solution — prepare fresh or follow vendor recommendations. Consider serum presence: some drugs lose potency in high serum; if doing serum‑free or low‑serum conditions, note physiological relevance.
