=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used (or late‑stage) drugs with published or strongly suggested activity against AML cell lines, including MOLM‑13, plus practical concentration ranges to test in an in‑vitro IC₅₀ assay.

**How to use these ranges**  
- For each compound, I give:  
  • A suggested **working range** for a 72‑h proliferation/viability IC₅₀ assay in MOLM‑13.  
  • A **starting stock** suggestion (usually 10 mM in DMSO).  
- When you first test a drug in your own lab, use **a wide log range** (e.g., 9–10 points, 1:3 or 1:2 dilutions) that brackets the range below, then refine around the observed IC₅₀.  
- All drugs below are used **off‑label/repurposing** for AML in this context; they are not all standard-of-care for AML.

---

## 1. Midostaurin (PKC412) – FLT3 & multi‑kinase inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved for FLT3‑mutated AML; MOLM‑13 is FLT3‑ITD–positive and highly sensitive.
- **Test range (MOLM‑13)**: 0.5 nM – 1 µM (log‑scale); expect IC₅₀ ~5–50 nM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 2. Gilteritinib (ASP2215) – FLT3 inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mut AML; strong activity in MOLM‑13.
- **Test range**: 0.1 nM – 300 nM; IC₅₀ often ~1–10 nM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 3. Quizartinib (AC220) – FLT3 inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Potent FLT3‑ITD inhibitor; MOLM‑13 is a classic sensitive line.
- **Test range**: 0.1 nM – 300 nM; IC₅₀ frequently <10 nM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 4. Sorafenib – multikinase (incl. FLT3)
- **Rationale**: Approved for solid tumors; off‑label use and many preclinical AML data, especially FLT3‑ITD.
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM; MOLM‑13 usually shows IC₅₀ ~50–300 nM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 5. Dasatinib – SRC/ABL multikinase inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved for CML/ALL; reported to inhibit proliferation and signaling in some AML lines.
- **Test range**: 1 nM – 10 µM; IC₅₀ in sensitive AML cells often ~10–200 nM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 6. Nilotinib – BCR‑ABL / KIT inhibitor
- **Rationale**: CML drug with activity against KIT/FLT3; some AML cell data (especially KIT/FLT3 dependent).
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM; look for IC₅₀ in low µM or sub‑µM range in MOLM‑13.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 7. Ponatinib – pan‑BCR‑ABL / multikinase inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Very broad tyrosine kinase inhibitor with FLT3 and other targets; shown to kill FLT3‑ITD AML cells.
- **Test range**: 0.5 nM – 1 µM; expect IC₅₀ ~5–50 nM in MOLM‑13.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 8. Venetoclax (ABT‑199) – BCL‑2 inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved (with HMA) for AML; MOLM‑13 is BCL‑2–dependent and usually very sensitive.
- **Test range**: 0.1 nM – 1 µM; IC₅₀ commonly 1–20 nM in BCL‑2‑high AML cells.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 9. Navitoclax (ABT‑263) – BCL‑2/BCL‑xL inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Clinical‑stage; strong apoptosis induction in many AML lines.
- **Test range**: 1 nM – 3 µM; IC₅₀ often ~30–300 nM in sensitive lines.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 10. Panobinostat (LBH589) – pan‑HDAC inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved for multiple myeloma; numerous reports of potent anti‑AML activity.
- **Test range**: 0.5 nM – 1 µM; AML IC₅₀s often ~5–50 nM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 11. Vorinostat (SAHA) – HDAC inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved for CTCL; widely used in AML preclinical studies.
- **Test range**: 0.1 µM – 20 µM; AML IC₅₀ typically 0.5–5 µM.
- **Stock**: 100 mM in DMSO.

## 12. Decitabine – DNA hypomethylating agent
- **Rationale**: Approved for AML/MDS; low‑nM DNA incorporation but functional cytotoxic IC₅₀ in vitro is higher.
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM (refresh daily if >48 h due to instability); many AML lines show IC₅₀ ~0.1–2 µM.
- **Stock**: 10–50 mM in aqueous solution prepared fresh or frozen aliquots.

## 13. Azacitidine – DNA/RNA hypomethylating agent
- **Rationale**: Approved for AML/MDS; cytotoxic in AML lines at low‑µM.
- **Test range**: 100 nM – 20 µM (refresh daily); IC₅₀ usually ~1–10 µM.
- **Stock**: 10–50 mM in aqueous solution, prepared fresh.

## 14. Ruxolitinib – JAK1/2 inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved for myelofibrosis; some AML (esp. with activated cytokine/JAK signaling) show sensitivity.
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM; AML IC₅₀ commonly 0.5–5 µM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 15. Idelalisib – PI3Kδ inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved for CLL/lymphoma; PI3K/AKT pathway is active in AML; some AML cell data exist.
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM; expect IC₅₀ in mid‑nM to low‑µM range if sensitive.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 16. Everolimus (RAD001) – mTORC1 inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Approved for various solid tumors; mTOR inhibition can suppress AML proliferation.
- **Test range**: 1 nM – 10 µM; AML IC₅₀s often ~10–500 nM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 17. Sirolimus (Rapamycin) – mTORC1 inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Immunosuppressant; similar rationale as everolimus with extensive AML cell data.
- **Test range**: 0.5 nM – 5 µM; typical AML IC₅₀ ~1–100 nM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO.

## 18. Metformin – AMPK activator / mitochondrial complex I inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Widely used antidiabetic; high‑µM–mM anti‑proliferative effects in AML and other cancers.
- **Test range**: 0.5 mM – 20 mM; AML lines often require 1–10 mM for measurable inhibition.
- **Stock**: 1–2 M in sterile water.

## 19. Disulfiram – ALDH inhibitor / proteasome‑related effects (often with Cu²⁺)
- **Rationale**: Alcohol‑aversion drug; reported to kill AML stem/progenitor cells, especially in presence of copper.
- **Test range** (without enforced copper supplementation, using serum‑containing media):  
  10 nM – 10 µM; many cancer lines show IC₅₀ ~0.1–2 µM.
- **Stock**: 100 mM in DMSO, protect from light; prepare fresh working dilutions.

## 20. Niclosamide – mitochondrial uncoupler / multi‑pathway inhibitor
- **Rationale**: Anthelmintic; preclinical activity reported in AML via Wnt/STAT3 and mitochondrial disruption.
- **Test range**: 50 nM – 10 µM; AML IC₅₀ often ~0.2–2 µM.
- **Stock**: 10 mM in DMSO; low aqueous solubility, ensure good mixing.

### Practical IC₅₀ assay setup for MOLM‑13 (summary)

- **Cells**: MOLM‑13 in log‑phase, viability >90%.  
- **Seeding density**: ~5×10³ cells/well in 96‑well plates (100 µL), adjust if cells overgrow by 72 h.  
- **Drug dilution**: 10‑point, half‑log or 3‑fold serial dilutions spanning the suggested range. Keep DMSO ≤0.1–0.2% v/v.  
- **Incubation**: 72 h is standard for proliferation/viability IC₅₀; 48 h if compounds are unstable.  
- **Readout**: CellTiter‑Glo / MTS / resazurin, or trypan blue counting.  
- **Analysis**: Fit a 4‑parameter logistic curve (nonlinear regression) to calculate IC₅₀.
