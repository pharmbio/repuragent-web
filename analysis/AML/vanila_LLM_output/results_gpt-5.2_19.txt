### Practical assay guidance (so the ranges make sense)
- **Run an 8–12 point dose–response**, 3-fold or 5-fold dilution series.
- **Top concentration** depends on solubility/toxicity: often **10–30 µM** for many repurposed drugs; for targeted kinase inhibitors often **0.1–1 µM** is enough.
- Report IC₅₀ after **72 h** (common for MOLM-13) using **CellTiter-Glo** or similar.
- Keep **DMSO ≤0.1%** (ideally ≤0.05%).
- Include controls: **quizartinib or gilteritinib** (FLT3) as positive controls in MOLM-13.

## Top 20 repurposing candidates (with testable IC₅₀ assay ranges)

**Key:** “Suggested IC₅₀ assay range” = a practical concentration window to bracket potency in MOLM-13-like settings. Start with that window as your dilution series (not as a claimed IC₅₀).

### A) High-priority targeted agents with strong MOLM-13 rationale (FLT3-ITD / signaling)
1) **Midostaurin** (PKC/FLT3 inhibitor; approved in AML)  
   - Suggested range: **0.3 nM – 3 µM** (top 1–3 µM)

2) **Sorafenib** (multi-kinase; FLT3/RAF/VEGFR; used off-label in FLT3-ITD AML)  
   - Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 3 µM**

3) **Sunitinib** (multi-kinase including FLT3)  
   - Suggested range: **1 nM – 10 µM**

4) **Pazopanib** (multi-kinase; some AML activity reports)  
   - Suggested range: **3 nM – 30 µM**

5) **Dasatinib** (SRC/ABL family; can impact AML signaling networks)  
   - Suggested range: **0.3 nM – 3 µM**

6) **Ruxolitinib** (JAK1/2; cytokine/JAK-STAT dependence in subsets; combos often better)  
   - Suggested range: **3 nM – 10 µM**

## Important practical notes (to avoid misleading IC₅₀s)
- **Media and serum** change apparent potency (e.g., venetoclax sensitivity shifts with metabolic context).  
- **Cell density** strongly impacts AML drug response; keep consistent (e.g., 25–100k cells/mL start).  
- Several drugs above are **light/temperature sensitive** (ATRA) or **chemically unstable** (azacitidine/decitabine); prepare fresh and minimize time in aqueous media.
- Consider confirming with **orthogonal readouts** (Annexin V/PI apoptosis, cell cycle) for agents that cause differentiation or cytostasis.
