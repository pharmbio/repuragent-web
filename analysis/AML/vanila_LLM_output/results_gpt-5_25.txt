=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing, clinically used drugs with literature support for inhibiting AML cell growth, including data in MOLM-13 (FLT3-ITD) or closely related FLT3-ITD AML models. For each, I list a concise rationale and the approximate literature-reported potency (viability/proliferation IC50) in MOLM-13 when available, or in similar FLT3-ITD AML cell lines (e.g., MV4-11) otherwise. These reported IC50 values can guide selection of concentration windows for your own IC50 assays, noting that exact potencies vary with assay format, serum, cell density, and readout.

Important notes
- MOLM-13 is FLT3-ITD; agents targeting FLT3, STAT5, proteasome, HDAC, nuclear export, and mitochondrial translation have shown activity.
- Values below are approximate ranges compiled from peer-reviewed studies and public pharmacogenomic datasets (e.g., GDSC/CancerRXGene, DepMap/PRISM) and should be cross-checked against the specific protocols used in those sources.

1) Ponatinib (CML/Ph+ ALL; multikinase including FLT3)
- Rationale: Potent FLT3-ITD inhibitor; active against resistant FLT3 mutants.
- Reported IC50 (MOLM-13/FLT3-ITD AML): low nanomolar (~1–10 nM).
- Example sources: Pemovska T, Nature 2013; PRISM/DepMap drug response.

2) Sorafenib (HCC/RCC; RAF/VEGFR/FLT3 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Inhibits FLT3-ITD signaling; clinical activity in FLT3-ITD AML.
- Reported IC50: submicromolar to low micromolar (~0.5–3 µM) in MOLM-13/MV4-11.
- Example sources: Zhang W, Clin Cancer Res 2008; GDSC.

3) Sunitinib (RCC; multikinase including FLT3)
- Rationale: Early FLT3 inhibitor with activity in FLT3-ITD AML lines.
- Reported IC50: tens to hundreds of nM (~50–300 nM).
- Example sources: O’Farrell AM, Blood 2003; GDSC.

4) Cabozantinib (RCC; MET/VEGFR; also inhibits FLT3)
- Rationale: Inhibits FLT3 and some resistance mutants.
- Reported IC50: low tens of nM (~10–60 nM) in FLT3-ITD AML lines.
- Example sources: Levis group reports; Clin Cancer Res 2015.

5) Dasatinib (CML; SRC/ABL family kinase inhibitor)
- Rationale: Inhibits signaling pathways relevant to AML; synergy with other agents.
- Reported IC50: ~0.2–1 µM in MOLM-13/AML panels.
- Example sources: Schittenhelm MM, Blood 2006; GDSC.

6) Selinexor (MM/PTCL; XPO1 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Traps tumor suppressors in nucleus; strong AML preclinical data.
- Reported IC50: ~50–200 nM in MOLM-13 and other AML lines.
- Example sources: Etchin J, Blood 2013; PRISM/DepMap.

7) Bortezomib (MM/MCL; proteasome inhibitor)
- Rationale: Proteotoxic stress induction; AML cell line sensitivity shown.
- Reported IC50: low nanomolar (~5–15 nM) in MOLM-13/AML lines.
- Example sources: Hu J, Leukemia 2011; GDSC.

8) Carfilzomib (MM; irreversible proteasome inhibitor)
- Rationale: Potent proteasome blockade; AML lines show susceptibility.
- Reported IC50: tens of nM (~20–100 nM).
- Example sources: AML cell line screens; GDSC/PRISM.

9) Panobinostat (MM; HDAC inhibitor)
- Rationale: Epigenetic modulation; synergy with FLT3 inhibitors reported.
- Reported IC50: single- to low–tens of nM (~5–20 nM) in AML lines including MOLM-13.
- Example sources: Fiskus W, Clin Cancer Res 2012; GDSC.

10) Vorinostat (CTCL; HDAC inhibitor)
- Rationale: Broad epigenetic effects; AML growth inhibition documented.
- Reported IC50: ~1–2 µM in AML lines including MOLM-13.
- Example sources: AML preclinical studies; GDSC.

11) Romidepsin (PTCL; HDAC inhibitor)
- Rationale: Potent class I HDAC inhibitor; AML cytotoxicity shown.
- Reported IC50: low nanomolar (~2–10 nM).
- Example sources: AML preclinical studies; GDSC.

12) Everolimus (RCC; mTORC1 inhibitor)
- Rationale: mTOR pathway dependence in AML; often cytostatic but can be cytotoxic.
- Reported IC50 (viability): ~1–5 µM in AML lines; nanomolar for pS6 signaling readouts.
- Example sources: Recher C, Blood 2005; GDSC.

13) Temsirolimus (RCC; mTOR inhibitor)
- Rationale: Similar to everolimus; AML antiproliferative effects reported.
- Reported IC50 (viability): ~0.5–2 µM.
- Example sources: AML cell line studies; GDSC.

14) Ruxolitinib (MF/PV; JAK1/2 inhibitor)
- Rationale: JAK/STAT signaling supports AML survival; activity in subsets and combinations.
- Reported IC50: ~1–5 µM in AML lines including MOLM-13.
- Example sources: AML preclinical data; GDSC/PRISM.

15) Fostamatinib (ITP; SYK inhibitor; active metabolite R406)
- Rationale: SYK signaling implicated in AML survival; preclinical AML activity.
- Reported IC50 (R406): ~1–3 µM in AML lines including FLT3-ITD contexts.
- Example sources: Puissant A, Cancer Cell 2014; datasets.

16) Tigecycline (antibiotic; mitochondrial translation inhibitor)
- Rationale: Targets mitochondrial translation; selective toxicity to AML cells/stem cells.
- Reported IC50: ~1–5 µM in AML lines including MOLM-13.
- Example sources: Skrtic M, PNAS 2011; Cell Stem Cell follow-ups.

17) Pimozide (antipsychotic; STAT5 pathway inhibitor)
- Rationale: STAT5 is downstream of FLT3-ITD; pimozide reduces STAT5 signaling.
- Reported IC50: low micromolar (~2–10 µM) in FLT3-ITD AML (MOLM-13/MV4-11).
- Example sources: Nelson EA, Blood 2011.

18) Ciclopirox (antifungal; iron chelator)
- Rationale: Disrupts iron-dependent pathways; induces apoptosis in AML lines.
- Reported IC50: ~1–5 µM.
- Example sources: Eberhard Y, Blood 2009; AML preclinical reports.

19) Disulfiram (alcohol aversion; aldehyde dehydrogenase inhibitor; Cu-dependent)
- Rationale: Forms Cu–DDC complex that targets proteasome/NPL4; AML cytotoxicity shown.
- Reported IC50: ~0.1–1 µM in the presence of physiological copper.
- Example sources: Skrott Z, Nature 2017; AML-focused preclinical studies.

20) Niclosamide (anthelmintic; multiple signaling effects incl. Wnt/mTOR)
- Rationale: Reported anti-leukemia activity via metabolic and signaling disruption.
- Reported IC50: submicromolar to low micromolar (~0.3–2 µM) in AML panels.
- Example sources: Oncotarget/AML preclinical literature; PRISM.

How to use these values
- Treat the numbers above as literature benchmarks for expected potency in MOLM-13/FLT3-ITD AML contexts. For experimental planning and assay setup, consult the original papers or curated databases (GDSC/CancerRXGene, DepMap PRISM, CTRP) to match conditions (medium, serum, time, readout), as these factors can shift apparent potency by an order of magnitude.

References and data resources
- GDSC (CancerRXGene): dose–response data for MOLM-13 and many drugs (www.cancerrxgene.org).
- DepMap PRISM Repurposing dataset: pooled cell line drug responses (depmap.org/portal).
- Selected primary literature (examples): Zhang W et al., Clin Cancer Res 2008 (sorafenib in FLT3-ITD AML); Etchin J et al., Blood 2013 (selinexor in AML); Nelson EA et al., Blood 2011 (pimozide/STAT5 in FLT3-ITD AML); Skrtic M et al., PNAS 2011 (tigecycline in AML).
