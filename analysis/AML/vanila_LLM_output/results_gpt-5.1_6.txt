=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 **existing, clinically used or late‑stage drugs** with evidence (preclinical or clinical) of activity against AML, including MOLM‑13 where data exist or are closely related (FLT3‑ITD–positive) lines. I’ll also give **practical concentration ranges for in‑vitro IC₅₀/proliferation assays**, based on published potency and standard in‑vitro practice.

**General notes for setting up IC₅₀ assays**

- Do a **10‑point, 3‑fold serial dilution**.
- A good default range is **0.1 nM – 10 or 30 μM**, with **0.1% DMSO** constant in all wells.
- For very potent inhibitors (sub‑nanomolar IC₅₀), start lower (e.g., 0.01 nM).
- Measure viability/proliferation at **48–72 h** (e.g. CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, resazurin).
- Include positive controls (e.g., cytarabine, midostaurin) and vehicle controls.

---

## 1. Midostaurin (PKC412) – FLT3 / multikinase inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for FLT3‑mutant AML.
- **Rationale:** Inhibits FLT3‑ITD; MOLM‑13 (FLT3‑ITD) is highly sensitive.
- **Reported AML IC₅₀:** Often in the **5–50 nM** range for FLT3‑ITD+ lines.
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay range (MOLM‑13):**  
  0.5 nM – 5 μM (3‑fold serial dilution; center around 5–100 nM).

---

## 2. Gilteritinib (ASP2215) – FLT3/AXL inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mutant AML.
- **Rationale:** Strong activity in FLT3‑ITD cell lines (MOLM‑13, MV4‑11).
- **Reported IC₅₀ vs FLT3:** ~0.3–1 nM (biochemical); cell viability often ~1–10 nM.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 1 μM (focus 1–100 nM).

---

## 3. Quizartinib (AC220) – FLT3 inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved/late‑stage FLT3 inhibitor (region‑dependent).
- **Rationale:** Very potent against FLT3‑ITD; widely used in MOLM‑13 experiments.
- **Reported IC₅₀ (MOLM‑13, MV4‑11):** often ~1–10 nM for viability.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 1 μM.

---

## 4. Sorafenib – multikinase (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR)

- **Status/Use:** Approved for solid tumors; off‑label/clinical studies in FLT3‑ITD AML.
- **Rationale:** Known to suppress MOLM‑13 and other FLT3‑ITD cell lines.
- **Typical AML IC₅₀ (cell growth):** ~10–200 nM.
- **Suggested range:** 1 nM – 10 μM (focus 10–500 nM).

---

## 5. Dasatinib – SRC/ABL family inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for CML/ALL; repurposing interest for AML.
- **Rationale:** Targets SRC family kinases, LYN, etc.; reported anti‑proliferative activity in AML lines.
- **AML IC₅₀ (proliferation):** commonly ~10–200 nM.
- **Suggested range:** 1 nM – 10 μM.

---

## 6. Venetoclax (ABT‑199) – BCL‑2 inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for AML (with hypomethylating agents or LDAC).
- **Rationale:** Potent in many AML lines, including FLT3‑ITD–positive; synergizes with FLT3 inhibitors.
- **Reported IC₅₀ (AML lines):** ~5–100 nM, but variable.
- **Suggested range:** 0.5 nM – 5 μM (focus 5–500 nM).

---

## 7. Azacitidine – DNA hypomethylating agent

- **Status/Use:** Standard of care for MDS/AML.
- **Rationale:** Widely used; affects DNA methylation and proliferation in AML lines.
- **In‑vitro AML IC₅₀:** usually in **low μM** after 72 h or longer.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 μM – 100 μM (focus 1–20 μM; extend incubation to 72–96 h).

---

## 8. Decitabine – DNA hypomethylating agent

- **Status/Use:** Approved for AML/MDS.
- **Rationale:** Similar to azacitidine; potent epigenetic modulator.
- **In‑vitro AML IC₅₀:** ~0.1–10 μM (time‑dependent).
- **Suggested range:** 0.03 μM – 30 μM (72–96 h exposure ideally).

---

## 9. Vorinostat (SAHA) – HDAC inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for cutaneous T‑cell lymphoma.
- **Rationale:** Broad anti‑leukemic activity; HDAC inhibition induces apoptosis in AML lines.
- **Reported AML IC₅₀:** typically ~0.5–5 μM.
- **Suggested range:** 0.03 μM – 30 μM (focus 0.3–10 μM).

---

## 10. Panobinostat – pan‑HDAC inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for multiple myeloma.
- **Rationale:** Very potent HDACi; active in multiple AML models.
- **AML IC₅₀ (proliferation):** roughly 5–50 nM in many lines.
- **Suggested range:** 0.5 nM – 5 μM (focus 5–500 nM).

---

## 11. Ruxolitinib – JAK1/2 inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for myelofibrosis, PV, GVHD.
- **Rationale:** JAK/STAT pathway relevant in some AML subsets; modest direct cytotoxicity but combinable.
- **Reported AML IC₅₀:** often around 0.5–10 μM depending on model.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 μM – 50 μM (focus 0.5–10 μM).

---

## 12. Ibrutinib – BTK inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for multiple B‑cell malignancies.
- **Rationale:** Off‑target inhibition of TEC/SRC pathways and others; some reports of AML growth inhibition, esp. in certain subtypes.
- **Reported AML IC₅₀:** often in low‑to‑mid μM.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 μM – 30 μM.

---

## 13. Imatinib – ABL/PDGFR/c‑KIT inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for CML, GIST, etc.
- **Rationale:** Some AMLs express c‑KIT or PDGFR alterations; partial anti‑proliferative effects in relevant subgroups.
- **AML IC₅₀:** commonly 1–10 μM (unless a strong target mutation is present).
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 μM – 50 μM (focus 1–20 μM).

---

## 14. Metformin – AMPK activator / mitochondrial complex I inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Widely used antidiabetic; repurposing interest in many cancers.
- **Rationale:** Inhibits mitochondrial respiration and mTOR; AML cells are often metabolically vulnerable.
- **In‑vitro AML IC₅₀:** usually **high μM to low mM** (pharmacological not physiologic).
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 mM – 20 mM (e.g. 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 20 mM).

---

## 15. Disulfiram (± copper) – ALDH inhibitor / proteasome modulator

- **Status/Use:** Approved for alcoholism.
- **Rationale:** Targets ALDH‑positive leukemic stem/progenitor cells; reported anti‑AML effects, often enhanced by Cu²⁺.
- **Reported AML IC₅₀:** usually 0.1–5 μM (with copper).
- **Suggested range:** 0.03 μM – 30 μM;  
  consider co‑treatment with CuCl₂ (0.5–2 μM) if you want to mimic published protocols.

---

## 16. Chloroquine or Hydroxychloroquine – autophagy inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Antimalarial/anti‑rheumatic; in cancer trials as autophagy modulators.
- **Rationale:** AML cells rely on autophagy under stress; inhibition can decrease viability or potentiate other drugs.
- **AML IC₅₀:** often 5–50 μM for monotherapy effects.
- **Suggested range:** 1 μM – 100 μM (focus 5–50 μM).

---

## 17. Arsenic trioxide (ATO) – PML‑RARα / oxidative stress inducer

- **Status/Use:** Approved for APL (PML‑RARα+ AML).
- **Rationale:** Some activity in non‑APL AML at higher doses; induces oxidative and mitochondrial stress.
- **AML IC₅₀:** typically 0.5–5 μM.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 μM – 20 μM (focus 0.5–5 μM).

---

## 18. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide – immunomodulatory drugs (IMiDs)

- **Status/Use:** Approved for multiple myeloma, MDS (5q‑) etc.
- **Rationale:** Cereblon‑mediated degradation of specific substrates; context‑dependent AML cytotoxicity.
- **AML IC₅₀:** often in mid‑μM range for direct effects.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 μM – 50 μM (focus 1–20 μM).

---

## 19. Everolimus (RAD001) – mTORC1 inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for several solid tumors, TSC.
- **Rationale:** mTOR pathway commonly activated in AML; everolimus shows anti‑proliferative effects.
- **AML IC₅₀:** typically 1–100 nM for signaling; 10 nM – 1 μM for viability.
- **Suggested range:** 0.5 nM – 10 μM (focus 5–500 nM).

---

## 20. Bortezomib – proteasome inhibitor

- **Status/Use:** Approved for multiple myeloma and mantle cell lymphoma.
- **Rationale:** Proteasome inhibition triggers apoptosis in AML blasts; has been tested preclinically with FLT3‑ITD+ lines.
- **AML IC₅₀:** often 2–50 nM.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 1 μM (focus 2–200 nM).

### Practical design tips for MOLM‑13 assays

- **Seeding density:** 2–5 × 10⁴ cells/well in 96‑well plates for 72 h assays, in log‑phase growth.
- **Assay window:** Verify >3–4 doublings in vehicle control over the assay period.
- **Endpoints:** Prefer ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or resazurin; avoid assays affected by mitochondrial inhibitors when testing mitochondrial drugs (e.g., metformin), or validate with an orthogonal readout (Annexin V/PI, cell counts).
- **Replicates:** At least triplicate wells per concentration; repeat independent experiments (n≥3).
