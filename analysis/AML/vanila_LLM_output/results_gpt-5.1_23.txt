=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 existing (approved or late‑stage) drugs with evidence of anti‑AML activity, especially in FLT3‑mutant AML lines like MOLM13. I’ll focus on compounds with published in‑vitro data and give a reasonable concentration range for generating IC50 curves in proliferation/viability assays (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, etc.).

**General note on IC50 assay setup**

- Typical starting point: **10‑point, 3‑fold serial dilution**.  
- For most kinase inhibitors: **0.3 nM – 10 µM** or **1 nM – 30 µM** is a good window.  
- For less potent cytotoxics: **1 nM – 100 µM**.  
- Use **MOLM13** plus at least one other AML line (e.g., MV4‑11, THP‑1, HL‑60) for comparison.  
- 48–72 h exposure is standard for proliferation/viability IC50.

## A. FLT3 and broad‑spectrum kinase inhibitors

These have the strongest rationale for MOLM13 (FLT3‑ITD).

### 1. **Midostaurin (PKC412)**
- Type: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, etc.). FDA‑approved for FLT3‑mutated AML.
- Reported MOLM13 IC50 (FLT3‑mutant AML): ~10–50 nM for FLT3 autophosphorylation; anti‑proliferative IC50 typically ~10–100 nM.
- **Test range:** 0.5 nM – 5 µM.

### 2. **Gilteritinib (ASP2215)**
- Type: FLT3/AXL inhibitor; approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mutant AML.
- Reported IC50 in FLT3‑ITD lines (e.g., MOLM13/MV4‑11): low‑nanomolar (1–10 nM).
- **Test range:** 0.1 nM – 1 µM.

### 3. **Quizartinib (AC220)**
- Type: Potent, selective FLT3 inhibitor (investigational/approved in some regions).
- Reported IC50 for FLT3‑ITD AML (MOLM13/MV4‑11): ~1–5 nM.
- **Test range:** 0.05 nM – 0.5 µM.

### 4. **Sorafenib**
- Type: Multikinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR, FLT3). Approved for HCC, RCC, etc.
- Reported IC50 in FLT3‑ITD AML cell lines: ~5–50 nM (FLT3) and low‑100 nM for antiproliferative.
- **Test range:** 1 nM – 10 µM.

### 5. **Sunitinib**
- Type: Multikinase inhibitor; approved for RCC, GIST.
- FLT3‑ITD AML lines show growth inhibition in low‑100 nM range.
- **Test range:** 1 nM – 20 µM.

### 6. **Crenolanib**
- Type: Potent FLT3 inhibitor (including TKD mutants).
- IC50 in FLT3‑mutant lines: very low nanomolar (1–10 nM).
- **Test range:** 0.1 nM – 1 µM.

### 7. **Ponatinib**
- Type: Pan‑BCR‑ABL/FLT3 inhibitor; approved for CML/Ph+ ALL.
- FLT3‑ITD AML lines: often IC50 ~5–30 nM.
- **Test range:** 0.5 nM – 5 µM.

### 8. **Dasatinib**
- Type: SRC/ABL family inhibitor; approved for CML/Ph+ ALL.
- AML lines (including some FLT3‑mutants): proliferation IC50 typically ~10–200 nM.
- **Test range:** 1 nM – 20 µM.

## B. Epigenetic and transcription‑related drugs

### 9. **Decitabine**
- Type: DNA hypomethylating agent; approved for MDS/AML.
- In vitro, effective at **100 nM – 1 µM** (higher concentrations become cytotoxic).
- **Test range:** 5 nM – 10 µM  
  (focus on 50 nM – 2 µM for clinically relevant range).

### 10. **Azacitidine**
- Type: DNA/RNA hypomethylating agent; approved for MDS/AML.
- Similar potency to decitabine; often used at **0.5 – 5 µM** in vitro.
- **Test range:** 50 nM – 20 µM.

### 11. **Panobinostat (LBH589)**
- Type: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma.
- AML lines (including MOLM13) often show IC50 ~5–50 nM.
- **Test range:** 0.5 nM – 5 µM.

### 12. **Vorinostat (SAHA)**
- Type: HDAC inhibitor; approved for CTCL.
- AML lines: antiproliferative IC50 usually ~0.5–5 µM.
- **Test range:** 50 nM – 50 µM.

### 13. **Ruxolitinib**
- Type: JAK1/2 inhibitor; approved for myelofibrosis, PV.
- Some AML subtypes and cytokine‑dependent lines show sensitivity in ~0.1–5 µM range.
- **Test range:** 10 nM – 30 µM.

---

## C. BCL2 / apoptosis and related pathways

### 14. **Venetoclax (ABT‑199)**
- Type: BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved (with HMA/LDAC) for AML in older/unfit patients.
- AML lines (esp. those BCL‑2 dependent) often IC50 ~1–50 nM.
- **Test range:** 0.1 nM – 1 µM.

### 15. **Navitoclax (ABT‑263)**
- Type: BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor (clinical‑stage).
- AML cell lines: frequently IC50 ~50–500 nM.
- **Test range:** 5 nM – 10 µM.

### 16. **Obatoclax (GX15‑070)**
- Type: Pan‑BCL‑2 family inhibitor (experimental, but widely used in vitro).
- AML lines: IC50 often ~0.1–5 µM.
- **Test range:** 50 nM – 30 µM.

---

## D. Other targeted or repurposable agents

### 17. **Ibrutinib**
- Type: BTK inhibitor; approved for CLL, mantle cell lymphoma, etc.
- Some AML models show off‑target anti‑proliferative activity (SRC, TEC, interactor pathways) in low‑µM range.
- **Test range:** 50 nM – 30 µM.

### 18. **Imatinib**
- Type: BCR‑ABL/KIT/PDGFR inhibitor; approved for CML, GIST.
- Direct effect in typical AML lines is modest, but certain KIT‑mutant or PDGFR‑rearranged AMLs can be sensitive.
- **Test range:** 100 nM – 50 µM.

### 19. **Bortezomib**
- Type: Proteasome inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma, MCL.
- AML lines: IC50 commonly ~5–50 nM.
- **Test range:** 0.5 nM – 5 µM.

### 20. **Selinexor**
- Type: XPO1 (CRM1) inhibitor; approved for myeloma, DLBCL.
- AML cell lines often respond in **50–500 nM** range.
- **Test range:** 5 nM – 10 µM.

## Suggested practical assay plan (brief)

1. **Cell line:** MOLM13 (plus at least one comparator AML line).  
2. **Seeding:** 3–10 × 10³ cells/well in 96‑well plate; low serum (e.g., 10% FBS) with cytokines appropriate for MOLM13 if routinely used.  
3. **Drug dilution:** 10‑point 3‑fold serial dilution covering the suggested range.  
4. **Duration:** 72 h incubation for proliferation/viability IC50.  
5. **Readout:** CellTiter‑Glo or MTS/XTT; normalize to DMSO control.  
6. **Analysis:** Fit a 4‑parameter logistic curve and extract IC50; repeat in ≥3 biological replicates.
