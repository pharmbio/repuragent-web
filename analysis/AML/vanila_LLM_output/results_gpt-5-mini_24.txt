=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing drugs (approved or well‑characterized investigational agents) with published evidence or strong rationale for anti‑AML activity — many have demonstrated activity in FLT3‑ITD AML models or AML cell lines (MOLM13 and/or MOLM13 relatives such as MV4‑11). For each I give a short rationale and an experimentally practical concentration range and a recommended dilution scheme you can use to determine an IC50 in MOLM13 (or other AML) cell lines.

General assay suggestions (apply to all compounds)
- Plate format: 96‑well or 384‑well. For suspension AML lines like MOLM13, 96‑well is common.  
- Seeding: 5–20 × 10^3 viable cells/well (96‑well) so cells are in log growth during the assay.  
- Incubation: 72 hours is standard for proliferation/viability IC50; consider also 48 h and 96 h timepoints for kinetics.  
- Vehicle: DMSO is usual; keep final DMSO ≤ 0.1% (ideally ≤ 0.05%). Prepare 1000× stock in DMSO then dilute. Check each drug’s solubility.  
- Readout: ATP (CellTiter‑Glo) or resazurin/CellTiter‑Blue or flow cytometry/live‑dead stain. Include positive control (e.g., gilteritinib or cytarabine) and no‑drug control.  
- Dilution series: I recommend an 11‑point 3‑fold serial dilution (or 10‑point 3‑fold) covering ~6 orders of magnitude, centered on the expected cellular IC50. This is stated per drug below. Run at least triplicate wells per concentration and perform ≥2 independent experiments.  
- Notes for metal‑dependent compounds: disulfiram and others often require Cu2+ to be active; test ±Cu (e.g., 1:1 molar ratio or 1–5 µM CuSO4).

Top 20 candidate drugs (short rationale + suggested IC50 testing range and dilution scheme)
1) Gilteritinib (FLT3/AXL TKI)
- Rationale: Approved FLT3 inhibitor with strong activity against FLT3‑ITD AML (MOLM13 is FLT3‑ITD).  
- Expected cellular IC50 (MOLM13 range): ~1–10 nM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 1 µM (11‑point 3‑fold: 0.01, 0.03, 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000 nM).

2) Quizartinib (AC220; FLT3 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Very potent FLT3 inhibitor active on FLT3‑ITD cells.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–10 nM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 1 µM (same series as gilteritinib).

3) Crenolanib (FLT3 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Potent against FLT3‑ITD and some resistance alleles.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–50 nM (cellular).  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 1 µM (11‑point 3‑fold).

4) Midostaurin (multikinase, FLT3 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Approved in combination for FLT3‑mutant AML.  
- Expected IC50: ~10–200 nM.  
- Test range: 0.1 nM – 10 µM (11‑point 3‑fold).

5) Sorafenib (multikinase TKI)
- Rationale: Off‑label/experimental activity against FLT3‑ITD models.  
- Expected IC50: ~10–500 nM.  
- Test range: 0.1 nM – 10 µM (11‑point 3‑fold).

6) Ponatinib (multi‑TKI)
- Rationale: Potent TKI with activity against FLT3 in some studies.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–100 nM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 1 µM (11‑point 3‑fold).

7) Venetoclax (BCL‑2 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Highly active BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved combinations in AML; many AML lines (including FLT3‑mutant) are sensitive.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–100 nM (line dependent).  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 1 µM (11‑point 3‑fold).

8) Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor)
- Rationale: Proteasome inhibition can be cytotoxic to AML cells; single‑agent activity in cell lines.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–100 nM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 1 µM (11‑point 3‑fold).

9) Carfilzomib (proteasome inhibitor)
- Rationale: More potent proteasome inhibitor; similar rationale to bortezomib.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–100 nM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 1 µM.

10) Auranofin (thioredoxin reductase inhibitor; anti‑rheumatic)
- Rationale: Repositioned for multiple cancers; reported AML line activity including MOLM13/MV4‑11 in the low µM range.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–5 µM.  
- Test range: 1 nM – 50 µM (11‑point 3‑fold: 0.001, 0.003, 0.01, 0.03, 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100 µM — adjust upper limit if insoluble; many labs use top 30–50 µM).

11) Niclosamide (anthelmintic; mitochondrial uncoupler / Wnt/STAT3 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Reported cytotoxicity in AML cell lines and stem‑like populations; active in low µM range.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.2–5 µM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 50 µM (practically focus 1 nM–10 µM or 0.1 nM–50 µM to cover).

12) Mebendazole (antihelmintic; microtubule inhibitor)
- Rationale: Repurposing candidate with cytotoxicity in AML models (low µM to sub‑µM).  
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–5 µM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 50 µM (practically 0.1 nM–10 µM covers expected).

13) Mefloquine (antimalarial)
- Rationale: Reported antileukemic effects in cell lines via multiple mechanisms.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–20 µM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 50 µM (practically 1 nM–50 µM).

14) Disulfiram (aldehyde dehydrogenase inhibitor) ± Cu
- Rationale: Disulfiram complexed with copper shows proteotoxic / ubiquitin–proteasome effects and AML cell killing. Test ±Cu.  
- Expected IC50 (with Cu): low µM (0.1–5 µM).  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 50 µM; run parallel sets with 1:1 molar CuSO4 (e.g., 1 µM Cu).

15) Clofazimine (antileprosy diarylquinone)
- Rationale: Disrupts mitochondrial function and NF‑κB/ER stress; reported AML cell activity.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–10 µM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 50 µM (practically 0.1 nM–10 µM).

16) Itraconazole (antifungal; hedgehog/VEGF pathway modulator)
- Rationale: Reported anticancer activity via multiple mechanisms; some AML cell sensitivity reported.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.5–10 µM (cell line dependent).  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 50 µM.

17) Simvastatin (HMG‑CoA reductase inhibitor)
- Rationale: Statins can induce apoptosis in AML by blocking prenylation; activity often in low to mid µM. Use the hydroxy acid form or activate the lactone.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–50 µM (line dependent).  
- Test range: 0.01 µM – 100 µM (try 10‑point 3‑fold: 0.01, 0.03, 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100 µM).

18) Tamoxifen (SERM)
- Rationale: Exhibit pro‑apoptotic effects in some AML lines via PKC/mitochondrial mechanisms.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–20 µM.  
- Test range: 0.01 nM – 50 µM (practically 0.1 nM–50 µM or 0.01–50 µM).

19) Hydroxychloroquine / Chloroquine (autophagy inhibitors)
- Rationale: May sensitize AML cells and show single‑agent effects at high concentrations. Often used in combination strategies.  
- Expected IC50: ~10–100 µM (often higher).  
- Test range: 0.1 µM – 200 µM (10‑point 3‑fold: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 µM — adjust to instrument limits).

20) Metformin (biguanide)
- Rationale: Alters mitochondrial metabolism/AMPK; single agent cytotoxicity often requires mM concentrations in vitro.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–20 mM (cell line dependent).  
- Test range: 0.01 mM – 50 mM (10‑point 3‑fold or 11‑point serial dilutions). Note: high concentrations may change media pH; include appropriate controls.

Prioritization for MOLM13
- Highest likelihood of single‑agent potency in MOLM13 (FLT3‑ITD): gilteritinib, quizartinib, crenolanib, midostaurin, ponatinib, sorafenib.  
- High plausibility for single‑agent cytotoxicity (non‑TKIs): venetoclax, bortezomib/carfilzomib, auranofin, niclosamide, mebendazole, disulfiram+Cu, clofazimine.  
- Drugs more likely to be effective in combinations or at higher concentrations: chloroquine, statins, itraconazole, tamoxifen, metformin.

Practical tips
- Start with a broad 11‑point 3‑fold series described above. If a compound shows no activity at the top concentration, you can increase the top concentration (subject to solubility/toxicity of vehicle). If the IC50 is very low (<lowest tested), rerun with a lower starting point.  
- For metal‑enhanced drugs (disulfiram, disulfiram/copper), include controls with copper only and run molar‑ratio titrations.  
- For prodrugs (simvastatin lactone), convert to active acid or use pravastatin/atorvastatin if preferred.  
- For compounds that aggregate or are poorly soluble, include 0.01–0.1% Tween‑20 in stocks (careful with cell effects) or lower DMSO stocks; check precipitation visually.
