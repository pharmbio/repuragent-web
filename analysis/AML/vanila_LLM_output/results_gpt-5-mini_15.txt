Results:

Below are 20 existing drugs (approved or well-studied) that have documented or plausible activity against AML cell lines — especially FLT3-ITD positive models like MOLM13 — with a short rationale and a practical, experimentally testable IC50 concentration series for each. I give one convenient 8‑point serial-dilution scheme for each drug (3‑fold dilutions are common and work well for IC50 curve fitting). Use these as starting conditions and adapt if you observe the IC50 is outside the tested range.

General assay notes (recommended):
- Cell line: MOLM13 (suspension). Plate 96‑well: 5,000–15,000 cells/well in 100 µL (optimize for your assay).  
- Incubation: 72 hours (many AML drug responses are evident by 72 h; for nucleoside analogues you may want 96 h).  
- Readout: CellTiter‑Glo or equivalent viability assay.  
- Diluent: DMSO (keep final DMSO ≤0.1% v/v). Prepare stock solutions appropriately.  
- Serial dilution: 8 points, 3‑fold dilution series from the indicated top concentration. Include vehicle control and a positive control (e.g., cytarabine or a known FLT3 inhibitor).  
- Special: Disulfiram is active in many systems only in presence of copper — include CuCl2 (1 µM final) where indicated.

Practical tips and follow-up experiments
- If a compound’s IC50 is at or near your top concentration, repeat with a higher top concentration or use a finer dilution (e.g., 2‑fold series). If IC50 is below your lowest concentration, add lower points.  
- For drugs that induce cytostasis rather than cell death, extend incubation or run apoptosis markers (Annexin V/PI, caspase assays) to confirm mechanism.  
- For combinations (e.g., venetoclax + azacitidine), consider fixed‑ratio combination matrices. MOLM13 is commonly sensitive to BCL‑2 inhibition + hypomethylating agents.  
- Maintain final solvent (DMSO) constant across wells. Include growth controls, vehicle controls, and at least one cytotoxic positive control (e.g., high‑dose cytarabine).
