=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Repurposing existing drugs for the treatment of acute myeloid leukemia (AML) offers a promising strategy for developing new therapies. While preclinical validation is necessary to ensure efficacy and safety, the following list includes drugs that have shown potential in inhibiting AML proliferation, including activity on MOLM-13 cells. The listed concentrations can serve as a starting point for IC50 assays in cell lines:

1. **Venetoclax**:
   - Originally approved for chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL).
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.01–1 µM.
2. **Sorafenib**:
   - Multi-kinase inhibitor known for treating renal cell carcinoma.
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 1–10 µM.
3. **Dasatinib**:
   - Approved for chronic myeloid leukemia (CML).
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.01–0.5 µM.
4. **Ruxolitinib**:
   - JAK1/2 inhibitor approved for myelofibrosis.
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.1–5 µM.
5. **Sorafenib**:
   - Dual FLT3/tyrosine kinase inhibitor.
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 1–5 µM.
6. **Ibrutinib**:
   - BTK inhibitor used in CLL and mantle cell lymphoma.
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.5–5 µM.
7. **Tofacitinib**:
   - JAK inhibitor, used in rheumatoid arthritis.
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 1–10 µM.
8. **Arsenic Trioxide**:
   - Used in acute promyelocytic leukemia (APL).
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.1–2 µM.
9. **Metformin**:
   - A diabetes medication with potential as an anti-cancer agent.
   - Suggested Concentration Range: 1–10 mM.
10. **Chloroquine**:
    - Anti-malarial medication.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 5–25 µM.
11. **Celecoxib**:
    - COX-2 inhibitor used for inflammation.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 5–20 µM.
12. **Atorvastatin**:
    - Statin used for lowering cholesterol.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 1–10 µM.
13. **Simvastatin**:
    - Another cholesterol-lowering statin.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.1–5 µM.
14. **Bortezomib**:
    - Proteasome inhibitor used in multiple myeloma.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 1–10 nM.
15. **Vorinostat (SAHA)**:
    - Histone deacetylase (HDAC) inhibitor.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.5–5 µM.
16. **Panobinostat**:
    - Potent pan-HDAC inhibitor.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.01–0.2 µM.
17. **Thalidomide**:
    - Immunomodulatory drug used in multiple myeloma.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 5–20 µM.
18. **Rapamycin (Sirolimus)**:
    - mTOR inhibitor with immune-suppressant properties.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 1–10 nM.
19. **Lenalidomide**:
    - Immunomodulatory agent in multiple myeloma.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.1–10 µM.
20. **Auranofin**:
    - Agent originally used for rheumatoid arthritis.
    - Suggested Concentration Range: 0.1–5 µM.

Note: The suggested concentration ranges are general estimations and starting points. Results and optimal concentrations can vary based on experimental conditions such as cell type, culture conditions, and assay sensitivity. It's crucial to determine the IC50 values experimentally in the specific model system being used.
