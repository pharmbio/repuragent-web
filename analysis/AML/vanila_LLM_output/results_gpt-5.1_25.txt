=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 existing, clinically used drugs (or advanced clinical candidates) with evidence of activity against AML, many with data in FLT3‑ITD–positive lines such as MOLM‑13. For each, I include:

- **Primary AML‑relevant target / rationale**
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay concentration range** (a 10‑point series is typical; listed as approximate top and bottom bounds). These ranges are based on reported in‑vitro potencies in AML or similar cell types and are intended as *starting points* for MOLM‑13 viability/proliferation assays (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTT, resazurin) over 48–72 h.

Use DMSO ≤0.1% v/v in all wells, include vehicle controls, and adjust ranges after first pass based on observed curve shifts.

---

## FLT3 and multikinase inhibitors

### 1. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Target/rationale:** Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, PKC). Approved for FLT3‑mutant AML. Strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML lines including MOLM‑13.
- **Suggested range:** 1 nM – 10 µM  
  (e.g., 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000, 3000, 10000 nM)

### 2. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)
- **Target/rationale:** Potent, selective FLT3/AXL inhibitor. Approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mutant AML. Very low‑nM IC₅₀ in MOLM‑13.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 1 µM  
  Narrower follow‑up if very potent (e.g., 0.1–300 nM) after first run.

### 3. Sorafenib
- **Target/rationale:** Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR). Used off‑label in FLT3‑ITD AML, active in MOLM‑13.
- **Suggested range:** 1 nM – 20 µM  

### 4. Quizartinib (AC220)
- **Target/rationale:** Highly potent FLT3 inhibitor, strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML lines.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 1 µM  

### 5. Sunitinib
- **Target/rationale:** Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, VEGFR). Shows anti‑proliferative effects in AML cell lines.
- **Suggested range:** 1 nM – 10 µM  

---

## BCL‑2 and apoptosis pathway

### 6. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Target/rationale:** BCL‑2 inhibitor. Approved in combination regimens in AML. Strong single‑agent activity in many AML lines; synergy with hypomethylating agents.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 5 µM  
  (AML line IC₅₀ often sub‑100 nM; use narrower range later if needed.)

### 7. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)
- **Target/rationale:** BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor; extensive preclinical AML data.
- **Suggested range:** 1 nM – 10 µM  

---

## DNA methylation / epigenetic drugs

### 8. Azacitidine (5‑AZA)
- **Target/rationale:** DNA methyltransferase inhibitor; approved for MDS/AML. At low µM exerts hypomethylating and antiproliferative effects.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 µM – 50 µM  
  (Longer exposure, e.g., 72–120 h, is often needed for robust readouts.)

### 9. Decitabine (5‑aza‑2′‑deoxycytidine)
- **Target/rationale:** DNMT inhibitor; approved for AML/MDS. Similar profile to azacitidine.
- **Suggested range:** 0.01 µM – 10 µM  
  (Again, consider ≥72 h exposure.)

### 10. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Target/rationale:** Pan‑HDAC inhibitor; induces differentiation and apoptosis in AML cells.
- **Suggested range:** 0.01 µM – 20 µM  

### 11. Panobinostat (LBH589)
- **Target/rationale:** Potent pan‑HDAC inhibitor with strong AML preclinical activity.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 1 µM  

---

## IDH, metabolic and signaling inhibitors

### 12. Ivosidenib (AG‑120)
- **Target/rationale:** Mutant IDH1 inhibitor; approved in IDH1‑mutated AML. Even in IDH1‑WT cells, can be useful for comparison and as a negative control if MOLM‑13 is WT.
- **Suggested range:** 0.01 µM – 20 µM  

### 13. Enasidenib (AG‑221)
- **Target/rationale:** Mutant IDH2 inhibitor; approved in IDH2‑mutated AML. Same rationale as above.
- **Suggested range:** 0.01 µM – 20 µM  

### 14. Ruxolitinib
- **Target/rationale:** JAK1/2 inhibitor; can suppress cytokine‑dependent survival pathways in some AML contexts and stromal co‑culture models.
- **Suggested range:** 0.01 µM – 10 µM  

### 15. Everolimus (RAD001)
- **Target/rationale:** mTORC1 inhibitor; mTOR signaling is frequently activated in AML, including FLT3‑ITD lines.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 5 µM  

### 16. Trametinib
- **Target/rationale:** MEK1/2 inhibitor. RAS/MAPK pathway is often active in AML; trametinib shows in‑vitro anti‑proliferative effects in various leukemias.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 5 µM  

---

## Proteasome, HSP90, and other stress‑response targets

### 17. Bortezomib
- **Target/rationale:** Proteasome inhibitor; induces apoptosis in AML cells and can sensitize to other agents.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 1 µM  

### 18. Carfilzomib
- **Target/rationale:** Irreversible proteasome inhibitor; strong cytotoxicity in AML lines in vitro.
- **Suggested range:** 0.1 nM – 1 µM  

### 19. 17‑AAG (Tanespimycin) or 17‑DMAG
- **Target/rationale:** HSP90 inhibitors. FLT3 and other oncogenic kinases in AML are HSP90 clients; these drugs destabilize them, including in MOLM‑13.
- **Suggested range:** 1 nM – 5 µM  

---

## Metabolism / differentiation agent

### 20. All‑trans retinoic acid (ATRA)
- **Target/rationale:** Retinoic acid receptor agonist. Standard in APL, but also exerts differentiation and anti‑proliferative effects in some non‑APL AML lines, often in combination with other agents.
- **Suggested range:** 0.01 µM – 20 µM  

---

### Practical notes for MOLM‑13 IC₅₀ assays

- **Exposure time:** 48–72 h is typical for small‑molecule viability/proliferation assays; DNMT inhibitors often need 72–120 h for full effect.
- **Cell density:** Common starting density: 1–2 × 10⁴ cells / 100 µL per well (96‑well plate), adjusted so vehicle controls remain in log‑phase for the full assay.
- **Serial dilutions:** 3‑fold or 10‑fold serial dilutions from a top dose are standard. Start broad (as suggested), then refine around the IC₅₀ window.
- **Readout:** ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or resazurin are robust for suspension cells like MOLM‑13; combine with trypan blue/flow cytometry for apoptosis vs cytostatic effects if needed.

If you specify whether you want mainly “non‑oncology” repurposing candidates (e.g., metabolic, cardiovascular, anti‑infective drugs) I can refine this list toward drugs farthest from classical AML therapeutics.
