=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a *starting set* of 20 clinically used drugs with published or plausible activity in AML (often including MOLM‑13 or closely related FLT3‑ITD AML lines). For each, I list:

- **Primary oncology‑relevant target(s)**
- **Evidence/notes in AML**
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay concentration range** for MOLM‑13 proliferation (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, etc.)

Use these as *screening ranges*; you will refine based on your own data. All concentrations are for in‑vitro use in standard 72‑h viability/proliferation assays unless noted.

---

## 1. Sorafenib  
- **Type**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR)  
- **Evidence**: Strong FLT3‑ITD AML activity; widely used in preclinical MOLM‑13 studies.  
- **Assay range**: **1 nM – 10 µM** (1:3 or 1:5 serial dilutions; expect IC₅₀ in low‑nM to low‑100‑nM range).

## 2. Midostaurin (PKC412)  
- **Type**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PKC)  
- **Evidence**: Approved for FLT3‑mutant AML; active in MOLM‑13.  
- **Assay range**: **1 nM – 10 µM** (IC₅₀ typically ~10–100 nM in FLT3‑ITD lines).

## 3. Quizartinib (AC220)  
- **Type**: Selective FLT3 inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Potently kills FLT3‑ITD lines (MOLM‑13, MV4‑11).  
- **Assay range**: **0.1 nM – 1 µM** (IC₅₀ often <5 nM).

## 4. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)  
- **Type**: FLT3/AXL inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mutant AML; robust activity in MOLM‑13.  
- **Assay range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**.

## 5. Dasatinib  
- **Type**: SRC/ABL, KIT, PDGFR inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Reported anti‑proliferative effects in subsets of AML; some lines including FLT3‑ITD show sensitivity.  
- **Assay range**: **1 nM – 10 µM** (IC₅₀ often ~10–200 nM for sensitive lines).

## 6. Nilotinib  
- **Type**: BCR‑ABL/KIT inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Modest activity in some AML models (esp. KIT or ABL‑dependent); may synergize with other agents.  
- **Assay range**: **10 nM – 20 µM**.

## 7. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)  
- **Type**: BCL‑2 inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Approved in AML (with HMA or LDAC); potent activity in MOLM‑13; often sub‑µM IC₅₀.  
- **Assay range**: **1 nM – 5 µM** (IC₅₀ frequently 5–200 nM in BCL‑2–dependent AML).

## 8. Idelalisib  
- **Type**: PI3Kδ inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: PI3K/AKT signaling is active in AML; idelalisib has shown preclinical anti‑AML activity.  
- **Assay range**: **10 nM – 20 µM** (start broad; expect µM‑range effects).

## 9. Ibrutinib  
- **Type**: BTK inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: BTK involvement in AML signaling; reported to reduce proliferation in some AML lines and stem/progenitor cells.  
- **Assay range**: **10 nM – 20 µM**.

## 10. Ruxolitinib  
- **Type**: JAK1/2 inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: JAK/STAT hyperactivation in some AML subsets; preclinical suppression of AML proliferation and cytokine‑driven survival.  
- **Assay range**: **10 nM – 20 µM**.

## 11. Trametinib  
- **Type**: MEK1/2 inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: RAS/RAF/MEK pathway active in many AMLs; MEK inhibition can impair proliferation and sensitize to other agents (including FLT3 inhibitors).  
- **Assay range**: **0.5 nM – 5 µM** (IC₅₀ often in low‑nM to 100‑nM range).

## 12. Everolimus (RAD001)  
- **Type**: mTORC1 inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: mTOR signaling drives AML proliferation; everolimus inhibits growth of several AML lines and leukemic stem cells.  
- **Assay range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

## 13. Panobinostat (LBH589)  
- **Type**: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Strong pro‑apoptotic/anti‑proliferative activity in AML lines including FLT3‑ITD; often synergistic with FLT3/BCL‑2 inhibitors.  
- **Assay range**: **0.5 nM – 1 µM** (IC₅₀ typically 5–100 nM).

## 14. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- **Type**: HDAC inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Broad anti‑AML activity; differentiation and apoptosis induction in multiple lines.  
- **Assay range**: **50 nM – 20 µM** (IC₅₀ frequently ~0.5–5 µM).

## 15. Azacitidine  
- **Type**: DNA methyltransferase inhibitor (nucleoside analog)  
- **Evidence**: Standard of care in AML/MDS; in vitro anti‑proliferative and differentiation effects in AML cells, including FLT3‑ITD lines.  
- **Assay range**: **0.1 µM – 50 µM** (longer exposure, e.g., 72–120 h, needed due to incorporation mechanism).

## 16. Decitabine  
- **Type**: DNMT inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Similar to azacitidine; widely used AML drug; reduces proliferation and induces apoptosis/differentiation.  
- **Assay range**: **0.01 µM – 10 µM** (72–120 h exposure).

## 17. Thalidomide  
- **Type**: IMiD; affects cereblon, TNF‑α, angiogenesis  
- **Evidence**: Limited clinical AML efficacy, but preclinical data show effects on AML cell survival and microenvironment; more useful in combo.  
- **Assay range**: **0.1 µM – 100 µM** (wide range; expect higher µM needed).

## 18. Lenalidomide  
- **Type**: IMiD (cereblon‑modulating)  
- **Evidence**: Some activity in AML/MDS with specific genetic contexts (e.g., del(5q)); moderate direct cytotoxicity in vitro.  
- **Assay range**: **0.01 µM – 50 µM**.

## 19. Metformin  
- **Type**: Biguanide; AMPK activator, mitochondrial complex I inhibitor  
- **Evidence**: Multiple reports of anti‑leukemic effects via metabolic stress and mTOR inhibition; often requires higher µM–mM concentrations in vitro.  
- **Assay range**: **0.1 mM – 20 mM** (consider 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 20 mM).

## 20. Atorvastatin (or Simvastatin)  
- **Type**: HMG‑CoA reductase inhibitor (statin)  
- **Evidence**: Mevalonate pathway crucial for leukemic cell survival; statins show anti‑AML activity in vitro and synergy with cytotoxics.  
- **Assay range**:  
  - Atorvastatin: **0.1 µM – 50 µM**  
  - Simvastatin (active acid form): **0.1 µM – 20 µM**

### Practical notes for IC₅₀ assays in MOLM‑13

1. **Dose spacing**  
   - Use 8–12 concentrations per drug (e.g., 1:3 or 1:5 dilutions) spanning at least 3–4 orders of magnitude around the expected IC₅₀.
   - Include DMSO‑only control; keep final DMSO ≤0.1–0.2%.

2. **Exposure time**  
   - 72 h is standard for proliferation/viability IC₅₀ in AML lines.  
   - For hypomethylating agents (azacitidine/decitabine), you may also test 96–120 h.

3. **Readouts**  
   - CellTiter‑Glo or similar ATP‑based assay is common for MOLM‑13.  
   - Confirm selected hits with annexin V/7‑AAD or PI and optionally cell‑cycle analysis.

4. **Replicates**  
   - At least triplicate wells per concentration; perform ≥2 independent experiments.
