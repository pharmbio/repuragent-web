=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing drugs (approved or clinically developed) that have documented or plausible activity against AML — especially FLT3-ITD AML lines such as MOLM‑13 — together with a practical, experimentally testable concentration range and a recommended dilution scheme for an IC50/proliferation assay. For each drug I give a short rationale (mechanism and why it’s relevant to MOLM‑13), a suggested top concentration for the assay, a simple dilution series (so an IC50 is likely to be captured), and a note when special handling is needed.

General assay notes (apply to all compounds)
- Cell line: MOLM‑13 (suspension). Seed density: typically 0.5–2 × 10^5 cells/mL; adjust to keep exponential growth over the assay period.  
- Readout: viable cell/proliferation assay (CellTiter‑Glo, resazurin, MTS/WST) or flow cytometric live/dead.  
- Exposure time: 48–72 h is standard for proliferation/IC50. For apoptosis‑inducing drugs (venetoclax) 24–48 h can be informative; for epigenetic drugs (azacitidine, decitabine) longer exposures may be required. Keep DMSO ≤0.1% final.  
- Dilution scheme: I recommend a 10‑point, 1:3 serial dilution from the indicated top concentration (or an 8–10 point series with 1:2 if you expect a narrow window). This covers ~4–6 log units and will usually capture IC50s from low nM to low µM. Include positive controls (e.g., midostaurin, cytarabine) and negative (vehicle) controls. Validate compound solubility and stability in assay medium.

- Top concentration: 1 μM. Recommended series: 1 μM → 1/3 dilutions (10 points: 1 μM down to ~0.0005 μM).  
- Expect IC50 in low nM for FLT3‑ITD lines.

Practical implementation tips
- Start with the recommended top concentration and a 10‑point 1:3 series. If a compound shows no activity at the top concentration, repeat with a higher top (while checking solubility/toxicity). If activity is confined to the highest two wells, repeat with a higher top concentration or smaller dilution factor. If activity is complete below the lowest conc, repeat with lower top conc / finer dilution.  
- For agents requiring co‑factors (e.g., disulfiram + Cu), include the cofactor in all assay wells.  
- For hydrophobic compounds, ensure proper DMSO stock preparation and keep final DMSO ≤0.1–0.2% to avoid vehicle toxicity.  
- For slow‑acting epigenetic drugs (azacitidine/decitabine) or agents affecting differentiation, consider longer exposures or sequential dosing.  
- Include at least 3 biological replicates and technical triplicates per concentration to estimate IC50 reliably; fit data to a 4‑parameter logistic curve.

Caveats and references
- Cell‑line sensitivity can vary by clone, culture conditions, and assay readout. MOLM‑13 is FLT3‑ITD positive and is typically sensitive to FLT3 inhibitors and BCL‑2/CDK9 pathway perturbation, but verify activity empirically.  
- This list mixes approved therapies and investigational compounds; some (OTX015, dinaciclib, alvocidib) are not approved for general use but have clinical data. The anthelmintics and auranofin are repurposing candidates with preclinical support.  
- Before running experiments, check primary literature for reported IC50s in MOLM‑13 (to set expectations) and confirm compound sourcing/purity.
