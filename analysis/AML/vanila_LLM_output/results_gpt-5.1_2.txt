=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 *already existing* (approved or late‑stage) drugs with evidence of activity in AML (often including FLT3‑ITD–positive lines such as MOLM13). For each, I include:  
- **Primary relevant target(s)**  
- **Typical in‑vitro IC₅₀ range in AML/MOLM13 (when available)**  
- **Suggested assay concentration range** (for a standard 72‑h cell‑viability IC₅₀ assay in MOLM13).  

Use these as starting points; you should always do a small pilot range‑finding experiment first, as sensitivity varies by lot, medium, serum, and readout.

---

## 1. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Type/target**: Multi‑kinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, PKC). FDA‑approved for FLT3‑mut AML.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: ~1–10 nM for FLT3 phosphorylation; proliferation IC₅₀ typically ~5–50 nM.  
- **Suggested test range**: **0.1 nM – 1 μM** (e.g., 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000 nM).

---

## 2. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)
- **Type/target**: FLT3/AXL inhibitor. FDA‑approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mut AML.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Often ~1–10 nM for FLT3‑ITD AML cell lines including MOLM13.  
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 nM – 1 μM**.

---

## 3. Quizartinib (AC220)
- **Type/target**: Potent FLT3 inhibitor.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Frequently reported ~0.1–10 nM in FLT3‑ITD lines (MOLM13, MV4‑11).  
- **Suggested range**: **0.01 nM – 300 nM** (very potent; you can compress upper range to 100 nM).

---

## 4. Sorafenib
- **Type/target**: Multi‑kinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR). Approved for several solid tumors.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: FLT3‑ITD AML lines usually in the low‑nM to low‑100‑nM range.  
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 10 μM**.

---

## 5. Decitabine
- **Type/target**: DNA hypomethylating agent; FDA‑approved for MDS/AML.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Typically ~0.1–5 μM (time‑dependent; cytostatic and cytotoxic).  
- **Suggested range**: **10 nM – 20 μM**.

---

## 6. Azacitidine
- **Type/target**: DNA/RNA hypomethylating agent; FDA‑approved for MDS/AML.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Often in the ~0.1–10 μM range with 48–72 h exposure.  
- **Suggested range**: **10 nM – 30 μM**.

---

## 7. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Type/target**: BCL‑2 inhibitor; widely used in AML in combination with HMA or LDAC.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Commonly ~1–100 nM in BCL‑2–dependent AML lines; MOLM13 shows strong sensitivity in many reports.  
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 nM – 3 μM**.

---

## 8. Panobinostat (LBH589)
- **Type/target**: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor (epigenetic modulator), approved for multiple myeloma.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Typically ~5–100 nM in AML cell lines.  
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 1 μM**.

---

## 9. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Type/target**: HDAC inhibitor; approved for CTCL.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Often in the 0.5–5 μM range for proliferation/viability.  
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 μM – 20 μM**.

---

## 10. Romidepsin
- **Type/target**: Class I HDAC inhibitor; approved for T‑cell lymphomas.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Commonly ~2–50 nM in AML lines; strong pro‑apoptotic effect.  
- **Suggested range**: **0.5 nM – 500 nM**.

---

## 11. Ruxolitinib
- **Type/target**: JAK1/2 inhibitor; approved for myelofibrosis, PV, GVHD.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: AML sensitivity is variable; typically ~0.5–10 μM in vitro.  
- **Suggested range**: **50 nM – 30 μM**.

---

## 12. Dasatinib
- **Type/target**: Multi‑kinase inhibitor (BCR‑ABL, SRC, KIT). Approved for CML/ALL.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Often ~10–200 nM for AML cells, depending on SRC/KIT dependence.  
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 10 μM**.

---

## 13. Imatinib
- **Type/target**: BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR inhibitor; CML/GIST drug.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Weak to moderate in unselected AML; IC₅₀ often in the **5–20 μM** range unless specific KIT/PDGFR lesions.  
- **Suggested range**: **0.5 μM – 50 μM**.

---

## 14. Ibrutinib
- **Type/target**: BTK inhibitor; approved for several B‑cell malignancies.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Mixed data; some AML lines show proliferation IC₅₀ ~1–10 μM, often through off‑target TEC/SRC inhibition.  
- **Suggested range**: **0.5 μM – 30 μM**.

---

## 15. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide
(Prefer lenalidomide for in‑vitro work due to better solubility and potency.)  
- **Type/target**: IMiD (CRBN‑dependent degradation of IKZF1/3 and others); approved for myeloma, MDS del(5q).  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Variable; often ~5–50 μM in AML lines.  
- **Suggested range**: **1 μM – 100 μM**.

---

## 16. Bortezomib
- **Type/target**: Proteasome inhibitor; approved for myeloma and mantle cell lymphoma.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Frequently ~5–50 nM in AML cell lines for viability.  
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 500 nM**.

---

## 17. Carfilzomib
- **Type/target**: Irreversible proteasome inhibitor; approved for myeloma.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Typically in the low‑nM to mid‑nM range (e.g., 5–50 nM).  
- **Suggested range**: **0.5 nM – 300 nM**.

---

## 18. Everolimus (RAD001)
- **Type/target**: mTORC1 inhibitor; approved for several solid tumors.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Often 1–10 μM for proliferation; effects can be more cytostatic.  
- **Suggested range**: **50 nM – 20 μM**.

---

## 19. Sunitinib
- **Type/target**: Multi‑target RTK inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, VEGFR, PDGFR). Approved for RCC, GIST.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: FLT3‑ITD AML often in the ~10–300 nM range.  
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 10 μM**.

---

## 20. Nilotinib
- **Type/target**: BCR‑ABL and KIT inhibitor; approved for CML.  
- **AML/MOLM13 IC₅₀**: Activity in AML is modest; proliferation IC₅₀ usually ~1–10 μM unless specific KIT/ABL lesions.  
- **Suggested range**: **100 nM – 30 μM**.

---

### Practical notes for your IC₅₀ assay in MOLM13

- **Assay duration**: 72 h recommended for most cytostatic/cytotoxic small molecules in AML; 48 h may be sufficient for fast‑acting kinase inhibitors.  
- **Readout**: Standard luminescent ATP (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo) or resazurin (AlamarBlue) is suitable for MOLM13.  
- **DMSO**: Keep final DMSO ≤0.1% (≤0.2% at most) in all wells, including controls.  
- **Dilution plan**: Use ~8–10 half‑log (3.16‑fold) or 3‑fold serial dilutions spanning the suggested ranges.  
- **Replicates**: At least triplicate wells per concentration; include positive control (e.g., quizartinib or gilteritinib) and vehicle control.
