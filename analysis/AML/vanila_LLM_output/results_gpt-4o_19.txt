Results:

Repurposing existing drugs for the treatment of acute myeloid leukemia (AML) is an exciting area of research, as it can potentially lead to new therapeutic options by using drugs that are already approved for other indications. When looking at existing drugs that might be repurposed, several factors such as existing evidence of activity against AML cells, mechanism of action, safety, and pharmacokinetics are considered. 4. **Itraconazole**: An antifungal agent with modulation of the Hedgehog pathway, tested around 1-10 µM.

7. **Disulfiram**: An alcohol dehydrogenase inhibitor, tested at 1-10 µM.
8. **Ruxolitinib**: A JAK1/2 inhibitor used in myelofibrosis, tested around 1-10 µM.
9. **Metformin**: An antidiabetic drug shown to impact cancer metabolism, tested at 1-10 mM.
10. **Lovastatin**: A cholesterol-lowering agent targeting HMG-CoA reductase, tested at 1-10 µM.
11. **Thalidomide**: Used in multiple myeloma, can be tested around 1-10 µM.
12. **Minocycline**: An antibiotic with anti-apoptotic properties tested at 10-20 µM.
13. **Clofazimine**: An anti-leprosy drug with potential anti-cancer effects, tested around 1-10 µM.
14. **Promethazine**: An antihistamine, shown to have cytotoxic effects on cancer cells, tested at around 10-30 µM.
15. **Fenretinide (4-HPR)**: A synthetic retinoid studied in various cancers, tested at 1-10 µM.
16. **Quinacrine**: An antimalarial drug, tested at 1-10 µM.
17. **Albendazole**: An anti-parasitic agent, tested at 1-10 µM.
18. **Riluzole**: Used in ALS with actions on glutamate release, tested at 1-10 µM.
19. **Ciclopirox**: An antifungal agent, tested at 1-10 µM.
20. **Propranolol**: A beta-blocker with anti-proliferative effects, tested at 10-50 µM.

Please note that the IC50 values can vary significantly depending on the experimental setup, such as cell line differences, assay type, and conditions. Thus, initial testing around the suggested concentrations with a wider range may be warranted to accurately determine the IC50 in MOLM13 cells or other AML cell lines.

Moreover, these suggestions are based on available literature and do not replace the need for thorough preclinical and clinical investigations to ascertain efficacy and safety in the context of AML.
