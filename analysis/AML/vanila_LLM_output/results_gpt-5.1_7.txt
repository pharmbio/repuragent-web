=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used (or late‑stage) drugs with published/strongly suggested activity against AML, including MOLM‑13 where data are available. For each, I give:

- **Primary current indication / target**
- **Rationale for AML/MOLM‑13**
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay concentration range** (final concentration in wells for a 72‑h proliferation/viability assay such as CellTiter‑Glo or MTT).  
  Use a **10‑point 3‑fold or 5‑fold dilution series** spanning this range (e.g., 0.5 nM–10 µM).  
  Values are based on reported IC₅₀s in AML lines plus typical in‑vitro test ranges for small molecules.

All concentrations are for **in vitro cell culture**, not for human use.

---

## FLT3 / multi‑kinase inhibitors

These are especially relevant because MOLM‑13 is **FLT3‑ITD–positive**.

1. **Midostaurin (PKC412)**
- Indication/target: FLT3/multi‑kinase inhibitor; approved for FLT3‑mutated AML.
- Rationale: Strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML lines including MOLM‑13; induces apoptosis and reduces proliferation.
- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 5 µM**  
  (IC₅₀ in FLT3‑ITD AML usually ~5–50 nM; include higher doses to fully define curve.)

2. **Gilteritinib (ASP2215)**
- Indication/target: Selective FLT3/AXL inhibitor; approved in relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mutated AML.
- Rationale: Very potent on FLT3‑ITD lines including MOLM‑13; sub‑nanomolar to low‑nanomolar IC₅₀ reported for phospho‑FLT3 and viability.
- Suggested range: **0.1 nM – 1 µM**  
  (Focus around 0.3–100 nM; include low and high extremes.)

3. **Quizartinib (AC220)**
- Indication/target: FLT3 inhibitor (approved/late stage in some regions).
- Rationale: One of the most potent FLT3 inhibitors; MOLM‑13 and MV4‑11 highly sensitive.
- Suggested range: **0.1 nM – 1 µM**  
  (Common IC₅₀ in FLT3‑ITD AML ~1–10 nM.)

4. **Sorafenib**
- Indication/target: Multi‑kinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR, FLT3, etc.); approved for solid tumors.
- Rationale: Shows activity in FLT3‑ITD AML including MOLM‑13; often used off‑label in AML.
- Suggested range: **5 nM – 10 µM**  
  (IC₅₀ often ~50–500 nM; AML can vary, so keep broad.)

5. **Crenolanib**
- Indication/target: FLT3/PDGFR inhibitor (investigational in AML).
- Rationale: Potent vs FLT3‑ITD and TKD mutants; MOLM‑13 is typically responsive.
- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 2 µM**  
  (Reported IC₅₀ in MOLM‑13 often ~1–10 nM.)

6. **Dasatinib**
- Indication/target: SRC/ABL family TKI; approved for CML/Ph+ ALL.
- Rationale: Inhibits multiple kinases (SRC, LYN, etc.) implicated in AML survival; documented anti‑proliferative effects in some AML lines.
- Suggested range: **1 nM – 5 µM**  
  (Cytotoxicity in leukemia generally tens of nM to low µM.)

## BCL‑2 and apoptosis‑related agents

7. **Venetoclax (ABT‑199)**
- Indication/target: BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved for AML (with HMA/LDAC) and CLL.
- Rationale: AML, especially FLT3‑mutant, can be highly BCL‑2 dependent; strong preclinical activity in MOLM‑13 reported (synergistic with FLT3 inhibitors).
- Suggested range: **0.1 nM – 5 µM**  
  (AML IC₅₀ often ~1–50 nM; but include wider.)

8. **Navitoclax (ABT‑263)**
- Indication/target: BCL‑2/BCL‑xL/BCL‑w inhibitor; clinical in lymphoid malignancies.
- Rationale: Broader BCL‑2 family inhibition; can show strong activity in AML cell lines.
- Suggested range: **1 nM – 5 µM**  
  (IC₅₀ in AML typically tens of nM to low µM.)

---

## Epigenetic and transcription‑related

9. **Azacitidine**
- Indication/target: DNA hypomethylating agent; approved for MDS/AML.
- Rationale: Reduces DNA methylation and induces differentiation/apoptosis in AML lines including MOLM‑13.
- Suggested range: **0.1 µM – 50 µM**  
  (In vitro demethylation/proliferation effects usually 0.5–10 µM; need higher for complete curve.)

10. **Decitabine**
- Indication/target: DNA hypomethylating agent; approved for MDS/AML.
- Rationale: Similar to azacitidine; commonly used in vitro with AML cell lines.
- Suggested range: **0.05 µM – 20 µM**  
  (Relevant IC₅₀ usually around 0.1–5 µM; stable sampling up to 20 µM.)

11. **Vorinostat (SAHA)**
- Indication/target: HDAC inhibitor; approved for CTCL.
- Rationale: HDAC inhibition is effective in many AML models; induces apoptosis and differentiation; MOLM‑13 is typically responsive.
- Suggested range: **0.05 µM – 20 µM**  
  (Leukemia IC₅₀ ~0.5–5 µM; extend either way.)

12. **Panobinostat (LBH589)**
- Indication/target: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma.
- Rationale: Very potent HDAC inhibitor with strong activity in AML cells including FLT3‑ITD models.
- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 1 µM**  
  (Hematologic IC₅₀ often 5–50 nM.)

13. **JQ1** *(tool compound, not approved but widely used; can be a benchmark for BET inhibition)*
- Indication/target: BET bromodomain inhibitor (BRD4, etc.).
- Rationale: BET inhibition down‑regulates MYC and key survival genes in AML; MOLM‑13 is a standard BET‑sensitive model.
- Suggested range: **5 nM – 5 µM**  
  (AML IC₅₀ usually 50–500 nM.)

14. **Ibrutinib**
- Indication/target: BTK inhibitor; approved for CLL/MCL and other B‑cell malignancies.
- Rationale: Off‑target kinase inhibition (including some TEC/SRC family members) and BTK‑axis roles in myeloid signaling; several reports of anti‑proliferative activity in subsets of AML.
- Suggested range: **0.1 µM – 20 µM**  
  (Effects in non‑B‑cell contexts typically at 1–10 µM.)

## Proteasome / stress‑response / cell‑cycle agents

15. **Bortezomib**
- Indication/target: Proteasome inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma and mantle cell lymphoma.
- Rationale: Triggers ER stress and apoptosis in AML cells; synergizes with FLT3 and BCL‑2 inhibitors; MOLM‑13 shows sensitivity.
- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 500 nM**  
  (AML IC₅₀ often 5–50 nM; keep upper range for complete curve.)

16. **Carfilzomib**
- Indication/target: Irreversible proteasome inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma.
- Rationale: Similar rationale to bortezomib; potent cytotoxicity in AML lines in vitro.
- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 500 nM**  
  (Typical IC₅₀ tens of nM.)

17. **Palbociclib**
- Indication/target: CDK4/6 inhibitor; approved for HR+/HER2‑ breast cancer.
- Rationale: AML cells with intact RB pathway or high cyclin D dependence can be sensitive; some data in AML cell lines show G1 arrest and reduced proliferation.
- Suggested range: **0.05 µM – 20 µM**  
  (IC₅₀ in various hematologic lines often 0.1–5 µM.)

18. **Ruxolitinib**
- Indication/target: JAK1/2 inhibitor; approved for myelofibrosis, PV.
- Rationale: Can inhibit JAK/STAT‑driven signaling pathways relevant in some AML subsets; combinations with FLT3 or BCL‑2 inhibitors often explored.
- Suggested range: **0.05 µM – 20 µM**  
  (Cell‑based JAK/STAT inhibition generally at 0.1–5 µM.)

## Metabolic and “non‑oncology” drugs with AML activity

19. **Metformin**
- Indication/target: Type 2 diabetes; AMPK activation, mitochondrial complex I inhibition.
- Rationale: Alters cellular metabolism and can inhibit proliferation/induce apoptosis in AML cells at high micromolar–millimolar range; acts as a tool for metabolic stress.
- Suggested range: **0.1 mM – 20 mM**  
  (In vitro anti‑proliferative effects usually ≥1–5 mM.)

20. **Arsenic trioxide (ATO)**
- Indication/target: PML‑RARα degradation and oxidative stress; approved for APL.
- Rationale: Also has activity in non‑APL AML cell lines, including induction of ROS and apoptosis in FLT3‑ITD AML models.
- Suggested range: **0.1 µM – 20 µM**  
  (Leukemia IC₅₀ commonly ~0.5–5 µM; higher doses can be very toxic.)

## Practical notes for your MOLM‑13 IC₅₀ assays

- **Assay duration**: 72 h is standard for proliferation/viability; you may also test 48 h to capture early effects.
- **Plate format**: 96‑ or 384‑well; ensure MOLM‑13 cells are in exponential growth phase (e.g., 1–2×10⁵ cells/mL seeding).
- **Vehicle**: Keep DMSO ≤0.1% (v/v). Include vehicle‑only and media‑only controls.
- **Curve design**:  
  - For potent TKIs and BCL‑2 inhibitors: starting at 1–10 µM, 10‑point 3‑fold dilution down to ~0.5–1 pM.  
  - For less potent / cytotoxic agents (metformin, azacitidine, etc.): 0.1–0.2× to 5–10× above/below literature IC₅₀.
- **Readouts**: ATP‑based viability assay (CellTiter‑Glo) works well for suspension lines like MOLM‑13; confirm hits by annexin‑V/PI apoptosis and Western blot for pathway markers (e.g., p‑FLT3, cleaved PARP, BCL‑2 family).
