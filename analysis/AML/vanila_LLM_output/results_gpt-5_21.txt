=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 approved, “existing” drugs with published activity against AML cell lines (and/or FLT3-ITD–positive models that closely mirror MOLM13). For each, I’ve included a concise rationale and an experimentally practical concentration range for 10-point, 3-fold serial dilutions in a 72-hour IC50 assay on MOLM13. Keep final DMSO ≤0.1% (v/v) across wells.

FLT3/MAPK pathway–focused (high likelihood of activity in FLT3-ITD MOLM13)
- Ponatinib (CML/Ph+ ALL TKI; multi-kinase incl. FLT3): Very potent versus FLT3-ITD AML lines. Test range: 0.1 nM–1 μM.
- Sorafenib (RCC/HCC; RAF/VEGFR/FLT3 inhibitor): Longstanding off-label activity in FLT3-ITD AML (incl. MOLM13-like models). Test range: 0.5 nM–5 μM.
- Sunitinib (RCC; multi-kinase incl. FLT3): Inhibits proliferation of FLT3-ITD AML lines. Test range: 1 nM–10 μM.
- Cabozantinib (RCC/HCC; MET/AXL/VEGFR; reported FLT3 activity): Active in FLT3-ITD AML in vitro. Test range: 0.5 nM–5 μM.
- Axitinib (RCC; VEGFR; reported FLT3 activity): Inhibits FLT3-ITD AML cells in vitro. Test range: 1 nM–10 μM.
- Regorafenib (CRC/GIST/HCC; multi-kinase incl. FLT3): Reported activity in FLT3-ITD AML models. Test range: 1 nM–10 μM.
- Trametinib (melanoma; MEK1/2 inhibitor): Blocks downstream FLT3/MAPK signaling; antiproliferative in AML lines. Test range: 0.1 nM–5 μM.

Signaling/kinase or translation inhibitors with AML activity
- Fostamatinib (ITP; SYK inhibitor; active moiety R406): SYK is a survival node in AML; activity reported in MOLM13-like lines. Test range: 30 nM–30 μM.
- Omacetaxine mepesuccinate (CML; protein synthesis inhibitor): Broadly cytotoxic to myeloid leukemia cells. Test range: 0.5 nM–1 μM.
- Selinexor (MM/DLBCL; XPO1 inhibitor): Prominent single-agent AML activity in vitro (incl. FLT3-ITD lines). Test range: 1 nM–10 μM.
- Bortezomib (MM/MCL; proteasome inhibitor): Potent anti-AML activity in many cell lines. Test range: 0.5 nM–500 nM.

Epigenetic modifiers with in vitro AML activity
- Panobinostat (MM; pan-HDAC inhibitor): Active at low-nanomolar in AML lines. Test range: 0.5 nM–1 μM.
- Romidepsin (CTCL; HDAC1/2 inhibitor): Very potent; strong AML cytotoxicity reported. Test range: 0.1 nM–100 nM.
- Vorinostat (CTCL; pan-HDAC inhibitor): Inhibits proliferation and induces apoptosis in AML cell lines. Test range: 10 nM–50 μM.

Metabolism/mitochondria–targeting agents
- Tigecycline (antibiotic; mitochondrial translation inhibitor): AML cells (incl. FLT3-ITD lines) are sensitive. Test range: 10 nM–50 μM.
- Niclosamide (anthelmintic; mitochondrial uncoupler/Wnt inhibitor): Cytotoxic to AML lines at low micromolar. Test range: 10 nM–20 μM.
- Auranofin (anti-rheumatic; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor): Induces ROS and apoptosis in AML cells. Test range: 10 nM–20 μM.
- Clofazimine (leprosy; redox/mitochondrial effects): Kills leukemia cells via ROS and membrane perturbation. Test range: 1 nM–10 μM.

STAT5/autophagy/ALDH-associated
- Pimozide (antipsychotic; STAT5 inhibitor): STAT5 is critical in FLT3-ITD AML (e.g., MOLM13); pimozide inhibits growth. Test range: 10 nM–30 μM.
- Disulfiram + Cu2+ (alcohol aversion; ALDH inhibitor/copper-dependent proteasome/NPL4 effects): AML cells sensitive with copper supplementation. Test range: 10 nM–10 μM disulfiram with 1 μM CuCl2 co-supplement.

Assay setup suggestions (to improve data quality)
- Cell line/conditions: MOLM13 (FLT3-ITD); 3,000–5,000 cells per well (96-well), RPMI-1640 + 10% FBS, 72 h exposure.
- Readout: ATP-based luminescence (e.g., CellTiter-Glo) or resazurin; include vehicle controls matched for DMSO ≤0.1% (v/v).
- Dilution scheme: 10-point, 3-fold serial dilutions spanning the ranges above; run ≥3 technical replicates.
- Special handling:
  - Disulfiram is unstable; prepare fresh and include 1 μM CuCl2; protect from light.
  - Tigecycline and romidepsin are light sensitive; minimize light exposure.
  - Niclosamide/clofazimine are highly lipophilic; pre-dissolve in DMSO (10 mM stocks), ensure thorough mixing; consider adding 0.1% Pluronic F-68 if precipitation is observed.
- Optional controls: Positive cytotoxic control (e.g., staurosporine 1 μM), FLT3 reference inhibitor (e.g., quizartinib, for benchmarking only), and mevalonate rescue for on-target validation with statins if you choose to include them in follow-up.

These ranges are chosen to bracket reported AML potencies and accommodate serum binding/assay-to-assay variability. If you need a short “top 8” prioritized set specifically for MOLM13, start with ponatinib, sorafenib, sunitinib, cabozantinib, axitinib, regorafenib, pimozide, and tigecycline.
