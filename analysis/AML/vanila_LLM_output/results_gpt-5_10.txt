=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 approved, “existing” drugs with published anti-AML activity (many with explicit data in FLT3-ITD AML lines such as MOLM13/MV4-11). For each, I summarize the rationale and a literature-anchored potency estimate, then suggest a practical concentration span to explore for an in vitro IC50 determination in AML cells. Notes: potency can vary with serum, exposure time, cell density, and readout; use these spans as starting brackets and refine around observed responses. References are representative, not exhaustive.

1) Sorafenib (multi-kinase; approved for HCC/RCC)
- Rationale: Potent FLT3-ITD inhibitor; robust activity in MOLM13/MV4-11.
- Reported AML cell IC50: ~5–50 nM in FLT3-ITD lines.
- Concentration span to test: 0.5–500 nM.
- Refs: Pratz et al., Clin Cancer Res 2009; Zarrinkar et al., Blood 2009.

2) Ponatinib (ABL/FLT3/multi-kinase; CML/Ph+ ALL)
- Rationale: Strong FLT3-ITD activity; active in MOLM13 models.
- Reported IC50: low single-digit nM in FLT3-ITD AML lines.
- Span: 0.05–100 nM.
- Refs: Smith et al., Blood 2013; Gozgit et al., Leukemia 2011.

3) Sunitinib (multi-kinase including FLT3; RCC/GIST)
- Rationale: Inhibits FLT3-ITD; active in MOLM13/MV4-11.
- Reported IC50: ~10–60 nM in FLT3-ITD lines.
- Span: 1–300 nM.
- Refs: Zhang et al., Blood 2006; Zarrinkar et al., Blood 2009.

4) Dasatinib (SRC/ABL family; CML/Ph+ ALL)
- Rationale: Inhibits SRC-family/other kinases; preclinical AML activity including FLT3-ITD contexts.
- Reported IC50: ~20–300 nM (cell-line dependent).
- Span: 1–3000 nM.
- Refs: Weisberg et al., Blood 2007; Quintás-Cardama et al., Cancer 2011.

5) Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor; MM/MCL)
- Rationale: Proteasome inhibition kills AML cells; synergy with FLT3 blockade.
- Reported IC50: ~5–20 nM in AML lines (incl. FLT3-ITD).
- Span: 0.5–200 nM.
- Refs: Attar et al., Clin Cancer Res 2008; Podar et al., Leukemia 2008.

6) Carfilzomib (proteasome inhibitor; MM)
- Rationale: Potent proteasome inhibitor; AML cytotoxicity reported (incl. FLT3-ITD lines).
- Reported IC50: ~10–50 nM.
- Span: 0.5–1000 nM.
- Refs: Niewerth et al., Leukemia 2014; Eytan et al., Oncotarget 2016.

7) Selinexor (XPO1 inhibitor; MM/DLBCL)
- Rationale: Nuclear export inhibition; strong activity in AML including MOLM13/MV4-11.
- Reported IC50: ~50–200 nM in AML lines.
- Span: 10–5000 nM.
- Refs: Etchin et al., Sci Transl Med 2013; Ranganathan et al., Clin Cancer Res 2012.

8) Tigecycline (antibiotic; mitochondrial translation inhibitor)
- Rationale: Selectively targets mitochondrial translation; effective in AML cells and stem/progenitors; MOLM13 used in multiple studies.
- Reported IC50: ~1–5 µM.
- Span: 0.3–30 µM.
- Refs: Skrtić et al., Cancer Cell 2011; Skrtić et al., Sci Transl Med 2014.

9) Niclosamide (anthelmintic; Wnt/STAT3/OXPHOS modulator)
- Rationale: Broad anticancer activity; AML cell killing incl. FLT3-ITD contexts reported.
- Reported IC50: ~0.2–1 µM.
- Span: 0.05–10 µM.
- Refs: Liu et al., Oncotarget 2016; Wieland et al., Leukemia 2013.

10) Auranofin (anti-rheumatic; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor)
- Rationale: Increases ROS, disrupts redox homeostasis; AML cytotoxicity including MOLM13.
- Reported IC50: ~0.3–2 µM.
- Span: 0.05–10 µM.
- Refs: Fiskus et al., Blood 2014; Raninga et al., Leukemia 2015.

11) Thioridazine (antipsychotic; dopamine receptor antagonist)
- Rationale: Targets leukemia stem/progenitor cells; AML activity in vitro and ex vivo.
- Reported IC50: ~1–10 µM.
- Span: 0.3–30 µM.
- Refs: Sadarangani et al., Sci Transl Med 2015; Zahreddine et al., Cell 2012.

12) Pimozide (antipsychotic; STAT5 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Inhibits STAT5 signaling downstream of FLT3-ITD; active in MOLM13/MV4-11.
- Reported IC50: ~2–5 µM.
- Span: 0.1–20 µM.
- Refs: Nelson et al., Blood 2011; Wu et al., Leukemia 2010.

13) Vorinostat/SAHA (HDAC inhibitor; CTCL)
- Rationale: Epigenetic modulation; AML cytotoxicity and differentiation effects.
- Reported IC50: ~0.5–2 µM.
- Span: 0.05–10 µM.
- Refs: Rosato et al., Clin Cancer Res 2003; Fiskus et al., Cancer Res 2009.

14) Panobinostat (HDAC inhibitor; MM)
- Rationale: Potent pan-HDACi; active in AML lines including FLT3-ITD, often synergistic with kinase inhibitors.
- Reported IC50: ~5–50 nM.
- Span: 0.001–1 µM.
- Refs: Fiskus et al., Clin Cancer Res 2012; Heider et al., Leukemia 2013.

15) Everolimus (mTORC1 inhibitor; RCC/TSC)
- Rationale: PI3K/mTOR pathway is active in AML; mTOR inhibitors reduce proliferation and survival.
- Reported IC50 (viability/growth): tens to low hundreds of nM (context-dependent).
- Span: 1–1000 nM.
- Refs: Recher et al., Blood 2005; Tamburini et al., Blood 2008.

16) Fostamatinib (SYK inhibitor; ITP)
- Rationale: SYK contributes to AML survival; SYK inhibition impairs AML growth.
- Reported IC50: ~0.5–3 µM.
- Span: 0.1–10 µM.
- Refs: Puissant et al., Blood 2014; Hahn et al., Leukemia 2009.

17) Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2 inhibitor; myelofibrosis/ET)
- Rationale: Dampens JAK/STAT signaling; can blunt STAT5 downstream of FLT3-ITD and shows activity/combination benefit in AML models.
- Reported IC50: ~0.1–5 µM (variable).
- Span: 0.01–10 µM.
- Refs: Eghtedar et al., Blood 2012; Pemmaraju et al., Leuk Res 2015.

18) Trametinib (MEK1/2 inhibitor; melanoma/NF1)
- Rationale: MAPK pathway targeting; activity in RAS/MAPK-driven AML and in combinations with FLT3 inhibitors.
- Reported IC50: ~0.05–1 µM in sensitive AML; often higher in FLT3-ITD monotherapy.
- Span: 0.01–10 µM.
- Refs: Gallipoli et al., Blood 2015; McCubrey et al., Oncotarget 2012.

19) Disulfiram (ALDH inhibitor; alcohol aversion), activity enhanced by bioavailable copper
- Rationale: Induces proteotoxic/ROS stress; AML cytotoxicity reported, especially with copper present in vitro.
- Reported IC50: sub-µM to low-µM in AML lines (with copper).
- Span: 0.01–5 µM (note: literature often includes copper, which can markedly shift potency).
- Refs: Yip et al., Leuk Res 2011; Skrott et al., Nature 2017.

20) Simvastatin (HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor; dyslipidemia)
- Rationale: Mevalonate pathway inhibition triggers AML apoptosis; preclinical synergy with conventional agents.
- Reported IC50: ~2–10 µM in AML lines.
- Span: 0.5–50 µM.
- Refs: Chapman et al., Br J Haematol 2010; Clendening & Penn, Nat Rev Cancer 2012.

Additional notes
- MOLM13 is FLT3-ITD; FLT3-active TKIs (sorafenib, ponatinib, sunitinib) and agents that impair FLT3 signaling output (pimozide/STAT5, proteasome inhibitors, selinexor) are often particularly potent.
- For solubility and stability, consult each drug’s chemical profile and published AML assay conditions; observed IC50s can shift with exposure time and serum binding.
- Consider counter-screens in non-FLT3-ITD AML lines (e.g., OCI-AML3) to benchmark selectivity for MOLM13.
