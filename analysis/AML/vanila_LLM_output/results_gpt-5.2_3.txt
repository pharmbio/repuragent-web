## Practical IC₅₀ assay setup (so these ranges are “experimentally testable”)
- **Cell line:** MOLM-13 (common seeding: **2–10k cells/well** in 96-well; optimize to stay in log growth over assay).  
- **Readout:** CellTiter-Glo or resazurin after **72 h** (48–96 h depending on mechanism).  
- **Dilution series:** 10–12 points, **1:3** or **1:2** serial dilutions.  
- **Controls:**  
  - DMSO vehicle control (constant % across wells; typically **0.1%**).  
  - A known MOLM-13 active comparator if available in your lab (e.g., a FLT3 inhibitor) to validate assay performance.  
- **Data:** Fit 4-parameter logistic curve; report IC₅₀ + Hill slope + top/bottom constraints; exclude precipitating concentrations.

## Notes to avoid common false negatives/positives
- **Solubility/aggregation:** niclosamide, benzimidazoles, celecoxib can precipitate—cap top concentration to what stays clear in your medium.  
- **Serum binding:** many hydrophobic drugs shift potency with FBS%; keep serum constant.  
- **Copper dependence:** disulfiram often looks weak unless copper is present—test a small copper matrix.  
- **mM drugs (metformin/VPA):** use sterile aqueous stocks; confirm osmolarity and pH effects with matched controls.
