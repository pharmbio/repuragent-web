=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a curated list of 20 repurposable, already‑approved (or late‑stage) drugs with reported anti‑AML activity, with emphasis on MOLM‑13 or closely related FLT3‑ITD AML lines when available. For each, I suggest an experimentally testable IC₅₀ assay concentration range (a screening range, not a guaranteed IC₅₀), suitable for 72‑h viability/proliferation assays (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, resazurin).

**Key notes about the ranges**
- Where literature IC₅₀s in AML/MOLM‑13 exist, I center the range around those values and span at least 2–3 logs.
- For most small molecules, a 10‑point half‑log or log₁₀ dilution within the listed range is practical.
- All concentrations are **nominal** (added to media). Always confirm solubility and cytotoxicity in your own conditions.

---

## 1. Metformin
- **Primary use**: Type 2 diabetes (biguanide; mitochondrial complex I inhibitor).
- **Rationale**: Inhibits oxidative phosphorylation; AML cells (including FLT3‑mut) show metabolic vulnerabilities to OXPHOS inhibition.
- **Suggested screening range (MOLM‑13)**:  
 1 µM – 10 mM (e.g., 1, 10, 50, 100, 500, 1,000, 2,000, 5,000, 10,000 µM).  
 In many leukemia lines, growth‑inhibitory effects often appear ≥0.5–1 mM.

---

## 2. Niclosamide
- **Primary use**: Anthelmintic.
- **Rationale**: Disrupts mitochondrial function, Wnt/β‑catenin, STAT3; reported anti‑leukemic activity.
- **Suggested range**:  
 10 nM – 20 µM.  
 IC₅₀ in various leukemia models is often in the low micromolar range.

---

## 3. Disulfiram (+/− Copper)
- **Primary use**: Alcohol aversion therapy (ALDH inhibitor).
- **Rationale**: ALDH inhibition and proteasome/ROS stress; AML stem/progenitor cells enriched in ALDH; activity enhanced by Cu²⁺.
- **Suggested range** (no copper or with 1–2 µM CuSO₄):  
 1 nM – 5 µM.  
 Many blood cancer cell lines show IC₅₀ ≈ 50–500 nM when copper is present.

---

## 4. Valproic Acid (Valproate)
- **Primary use**: Antiepileptic, mood stabilizer; weak HDAC inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Epigenetic modulation, differentiation induction in AML, often combined with ATRA or hypomethylating agents.
- **Suggested range**:  
 50 µM – 5 mM.  
 AML cell lines typically show chromatin/viability effects in the 0.3–2 mM range.

---

## 5. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Primary use**: HDAC inhibitor approved for CTCL.
- **Rationale**: Potent HDAC inhibitor; strong in vitro activity in AML lines including FLT3‑mut.
- **Suggested range**:  
 10 nM – 10 µM.  
 AML IC₅₀s often 0.2–2 µM; MOLM‑13 commonly responds in the sub‑ to low‑micromolar range.

---

## 6. Panobinostat
- **Primary use**: HDAC inhibitor (multiple myeloma).
- **Rationale**: Broad HDAC inhibition; synergy with FLT3 inhibitors in FLT3‑ITD AML.
- **Suggested range**:  
 1 nM – 1 µM.  
 IC₅₀ for many AML lines: ~5–50 nM; expect potent activity in MOLM‑13.

---

## 7. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide (representative IMiD)
- **Primary use**: Multiple myeloma, MDS with del(5q).
- **Rationale**: Cereblon‑mediated degradation of transcription factors; immunomodulation; some activity in AML subtypes, though less consistent.
- **Suggested range** (Lenalidomide as example):  
 10 nM – 50 µM.  
 Direct cytotoxic IC₅₀ is often >1–5 µM in AML, but may show differentiation or combinatorial effects.

---

## 8. Mebendazole
- **Primary use**: Anthelmintic (tubulin inhibitor).
- **Rationale**: Microtubule disruption; reported anti‑leukemia activity.
- **Suggested range**:  
 10 nM – 20 µM.  
 AML cell line IC₅₀s frequently 0.1–5 µM.

---

## 9. Fluvoxamine (as representative SSRI)
- **Primary use**: Antidepressant (SSRI).
- **Rationale**: Several SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline, fluvoxamine) show cytotoxicity in leukemia cell lines via mitochondrial and ER stress.
- **Suggested range**:  
 0.5 µM – 100 µM.  
 Leukemia IC₅₀ reported commonly in ~5–30 µM range.

---

## 10. Sertraline
- **Primary use**: Antidepressant (SSRI).
- **Rationale**: Direct pro‑apoptotic effects in AML lines; reported synergy with BCL‑2 inhibition.
- **Suggested range**:  
 0.5 µM – 100 µM.  
 AML IC₅₀ usually ~5–20 µM.

---

## 11. Clofazimine
- **Primary use**: Leprosy.
- **Rationale**: Redox‑cycling, ROS generation, mitochondrial disruption; published anti‑AML activity (including FLT3‑ITD).
- **Suggested range**:  
 10 nM – 10 µM.  
 AML IC₅₀ typically 0.1–3 µM.

---

## 12. Doxycycline
- **Primary use**: Antibiotic (tetracycline class).
- **Rationale**: Inhibits mitochondrial protein synthesis; some reports of AML cell growth inhibition via mitochondrial stress.
- **Suggested range**:  
 1 µM – 200 µM.  
 Effects on proliferation often in the 10–100 µM range.

---

## 13. Itraconazole
- **Primary use**: Antifungal.
- **Rationale**: Hedgehog pathway and angiogenesis inhibition; cholesterol trafficking; some preclinical leukemia data.
- **Suggested range**:  
 10 nM – 20 µM.  
 Many cancer cell IC₅₀s in 0.5–5 µM range.

---

## 14. Terfenadine (or other repurposed antihistamine, e.g., astemizole)
- **Primary use**: Antihistamine (withdrawn for QT issues but useful in vitro).
- **Rationale**: hERG/K⁺ channel and off‑target effects; reported apoptosis in leukemic lines.
- **Suggested range**:  
 0.1 µM – 50 µM.  
 IC₅₀ for leukemia lines often ~1–10 µM.

---

## 15. Simvastatin (representative statin)
- **Primary use**: Hyperlipidemia.
- **Rationale**: Mevalonate pathway inhibition; AML lines can be sensitive to statin‑induced apoptosis.
- **Suggested range**:  
 0.1 µM – 50 µM.  
 AML IC₅₀ commonly 1–10 µM with adequate exposure.

---

## 16. Atorvastatin
- **Primary use**: Hyperlipidemia.
- **Rationale**: Similar to simvastatin; some studies suggest potent anti‑leukemic effects at micromolar concentrations.
- **Suggested range**:  
 0.1 µM – 50 µM.  
 Test overlapping doses with simvastatin for comparison.

---

## 17. Chloroquine / Hydroxychloroquine (autophagy inhibitor)
- **Primary use**: Antimalarial; autoimmune diseases.
- **Rationale**: Autophagy inhibition; can sensitize AML cells to cytotoxic and targeted agents; some direct growth inhibition.
- **Suggested range** (Hydroxychloroquine):  
 1 µM – 100 µM.  
 Direct IC₅₀ for AML lines often ~20–60 µM.

---

## 18. Propranolol
- **Primary use**: Non‑selective β‑blocker.
- **Rationale**: Anti‑angiogenic and direct anti‑tumor effects; some data in hematologic malignancies and leukemia models.
- **Suggested range**:  
 0.5 µM – 100 µM.  
 Leukemic cells often respond in 5–50 µM range.

---

## 19. Imatinib (as a control / repurposed kinase inhibitor)
- **Primary use**: CML, Ph+ ALL, GIST.
- **Rationale**: Not effective for FLT3‑ITD, but good benchmark for assay and for AML with rare BCR‑ABL or KIT mutations; also acts on some off‑targets.
- **Suggested range**:  
 10 nM – 20 µM.  
 AML line IC₅₀s vary widely; many non‑BCR‑ABL AML cells are relatively resistant (>5–10 µM).

---

## 20. Nilotinib / Dasatinib (second‑generation ABL/Src inhibitors)
- **Primary use**: CML.
- **Rationale**: Inhibit multiple kinases (Src family, KIT, etc.); have shown some activity in subsets of AML and as combination partners.
- **Suggested range** (Nilotinib as example):  
 1 nM – 10 µM.  
 Sensitive cells often have IC₅₀s in 10–200 nM range; MOLM‑13 may be less sensitive, but still useful as a kinase‑inhibitor control.

---

### Practical notes for MOLM‑13 assays

1. **Assay setup**
   - Seed MOLM‑13 at ~5–10 × 10³ cells/well in 96‑well plates (RPMI‑1640 + 10–20% FBS + appropriate supplements).
   - Incubate 72 h with compounds in 0.1% DMSO (or vehicle‑matched), then measure viability.

2. **Concentration design**
   - Use a 10‑point log or half‑log dilution within each suggested range.
   - Include at least:  
     - vehicle control,  
     - positive cytotoxic control (e.g., cytarabine 1–10 µM, or a known FLT3 inhibitor like quizartinib if available),  
     - media‑only background.

3. **Secondary readouts**
   - For hits (IC₅₀ ≤ ~5–10 µM), follow up with annexin V/PI, cell‑cycle analysis, and possibly FLT3 signaling or mitochondrial assays where mechanistically relevant.
