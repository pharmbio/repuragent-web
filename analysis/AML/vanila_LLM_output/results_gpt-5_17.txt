=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 approved, “existing” drugs with literature support for anti-AML activity and strong rationale for activity in FLT3-ITD–positive lines like MOLM13. For each, I suggest a practical screening range for a 10-point, 3-fold dilution IC50 assay in MOLM13. Adjust ranges as needed based on pilot results and solubility; DMSO commonly ≤0.1% v/v.

FLT3/multikinase inhibitors (particularly relevant to MOLM13, which is FLT3-ITD+)
- Sorafenib (HCC/RCC; FLT3, RAF, VEGFR): 0.5 nM to 5 μM
- Ponatinib (CML/ALL; ABL, FLT3, KIT): 0.03 nM to 1 μM
- Sunitinib (RCC/GIST; FLT3, KIT, VEGFR): 1 nM to 10 μM
- Regorafenib (CRC/GIST/HCC; FLT3, VEGFR, TIE2): 1 nM to 10 μM
- Cabozantinib (RCC/HCC; MET, AXL, FLT3): 0.5 nM to 5 μM
- Axitinib (RCC; primarily VEGFR, reported FLT3 activity): 0.5 nM to 5 μM
- Dasatinib (CML/ALL; SRC/ABL/KIT; some AML activity): 1 nM to 5 μM

Apoptosis/BCL2 pathway
- Venetoclax (CLL/SLL, repurposed to AML; BCL2): 0.1 nM to 1 μM

Proteostasis/protein synthesis
- Bortezomib (MM/MCL; proteasome): 0.5 nM to 200 nM
- Carfilzomib (MM; proteasome): 0.5 nM to 200 nM
- Omacetaxine (CML; protein synthesis inhibitor): 1 nM to 500 nM

Epigenetic modulators
- Panobinostat (MM; HDAC): 0.5 nM to 200 nM
- Vorinostat (CTCL; HDAC): 50 nM to 15 μM

Nuclear export
- Selinexor (MM, DLBCL; XPO1): 5 nM to 2 μM

MAPK pathway
- Trametinib (melanoma; MEK1/2): 0.1 nM to 1 μM
- Selumetinib (NF1 PN; MEK1/2): 1 nM to 3 μM

mTOR pathway
- Everolimus (various solid tumors; mTORC1): 1 nM to 10 μM

Metabolism/redox and other repurposed agents
- Leflunomide (RA; via teriflunomide, DHODH): 1 μM to 200 μM
- Auranofin (RA; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor): 10 nM to 5 μM
- Niclosamide (anthelmintic; Wnt/mitochondrial/STAT3 disruptor): 10 nM to 5 μM

Notes
- These ranges are chosen to bracket reported AML potencies (including MOLM13/MV4-11 where available) and accommodate variability from serum, media, and assay format. If unsure, use the upper bound as the top dose and perform 10-point 3-fold serial dilutions downward.
- For highly potent agents (ponatinib, bortezomib, panobinostat, trametinib, omacetaxine), include sub-nanomolar to low-nanomolar points to avoid top-plateau truncation.
- Confirm compound solubility and cell-line DMSO tolerance; many stocks are in DMSO.
- As a positive control for FLT3-ITD dependency in MOLM13, a potent FLT3 inhibitor (e.g., ponatinib or sorafenib) is useful.
