=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used (or late‑stage) drugs with evidence or strong rationale for repurposing in AML, especially FLT3‑ITD–positive lines like MOLM‑13. For each, I list a reasonable **IC₅₀ assay concentration range** to test in vitro. These ranges are based on published in vitro IC₅₀ values (where available) or on usual screening practice (e.g., covering 0.01–50 μM on a log scale). Final optimization should be done empirically.

**General assay note**

- Use a broad **10‑point, half‑log dilution** series (e.g., 0.01–30 or 0.03–100 μM), then refine around the active window.
- For potent kinase/epigenetic inhibitors, start in the **low‑nM to low‑μM** range.
- For metabolic or CNS drugs with weaker reported antiproliferative effects, allow up to **30–100 μM** (watch DMSO <0.1–0.2%).

---

## 1. Sorafenib  
- **Primary use:** Multi‑kinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR); approved for HCC, RCC, thyroid cancer.  
- **Rationale in AML:** Known off‑label activity against **FLT3‑ITD AML**; multiple reports of activity in MOLM‑13.  
- **Suggested test range:** **1 nM – 10 μM** (e.g., 0.001, 0.003, 0.01, 0.03, 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10 μM).  
- Expect IC₅₀ on MOLM‑13 typically in **low‑nM to sub‑μM** range.

## 2. Sunitinib  
- **Use:** Multi‑target RTK inhibitor (VEGFR, PDGFR, KIT, FLT3).  
- **Rationale:** Inhibits **FLT3**; reported antiproliferative effects in FLT3‑ITD AML cell lines.  
- **Range:** **1 nM – 10 μM.**

## 3. Midostaurin (PKC412)  
- **Use:** FLT3 inhibitor; approved in combination therapy for FLT3‑mutated AML.  
- **Rationale:** Directly relevant; strong activity in MOLM‑13.  
- **Range:** **0.1 nM – 3 μM** (mid‑nM IC₅₀ expected).

## 4. Dasatinib  
- **Use:** BCR‑ABL/Src family TKI (CML, Ph+ ALL).  
- **Rationale:** Inhibits multiple kinases; documented cytotoxicity in some AML lines, including FLT3‑mutant contexts and LSC‑like populations.  
- **Range:** **0.1 nM – 3 μM.**

## 5. Nilotinib  
- **Use:** BCR‑ABL TKI (CML).  
- **Rationale:** Shows activity in some AML subsets; may affect KIT/PDGFR‑dependent signaling.  
- **Range:** **1 nM – 10 μM.**

## 6. Imatinib  
- **Use:** BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR inhibitor (CML, GIST).  
- **Rationale:** Limited broadly in AML, but can be active in **KIT‑dependent** AML subsets; useful as a comparator.  
- **Range:** **0.1 – 30 μM** (activity usually in the higher μM in non‑BCR‑ABL cells).

## 7. Pazopanib  
- **Use:** Multi‑TKI (VEGFR, PDGFR, c‑KIT).  
- **Rationale:** Some data in leukemias; antiangiogenic and direct tumor cell effects.  
- **Range:** **0.03 – 30 μM.**

## 8. Ponatinib  
- **Use:** Multi‑kinase TKI (BCR‑ABL, FLT3, KIT, FGFR, VEGFR); approved for resistant CML/Ph+ ALL.  
- **Rationale:** Strong **FLT3 inhibition**; reported potent activity in FLT3‑ITD AML cell lines including MOLM‑13.  
- **Range:** **0.1 nM – 1 μM** (expect low‑nM IC₅₀).

## 9. Vandetanib  
- **Use:** RET, VEGFR2, EGFR inhibitor (medullary thyroid carcinoma).  
- **Rationale:** Multi‑targeted TKI with some hematologic preclinical data.  
- **Range:** **0.03 – 30 μM.**

## 10. Belinostat  
- **Use:** HDAC inhibitor (peripheral T‑cell lymphoma).  
- **Rationale:** Epigenetic modulation; HDAC inhibitors reduce AML proliferation, alter differentiation, including in MOLM‑13.  
- **Range:** **0.01 – 10 μM** (IC₅₀ often ~0.1–1 μM).

## 11. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- **Use:** Pan‑HDAC inhibitor (CTCL).  
- **Rationale:** Extensively studied in AML cell lines; induces apoptosis and differentiation.  
- **Range:** **0.1 – 20 μM** (typical IC₅₀ around 1–5 μM in AML lines).

## 12. Panobinostat  
- **Use:** HDAC inhibitor (multiple myeloma).  
- **Rationale:** Potent epigenetic agent with activity in AML preclinically.  
- **Range:** **0.001 – 1 μM** (very potent; IC₅₀ often low‑nM).

## 13. Decitabine  
- **Use:** DNA hypomethylating agent; standard in MDS/AML.  
- **Rationale:** Epigenetic reprogramming, antiproliferative at clinically relevant exposures.  
- **Range:** **0.03 – 10 μM** (consider 24–72 h exposure with fresh medium ± drug).

## 14. Azacitidine  
- **Use:** Hypomethylating agent (MDS, AML).  
- **Rationale:** Similar to decitabine; affects DNA and RNA, killing proliferating blasts.  
- **Range:** **0.1 – 30 μM** (longer exposure often required).

## 15. Venetoclax  
- **Use:** BCL‑2 inhibitor (CLL, AML in combination regimens).  
- **Rationale:** Established activity in AML; MOLM‑13 is generally BCL‑2–dependent, though sensitivity can vary.  
- **Range:** **0.001 – 3 μM** (nM potency; use low DMSO).

## 16. Chloroquine or Hydroxychloroquine  
- **Use:** Antimalarial/antirheumatic; autophagy inhibitor.  
- **Rationale:** Autophagy inhibition can sensitize AML cells, including FLT3‑mutant lines, to stress and TKIs.  
- **Range:** **1 – 50 μM** (higher μM often needed; monitor off‑target toxicity).

## 17. Metformin  
- **Use:** Type 2 diabetes; AMPK activator, mitochondrial complex I inhibitor.  
- **Rationale:** Reported antiproliferative and pro‑apoptotic effects in AML via metabolic stress.  
- **Range:** **0.1 – 30 mM** (in vitro activity is typically in the mM range; consider physiologic relevance).

## 18. Simvastatin (or Atorvastatin)  
- **Use:** HMG‑CoA reductase inhibitors (hyperlipidemia).  
- **Rationale:** Mevalonate‑pathway blockade impairs prenylation; reported to induce apoptosis in AML cell lines.  
- **Range:** **0.1 – 30 μM** (statins often show effects in 1–20 μM).

## 19. Disulfiram (± Copper)  
- **Use:** Alcohol aversion; ALDH inhibitor, copper ionophore.  
- **Rationale:** Targets ALDH‑high leukemic cells, proteasome/ROS pathways; preclinical AML data exist. Copper (CuCl₂ ~0.5–1 μM) often enhances effect.  
- **Range:** **0.01 – 10 μM** disulfiram ± fixed low μM copper.

## 20. Thioridazine  
- **Use:** Antipsychotic (phenothiazine).  
- **Rationale:** Reported selective toxicity to leukemia stem‑like cells via dopamine receptor and mitochondrial effects.  
- **Range:** **0.1 – 20 μM** (above ~10–20 μM risk of nonspecific cytotoxicity).

### Practical concentration‑setting strategy (for a 72‑h proliferation/viability assay)

For each drug:

1. **Design a 10‑point dilution series** around the ranges above (half‑log or log₁₀ steps).  
2. Include:
   - Vehicle control (DMSO at highest % used).
   - Positive control (e.g., a known FLT3 inhibitor like sorafenib or midostaurin).  
3. Use **MOLM‑13** and optionally a second AML line with different genetics (e.g., OCI‑AML3) to gauge selectivity.  
4. Readout: ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo), resazurin, or similar, at 48–72 h; fit a 4‑parameter logistic curve to derive IC₅₀.
