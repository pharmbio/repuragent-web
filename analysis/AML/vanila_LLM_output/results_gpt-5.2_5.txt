- If you want “repurposing” *outside* standard AML agents, consider expanding with **statins (simvastatin), disulfiram(+Cu), ivermectin, niclosamide, mebendazole, thioridazine**, etc.—but their potency and reproducibility can vary and often require higher µM ranges and careful controls.

## Suggested plate design (simple and works with the ranges)
For each drug, run **10 concentrations** in **3-fold** steps centered to cover the stated window; e.g.:
- For potent kinase inhibitors: **0.03, 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1 µM**
- For moderate potency drugs: **3 nM → 30 µM**
- For weak drugs (e.g., valproate/chloroquine): **0.1 mM → 10 mM** or **0.3 µM → 100 µM**

Include:
- **Positive control**: gilteritinib (for MOLM13) and/or bortezomib
- **Vehicle control**: DMSO matched
- **Replicates**: ≥3 technical replicates
