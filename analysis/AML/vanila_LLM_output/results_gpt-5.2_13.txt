2) **Gilteritinib** (approved for relapsed FLT3+ AML)  
   - Rationale: FLT3/AXL inhibitor  
   - MOLM‑13: very high  
   - Typical active range: **low‑nM to nM**

3) **Quizartinib** (approved in some regions; clinical elsewhere)  
   - Rationale: potent FLT3 inhibitor  
   - MOLM‑13: very high  
   - Typical active range: **low‑nM to nM**

4) **Sorafenib** (approved in RCC/HCC; used off‑label in FLT3‑ITD AML)  
   - Rationale: FLT3/RAF/VEGFR multi‑kinase  
   - MOLM‑13: high

5) **Dasatinib** (approved CML/ALL)

6) **Trametinib** (approved melanoma/solid tumors)

## How to turn this into an IC50 plan (non-prescriptive)
- Use a **log-spaced dilution series** spanning the expected potency class:
  - **Kinase inhibitors / HDAC inhibitors / proteasome inhibitors**: usually **low‑nM → sub‑µM** window.
  - **Epigenetic nucleosides (aza/decitabine)**: often need **longer exposure** and can appear weaker in short assays.
  - **Differentiation/metabolic repurposing agents (ATRA, disulfiram)**: often **sub‑µM → µM** and sensitive to media/serum/copper, etc.
- Confirm **MOLM‑13 genotype/phenotype** (FLT3‑ITD) and include a comparator AML line (e.g., **THP‑1**, **OCI‑AML3**, **MV4‑11**) to interpret specificity.
- Pull exact reported IC50s from: **DepMap PRISM**, **GDSC**, **CTRP**, and PubMed (“MOLM‑13 IC50 [drug]”).
