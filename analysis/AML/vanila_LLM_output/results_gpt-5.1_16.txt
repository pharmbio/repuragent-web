=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a **shortlist of 20 existing (mostly FDA‑approved or late‑stage) drugs** that have evidence or strong rationale for repurposing in AML, with particular relevance to FLT3‑ITD–positive lines like **MOLM‑13**. For each, I give:

- **Mechanism / rationale**
- **Suggested IC50 assay concentration range** (e.g., 10‑point, half‑log or log spacing)
- Notes specific to **MOLM‑13 / AML** where available

IC50 ranges are *in vitro* starting suggestions based on published potencies in leukemia cells; you would typically run at least a 72‑h viability assay (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, Alamar Blue) with 6–10 concentrations in triplicate.

---

## 1. Sorafenib  
- **Class / target**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR).  
- **Rationale**: Potent against FLT3‑ITD AML; MOLM‑13 is FLT3‑ITD+.  
- **Typical IC50 in FLT3‑ITD AML**: ~1–10 nM in biochemical assays, ~10–100 nM in cells.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.5 nM – 5 µM (e.g., 0.5, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000, 3000, 5000 nM).  

---

## 2. Midostaurin (PKC412)  
- **Class / target**: Multikinase inhibitor; approved for FLT3‑mut AML.  
- **Rationale**: Direct clinical use in AML; strong MOLM‑13 activity reported.  
- **Cellular IC50**: Often in the 10–100 nM range in FLT3‑ITD AML.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 1 nM – 10 µM.

---

## 3. Quizartinib (AC220)  
- **Class / target**: FLT3 inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Highly potent against FLT3‑ITD cell lines including MOLM‑13.  
- **Cellular IC50**: Low nanomolar range (1–10 nM).  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.1 nM – 1 µM.

---

## 4. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)  
- **Class / target**: FLT3/AXL inhibitor; approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mut AML.  
- **Rationale**: Direct clinical AML agent; strong in MOLM‑13.  
- **Cellular IC50**: ~1–30 nM reported in FLT3‑ITD AML.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.1 nM – 3 µM.

---

## 5. Dasatinib  
- **Class / target**: Src/Abl family kinase inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Broad TK inhibitor; can affect signaling nodes (SRC, LYN) used by AML cells; some activity in FLT3‑mut contexts.  
- **Cellular IC50 in leukemias**: Often 1–100 nM.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.5 nM – 5 µM.

---

## 6. Sunitinib  
- **Class / target**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, VEGFR, PDGFR).  
- **Rationale**: Reported activity in AML including FLT3‑mut lines.  
- **Cellular IC50**: ~10–500 nM in various leukemia lines.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 1 nM – 10 µM.

---

## 7. Nilotinib  
- **Class / target**: BCR‑ABL, c‑KIT, PDGFR inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Targets kinases relevant in some AML subsets; documented anti‑proliferative effects in AML cell lines.  
- **Cellular IC50**: ~100 nM – low µM in non‑BCR‑ABL cells.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 10 nM – 20 µM.

---

## 8. Imatinib  
- **Class / target**: BCR‑ABL, PDGFR, c‑KIT inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Repurposing especially in c‑KIT or PDGFR‑driven AML subsets; baseline for comparison.  
- **Cellular IC50 (non‑BCR‑ABL)**: Often low‑µM.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 100 nM – 50 µM.

---

## 9. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)  
- **Class / target**: BCL‑2 inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Now standard in AML combinations; strong activity in many AML lines including MOLM‑13.  
- **Cellular IC50**: Frequently ~1–100 nM in BCL‑2–dependent hematologic cells.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.5 nM – 5 µM.

---

## 10. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)  
- **Class / target**: BCL‑2/BCL‑xL inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Strong preclinical AML data; can sensitize MOLM‑13 to various agents.  
- **Cellular IC50**: ~10–200 nM in many hematologic malignancies.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 1 nM – 10 µM.

---

## 11. Ruxolitinib  
- **Class / target**: JAK1/2 inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: AML cells with activated JAK/STAT signaling; shown to impair proliferation in subsets and in FLT3‑mut contexts via downstream signaling.  
- **Cellular IC50**: Typically ~0.1–3 µM.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 10 nM – 30 µM.

---

## 12. Tofacitinib  
- **Class / target**: JAK1/3 inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: JAK/STAT pathway relevance in AML microenvironmental signaling; repurposing from RA.  
- **Cellular IC50**: Usually 0.1–5 µM.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 10 nM – 50 µM.

---

## 13. Everolimus (RAD001)  
- **Class / target**: mTORC1 inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: PI3K/AKT/mTOR signaling is hyperactive in AML including MOLM‑13; synergy shown with FLT3 or BCL‑2 inhibitors.  
- **Cellular IC50**: Often ~1–100 nM.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.5 nM – 5 µM.

---

## 14. Temsirolimus  
- **Class / target**: mTOR inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Similar to everolimus; active in AML models and used in combination studies with TKIs.  
- **Cellular IC50**: ~1–100 nM in leukemia lines.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.5 nM – 5 µM.

---

## 15. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- **Class / target**: HDAC inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Epigenetic therapy; extensive AML cell line data including synergy with FLT3/BCL‑2 inhibitors.  
- **Cellular IC50**: Usually ~0.5–5 µM.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 50 nM – 50 µM.

---

## 16. Panobinostat (LBH589)  
- **Class / target**: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Very potent epigenetic agent; reported to inhibit proliferation and induce apoptosis in FLT3‑ITD AML lines.  
- **Cellular IC50**: low‑nanomolar to tens of nM.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.5 nM – 1 µM.

---

## 17. Decitabine  
- **Class / target**: DNA methyltransferase inhibitor (hypomethylating agent).  
- **Rationale**: Already used in AML; can be benchmark or combination partner.  
- **Cellular effective range**: Typically low‑µM with longer exposures (48–96 h).  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 100 nM – 20 µM (extended exposure recommended).

---

## 18. Azacitidine  
- **Class / target**: DNMT inhibitor (HMA).  
- **Rationale**: Standard AML agent; epigenetic modulation in MOLM‑13 and other lines.  
- **Cellular effective range**: ~0.5–10 µM with prolonged exposure.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 100 nM – 20 µM (72–96 h).

---

## 19. Metformin  
- **Class / target**: AMPK activator; indirect mTOR inhibition.  
- **Rationale**: Broad anticancer repurposing candidate; AML cells can be sensitive in high‑µM to low‑mM range; investigate metabolic vulnerability.  
- **Cellular IC50**: Often 1–10 mM in cancer cell lines.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 100 µM – 30 mM (pay attention to osmolarity and medium pH).

---

## 20. Disulfiram (± copper supplementation)  
- **Class / target**: ALDH inhibitor; induces oxidative and proteotoxic stress (especially with Cu²⁺).  
- **Rationale**: Targeting ALDH‑positive leukemic stem/progenitor cells; some AML line data show µM‑range cytotoxicity.  
- **Cellular IC50**: ~0.2–5 µM (often tested at 0.5–10 µM) + low‑µM Cu²⁺.  
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - Disulfiram: 50 nM – 10 µM  
  - CuCl₂ or CuSO₄: 0.5–5 µM (constant across curve).

---

### General experimental notes for MOLM‑13 IC50 assays

- **Exposure time**: 72 h is typical for proliferation/viability IC50, especially for signaling and apoptosis‑targeted agents.
- **Seeding density**: ~5×10³–1×10⁴ cells per well (96‑well) in 100 µL RPMI + 10% FBS (optimize so control wells remain in log phase).
- **Readout**: ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or resazurin; include DMSO (% matched) controls.
- **Curve fitting**: Use 4‑parameter logistic model to derive IC50; repeat at least twice independently.
