=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.

Results:

Below are 20 existing drugs (approved or in late-stage development) with rationale for testing in AML and experimentally actionable suggestions for IC50 experiments in MOLM13 (an FLT3‑ITD AML cell line). For each drug I give a short rationale and a practical concentration plan (recommended top concentration and a suggested dilution scheme). The dilution scheme I recommend by default is a 10‑point, 3‑fold serial dilution from the listed top concentration (this covers ~4 logs and is appropriate for determining IC50s for most compounds). Adjust the top concentration downward if you observe solubility or cytotoxicity issues.

General assay notes (keeps assays comparable):
- Use a vehicle (DMSO) control; keep final DMSO ≤0.1% (ideally ≤0.05%) across all wells.
- Use biological replicates (≥3) and at least technical triplicates per concentration.
- Typical exposure time for proliferation assays: 48–72 h (72 h is common for small-molecule IC50s in AML); some drugs (e.g., nucleoside analogs, hypomethylating agents) may require different exposure schemes—see notes below.
- Readout: ATP (CellTiter‑Glo) or other viability assays validated for MOLM13.
- Check compound solubility and stability in assay medium. For agents that need cofactors (e.g., disulfiram + Cu), add the cofactor as indicated.

- Test plan: top 1 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold serial dilution (1 µM → ~1.7 pM).

Additional assay considerations:
- MOLM13 specifics: MOLM13 is FLT3‑ITD positive and often sensitive to FLT3 and BCL‑2 inhibitors; consider testing combinations (e.g., venetoclax + FLT3 inhibitor) after single‑agent profiling.
- For nucleoside analogs/hypomethylating agents (e.g., azacitidine, decitabine), standard short‑term proliferation assays are less informative—use different treatment schedules (repeated dosing or longer exposure) and appropriate concentrations.
- For compounds requiring co‑factors (e.g., disulfiram + copper), maintain cofactor across all wells including controls.
- If a compound precipitates at the top concentration, lower the top concentration and re‑run the dilution series.
- Always confirm reversible vs irreversible binding and whether readout time needs adjustment (some targeted agents show rapid cell death, others require longer incubations to manifest effects).
