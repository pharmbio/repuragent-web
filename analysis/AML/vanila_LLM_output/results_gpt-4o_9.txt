=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Repurposing existing drugs for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment, especially targeting cell lines like MOLM13, involves identifying compounds with potential anti-proliferative properties. 3. **Arsenic Trioxide** - A chemotherapy agent used for acute promyelocytic leukemia with potential in AML.

4. **Ibrutinib** - A BTK inhibitor used in B-cell malignancies, with some evidence of anti-AML activity.

5. **Ixazomib** - A proteasome inhibitor used in multiple myeloma, potentially effective against AML.

6. **Decitabine** - A demethylating agent already used for certain types of AML.

7. **Dasatinib** - Originally a BCR-ABL inhibitor in CML, with potential broader kinase inhibition in AML.

8. **Tamoxifen** - An estrogen receptor modulator shown to inhibit growth in various cancer cell lines.

9. **Metformin** - Commonly used for diabetes, with studies indicating anti-cancer properties.

10. **Ruxolitinib** - A JAK inhibitor used in myeloproliferative neoplasms, investigated for AML.

11. **Simvastatin** - A statin with potential anti-leukemic effects through cholesterol pathway inhibition.

12. **Chloroquine** - An anti-malarial that has shown ability to enhance chemotherapy actions.

13. **Carfilzomib** - A proteasome inhibitor with effects on protein degradation pathways in AML.

14. **Rapamycin (Sirolimus)** - An mTOR inhibitor with studies showing anti-leukemic potential.

15. **Bortezomib** - Another proteasome inhibitor used in multiple myeloma, with AML potential.

16. **Itraconazole** - An antifungal with possible Hedgehog signaling pathway inhibition.

17. **Mebendazole** - An anti-helminthic drug with documented off-target anti-cancer effects.

18. **Sulfasalazine** - Used for inflammatory diseases, with glutamate antiporter (xCT) inhibition.

19. **Disulfiram** - Used for alcohol dependency, interesting for its ALDH inhibition effect.

20. **Celecoxib** - A COX-2 inhibitor that has shown some anti-proliferative effects in cancer cells.
