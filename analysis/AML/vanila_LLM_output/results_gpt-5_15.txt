=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 approved, “existing” drugs with published activity against AML cell lines (often including FLT3-ITD–dependent lines like MOLM13/MV4-11). For each, I list a practical 10-point, 3-fold serial dilution range to run an IC50 assay in MOLM13. Ranges are centered to bracket literature potencies and typical protein-binding effects in serum. Use DMSO ≤0.2% final unless noted.

FLT3/MAPK and other targeted inhibitors (strong MOLM13 rationale)
- Sorafenib (HCC/RCC; multi-kinase incl. FLT3): 10-point 3-fold from 1 µM down to 0.5 nM. Expect low-nM activity in MOLM13.
- Sunitinib (RCC/GIST; FLT3): 3 µM to 1.5 nM. Expect ~10–100 nM.
- Ponatinib (CML/ALL; FLT3 including TKD mutants): 300 nM to 0.15 nM. Expect single–low nM.
- Cabozantinib (RCC/HCC; FLT3/MET/AXL): 3 µM to 1.5 nM. Expect ~10–100 nM.
- Regorafenib (CRC/GIST/HCC; FLT3/RAF/VEGFR): 10 µM to 5 nM. Expect ~0.1–1 µM in cells.
- Lenvatinib (DTC/HCC/EC; multi-kinase with reported FLT3 activity): 10 µM to 5 nM. Expect ~0.05–0.5 µM.
- Axitinib (RCC; reported FLT3 activity): 10 µM to 5 nM. Expect ~0.3–3 µM.
- Vandetanib (MTC; multi-kinase, reported FLT3 coverage): 30 µM to 15 nM. Expect ~0.3–3 µM.
- Dasatinib (CML/ALL; SRC/SYK; anti-AML signaling): 1 µM to 0.5 nM. Expect ~10–100 nM.
- Ruxolitinib (MF/PV; JAK1/2): 30 µM to 15 nM. Expect ~1–10 µM (context-dependent).
- Everolimus (RCC/NET; mTOR): 3 µM to 1.5 nM. Expect ~10–100 nM anti-proliferative effects.

Proteasome inhibitors (broad AML cytotoxicity)
- Bortezomib (MM/MCL): 100 nM to 0.05 nM. Expect ~5–20 nM.
- Carfilzomib (MM): 300 nM to 0.15 nM. Expect ~10–50 nM.

Metabolism/mitochondria, redox, and other repurposing leads with AML data
- Tigecycline (antibiotic; mitochondrial translation): 60 µM to 30 nM. Expect ~1–10 µM; prepare fresh; light-sensitive.
- Auranofin (RA; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor): 10 µM to 5 nM. Expect ~0.2–2 µM.
- Disulfiram + Cu (AUD; ALDH inhibition/redox; copper-dependent): Disulfiram 5 µM to 2.5 nM with 1–2 µM CuCl2 constant. Expect ~0.05–1 µM in presence of Cu. Note: add copper to reveal potency.
- Mebendazole (anthelmintic; microtubules/HH): 10 µM to 5 nM. Expect ~0.1–1 µM; watch solubility.
- Niclosamide (anthelmintic; Wnt/STAT3/oxidative phosphorylation): 10 µM to 5 nM. Expect ~0.2–2 µM; low aqueous solubility—use fresh DMSO stocks and ≤0.2% DMSO final.
- Thioridazine (antipsychotic; dopamine receptor; leukemia stem cell targeting): 30 µM to 15 nM. Expect ~1–10 µM; QT liability—handle with care.
- Artesunate (antimalarial; ROS/iron-dependent): 30 µM to 15 nM. Expect ~1–10 µM.

Assay setup tips for MOLM13 IC50 determination
- Cells: MOLM13 (FLT3-ITD). Seed 5,000–10,000 cells per well (96-well), 100 µL RPMI + 10% FBS, antibiotics optional.
- Compound handling: 10 mM DMSO stocks; final DMSO ≤0.2%. Include vehicle controls on every plate.
- Incubation: 72 h for viability (many TKIs show effects by 48 h; 72 h improves signal for slower-acting drugs).
- Readout: ATP-based viability (e.g., CellTiter-Glo). Normalize to vehicle.
- Controls: Positive control FLT3 inhibitor (e.g., gilteritinib 0.3–300 nM) to verify assay sensitivity; optional staurosporine for assay ceiling.
- Notes:
  - Protein binding can shift apparent potency upward; the ranges above already account for 10% FBS conditions.
  - For disulfiram, include copper (1–2 µM CuCl2) in all wells for that series to reveal activity.
  - Light/serum sensitivity: prepare tigecycline fresh, protect from light; mebendazole/niclosamide require careful solubilization.
  - Run at least n=3 technical replicates; confirm with ≥2 biological replicates.
