=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 clinically approved drugs with published or strongly suggested activity against AML cells (often including FLT3‑mutant lines like MOLM‑13). For each, I list:

- **Primary relevant mechanism in AML**
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay concentration range** (a 10‑point half‑log or log series within these bounds is typical; adjust for solubility and toxicity)
- **Key AML‑related note** (esp. data in MOLM‑13 or similar FLT3‑ITD lines when available)

Concentration ranges are for **in vitro proliferation/viability assays** (e.g., 72 h CellTiter‑Glo, MTT) in AML cell lines. They are meant to bracket likely IC₅₀ values, not to suggest dosing in patients.

---

## 1. Sorafenib
- **Mechanism**: Multi‑kinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR); potent against FLT3‑ITD AML.
- **Test range**: **1–10,000 nM** (0.001–10 µM); e.g., 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1,000, 3,000, 10,000 nM.
- **Notes**: Robust activity in MOLM‑13 and other FLT3‑ITD lines; often low‑nanomolar IC₅₀ for FLT3 phosphorylation and proliferation.

## 2. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Mechanism**: Multi‑kinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, PKC).
- **Test range**: **1–10,000 nM**.
- **Notes**: Approved for FLT3‑mutated AML; MOLM‑13 is a standard sensitive line.

## 3. Sunitinib
- **Mechanism**: Multi‑target RTK inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, VEGFR, PDGFR).
- **Test range**: **1–20,000 nM** (0.001–20 µM).
- **Notes**: Shows proliferation inhibition in FLT3‑mutant AML cell lines, including MOLM‑13 and MV4‑11.

## 4. Ponatinib
- **Mechanism**: Pan‑BCR‑ABL inhibitor with strong FLT3 inhibition.
- **Test range**: **0.1–10,000 nM** (0.0001–10 µM).
- **Notes**: Very potent against FLT3‑ITD and FLT3‑TKD mutants; MOLM‑13 often shows sub‑100 nM IC₅₀.

## 5. Dasatinib
- **Mechanism**: Src/Abl family inhibitor; also inhibits some KIT and other kinases.
- **Test range**: **1–10,000 nM**.
- **Notes**: Antileukemic effects in several AML lines; more modest vs FLT3 than sorafenib/ponatinib but can hit alternative signaling.

## 6. Quizartinib (AC220)
- **Mechanism**: Highly selective FLT3 inhibitor.
- **Test range**: **0.1–1,000 nM** (0.0001–1 µM).
- **Notes**: Very potent in MOLM‑13 and MV4‑11; IC₅₀ often in low‑nanomolar range.

## 7. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)
- **Mechanism**: FLT3/AXL inhibitor.
- **Test range**: **0.1–10,000 nM**.
- **Notes**: Strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML; MOLM‑13 widely used for preclinical work.

---

## 8. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Mechanism**: Selective BCL‑2 inhibitor; promotes apoptosis.
- **Test range**: **1–10,000 nM**.
- **Notes**: Powerful in many AML lines; often nanomolar IC₅₀s; synergy with hypomethylating agents or cytarabine.

## 9. Ruxolitinib
- **Mechanism**: JAK1/2 inhibitor.
- **Test range**: **10–50,000 nM** (0.01–50 µM).
- **Notes**: Can impair growth in some AML subsets (especially cytokine‑driven/JAK‑activated); sometimes combined with FLT3 or BCL‑2 inhibitors.

## 10. Idelalisib
- **Mechanism**: PI3Kδ inhibitor.
- **Test range**: **10–50,000 nM**.
- **Notes**: PI3K/AKT is active in AML; less selective for AML but can reduce survival and proliferation, often in combination.

## 11. Everolimus (or Temsirolimus)
- **Mechanism**: mTORC1 inhibitor.
- **Test range**:  
  - **Everolimus**: **1–10,000 nM**  
  - **Temsirolimus**: **1–20,000 nM**
- **Notes**: mTOR pathway commonly active in AML; effects include reduced proliferation and increased apoptosis.

## 12. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Mechanism**: HDAC inhibitor (pan‑HDAC).
- **Test range**: **0.1–20,000 nM** (0.0001–20 µM).
- **Notes**: Induces cell‑cycle arrest and apoptosis in multiple AML lines; often micromolar IC₅₀.

## 13. Panobinostat
- **Mechanism**: Potent pan‑HDAC inhibitor.
- **Test range**: **0.1–5,000 nM** (0.0001–5 µM).
- **Notes**: More potent than vorinostat; AML lines frequently respond at low‑micromolar or submicromolar levels.

---

## 14. Decitabine
- **Mechanism**: DNA hypomethylating agent (DNMT inhibitor).
- **Test range**: **10–20,000 nM** (0.01–20 µM).
- **Notes**: Requires incorporation into DNA (longer exposure, 48–96 h). Inhibits AML cell proliferation and can induce differentiation.

## 15. Azacitidine
- **Mechanism**: DNMT inhibitor, also RNA incorporation.
- **Test range**: **50–50,000 nM** (0.05–50 µM).
- **Notes**: Similar considerations to decitabine; often used with venetoclax; antiproliferative in AML lines over longer incubations.

---

## 16. Bortezomib
- **Mechanism**: 26S proteasome inhibitor.
- **Test range**: **1–2,000 nM** (0.001–2 µM).
- **Notes**: Induces apoptosis in AML lines; nanomolar potency but with a narrow window—use finer spacing at the low end.

## 17. Carfilzomib
- **Mechanism**: Irreversible proteasome inhibitor.
- **Test range**: **1–2,000 nM**.
- **Notes**: Strong induction of apoptosis in some AML models; test lower concentrations carefully.

## 18. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide (representative IMiD)
- **Mechanism**: Cereblon‑modulating immunomodulatory drugs; alter substrate ubiquitination (e.g., IKZF1/3).
- **Test range**:  
  - **Lenalidomide**: **10–50,000 nM**  
  - **Thalidomide**: **0.1–100 µM** (100–100,000 nM; poorer solubility)
- **Notes**: Direct antiproliferative effects in AML are variable but present in subsets; more recognized in MDS/5q‑ but still worth screening in MOLM‑13.

## 19. Metformin
- **Mechanism**: Indirect AMPK activation; inhibits mitochondrial complex I; metabolic stress.
- **Test range**: **0.1–10 mM** (100–10,000 µM).
- **Notes**: Requires high in vitro concentrations; reported to reduce proliferation and induce energetic crisis in several AML lines.

## 20. Chloroquine (or Hydroxychloroquine)
- **Mechanism**: Lysosomotropic autophagy inhibitor.
- **Test range**: **1–50 µM** (1,000–50,000 nM).
- **Notes**: Often used to inhibit protective autophagy in AML; can have modest direct antiproliferative effects and strong synergy with FLT3 or BCL‑2 inhibitors.

---

### Practical IC₅₀ Assay Notes for MOLM‑13

- **Exposure time**: 72 h is standard for proliferation/viability; for nucleoside analogs (azacitidine/decitabine) consider 72–96 h.
- **Plate format**: 96‑ or 384‑well plates, 0.5–1 × 10⁵ cells/mL starting density.
- **Readouts**: CellTiter‑Glo or similar ATP‑based assay; cross‑check selected hits with annexin V/PI or caspase assays.
- **Controls**:
  - Positive death control: doxorubicin or staurosporine at a high concentration.
  - Vehicle control: matched DMSO (typically ≤0.1% v/v in all wells).
- **Curve design**: For each drug, a **10‑point 3‑ or 5‑fold dilution series** spanning the ranges above usually yields good IC₅₀ curves.
