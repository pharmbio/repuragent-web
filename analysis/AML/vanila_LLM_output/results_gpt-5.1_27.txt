=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 existing drugs with published or strong preclinical rationale for repurposing in AML, especially FLT3‑ITD–positive models like MOLM‑13. For each, I include:

- **Primary known target/use**
- **Rationale in AML/MOLM13 (brief)**
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay concentration range** (final drug concentration in wells), suitable for proliferation/viability assays (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTT, resazurin) over 48–72 h in MOLM13.

Unless otherwise noted, use a **10‑point or 12‑point half‑log dilution series** spanning the indicated range.

---

## 1. Sorafenib
- **Original use**: Multikinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR); approved for renal cell carcinoma, HCC.
- **Rationale**: Strong activity against FLT3‑ITD AML; MOLM13 is FLT3‑ITD–positive and sorafenib is widely used as FLT3 inhibitor in preclinical AML work.
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 10 µM** (e.g., 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM; 1, 3, 10 µM).

---

## 2. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Original use**: Multikinase inhibitor; approved for FLT3‑mutated AML but originally developed for solid tumors.
- **Rationale**: Established FLT3 inhibitor with activity in MOLM13 and other FLT3‑ITD lines.
- **Suggested range**: **0.3 nM – 3 µM** (often low‑nanomolar IC₅₀ in FLT3‑ITD cells).

---

## 3. Ponatinib
- **Original use**: BCR‑ABL inhibitor (including T315I); approved for CML/ALL.
- **Rationale**: Potent inhibitor of FLT3 (including resistant mutants). Strong preclinical data in FLT3‑ITD AML lines including MOLM13.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 nM – 1 µM**.

---

## 4. Dasatinib
- **Original use**: SRC/ABL inhibitor; approved for CML/Ph+ ALL.
- **Rationale**: Inhibits multiple tyrosine kinases (SRC family, KIT, PDGFR). Shows activity in some AML subtypes and can inhibit downstream signaling in FLT3‑ITD cells.
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

---

## 5. Imatinib
- **Original use**: BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR inhibitor; CML/GIST.
- **Rationale**: Limited direct efficacy in most AML, but activity in KIT‑dependent subsets. Useful as a repurposing candidate in specific genetic backgrounds and as a control compound.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 µM – 50 µM** (MOLM13 is not KIT‑addicted; expect weaker effect but still testable).

---

## 6. Nilotinib
- **Original use**: Second‑generation BCR‑ABL inhibitor; CML.
- **Rationale**: Broader kinase inhibition than imatinib; some activity in AML models, particularly with KIT/PDGFR involvement.
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

---

## 7. Sunitinib
- **Original use**: VEGFR, PDGFR, KIT inhibitor; renal cell carcinoma, GIST.
- **Rationale**: Inhibits FLT3 and related RTKs. Shown to inhibit proliferation in FLT3‑mutant AML lines.
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 20 µM**.

---

## 8. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Original use**: BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved for CLL and AML (but originally for CLL/NHL).
- **Rationale**: Highly potent pro‑apoptotic agent in AML; MOLM13 is BCL‑2‑dependent and sensitive at low‑nanomolar levels.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 nM – 1 µM**.

---

## 9. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)
- **Original use**: BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor (hematologic malignancies).
- **Rationale**: Broad anti‑apoptotic protein inhibitor with known efficacy in AML cell lines; used as a tool for BCL‑2 family addiction.
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

---

## 10. Panobinostat (LBH589)
- **Original use**: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma.
- **Rationale**: Epigenetic modulation in AML; induces apoptosis and differentiation in AML cell lines including FLT3‑ITD models.
- **Suggested range**: **0.3 nM – 3 µM**.

---

## 11. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Original use**: HDAC inhibitor; CTCL.
- **Rationale**: Widely used HDAC inhibitor with antiproliferative effects in AML cell lines. Often used in combination with hypomethylating agents or FLT3 inhibitors.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 µM – 20 µM**.

---

## 12. Decitabine (5‑aza‑2′‑deoxycytidine)
- **Original use**: DNA hypomethylating agent; approved for MDS/AML.
- **Rationale**: Induces DNA hypomethylation and differentiation/apoptosis. Although a standard AML drug, it was originally MDS‑focused and is an archetypal epigenetic repurposing candidate.
- **Suggested range**: **1 nM – 10 µM** for 72‑h assays (fresh drug every 24 h if possible due to instability).

---

## 13. Azacitidine
- **Original use**: Hypomethylating agent; MDS/AML.
- **Rationale**: Similar to decitabine; used in AML and high‑risk MDS, affects proliferation and differentiation of AML lines.
- **Suggested range**: **10 nM – 30 µM** (again, consider replenishing).

---

## 14. Rapamycin (Sirolimus)
- **Original use**: mTOR inhibitor; immunosuppressant.
- **Rationale**: AML cells often show activated PI3K/AKT/mTOR signaling. Rapamycin can reduce growth and clonogenicity in AML lines including FLT3‑ITD.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 nM – 1 µM**.

---

## 15. Everolimus (RAD001)
- **Original use**: mTOR inhibitor; renal cell carcinoma, breast cancer, NETs.
- **Rationale**: Similar to rapamycin with better pharmacologic properties; has shown growth inhibition in AML preclinical studies.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**.

---

## 16. Metformin
- **Original use**: Type 2 diabetes; AMPK activator, mitochondrial complex I inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Anti‑proliferative and pro‑differentiation effects in leukemia models; impacts cellular metabolism and mTOR signaling. Typical IC₅₀ in vitro is in the high‑micromolar to millimolar range.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 mM – 20 mM** (use higher end cautiously and check osmolarity).

---

## 17. Disulfiram (± copper)
- **Original use**: Alcohol aversion therapy (ALDH inhibitor).
- **Rationale**: Targets ALDH‑positive leukemic stem/progenitor cells; with copper, can inhibit NF‑κB and proteasome activity in AML cells.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - Disulfiram alone: **10 nM – 10 µM**  
  - If adding Cu²⁺ (e.g., CuCl₂ ~0.5–1 µM), keep disulfiram in the **10 nM–1 µM** window to minimize nonspecific toxicity.

---

## 18. Thioridazine
- **Original use**: Antipsychotic (dopamine receptor antagonist).
- **Rationale**: Reported to target leukemic stem cells and disrupt autophagy and calcium homeostasis; kills AML cells preferentially over normal HSCs in some studies.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 µM – 30 µM** (watch for nonspecific toxicity at high doses).

---

## 19. Mebendazole
- **Original use**: Anthelmintic; microtubule inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Microtubule disruption and potential hedgehog pathway inhibition; antiproliferative effects reported in several cancers including leukemias.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 µM – 30 µM**.

---

## 20. Arsenic trioxide (ATO)
- **Original use**: APL (PML‑RARA+) treatment.
- **Rationale**: Induces oxidative stress, apoptosis, and degradation of oncogenic proteins. Though classically for APL, ATO has broader activity in AML lines at higher concentrations.
- **Suggested range**: **0.1 µM – 20 µM**.

---

### Practical IC₅₀ Assay Setup (brief)

- **Cells**: MOLM13 in log‑phase, viability >90%; seed at ~5–10×10³ cells/well (96‑well, 100 µL).
- **Incubation**: 48–72 h (many kinase inhibitors show clear effects at 72 h; hypomethylating agents may need 72 h + replenishment).
- **Readout**: ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or similar viability assay.
- **Dilutions**: Prepare serial half‑log dilutions from the top concentration down to at least 2–3 orders of magnitude below expected IC₅₀.
- **Controls**: 0.1–0.2% DMSO vehicle; optional positive control (e.g., sorafenib or venetoclax).

These ranges are intended to bracket likely IC₅₀ values while avoiding immediate extreme toxicity or solubility issues. For any specific compound you prioritize, you can narrow the window after a first pass.
