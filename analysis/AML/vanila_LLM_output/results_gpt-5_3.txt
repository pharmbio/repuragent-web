=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 already-marketed drugs with published preclinical activity against AML cell lines (with emphasis on FLT3-ITD models closely related to MOLM-13, and MOLM-13 where specifically reported). For each, I summarize the target, a brief rationale, and literature-reported potency in MOLM-13 or closely related FLT3-ITD AML lines (e.g., MOLM-13, MV4-11). Potency values are approximate and vary by assay format and conditions; please consult the cited primary sources for the exact methods and full concentration–response details.

Kinase inhibitors with FLT3 or pathway activity
1) Sorafenib (multi-kinase; FLT3-active)
- Rationale: Inhibits FLT3 signaling; strong activity in FLT3-ITD AML lines including MOLM-13/MV4-11.
- Reported potency: Low-nanomolar proliferation/viability IC50 in FLT3-ITD AML lines (often ~1–20 nM).
- Key refs: Weisberg et al., Blood 2009; Zarrinkar et al., Blood 2009 (FLT3 inhibitor profiling, includes sorafenib).

2) Sunitinib (multi-kinase; FLT3-active)
- Rationale: Inhibits FLT3 and related kinases; active in FLT3-ITD AML.
- Reported potency: Low- to mid-nanomolar in FLT3-ITD lines (tens of nM typical).
- Key refs: Zarrinkar et al., Blood 2009.

3) Ponatinib (ABL/FLT3/VEGFR; broad TKI)
- Rationale: Potent against FLT3 WT and mutants; active in MOLM-13 and other FLT3-ITD lines.
- Reported potency: Low-nanomolar viability IC50 in FLT3-ITD AML (often ~5–30 nM).
- Key refs: Smith et al., Blood 2012; Gozgit et al., PLoS One 2011.

4) Cabozantinib (MET/VEGFR; FLT3-active preclinically)
- Rationale: Inhibits FLT3 signaling; preclinical activity in FLT3-ITD AML cell lines.
- Reported potency: Low- to mid-nanomolar in FLT3-ITD AML models.
- Key refs: Choi et al., Mol Cancer Ther 2012; Li et al., PLoS One 2013 (cabozantinib vs FLT3).

5) Regorafenib (multi-kinase; VEGFR/RAF; reported FLT3 activity)
- Rationale: Preclinical activity in FLT3-ITD AML lines via FLT3/RAF pathway inhibition.
- Reported potency: Submicromolar in FLT3-ITD AML cell viability assays.
- Key refs: Kampa-Schittenhelm et al., PLoS One 2013 (multi-kinase TKIs in AML).

6) Dasatinib (SRC/ABL/KIT)
- Rationale: Inhibits SRC family and KIT; reported anti-AML activity including in lines with activated tyrosine kinase signaling.
- Reported potency: Typically submicromolar in AML cell lines; sensitivity varies by genotype.
- Key refs: Weisberg et al., Leukemia 2010.

7) Nilotinib (ABL/KIT)
- Rationale: Activity in KIT-dependent AML and some FLT3-activated contexts; preclinical anti-AML effects.
- Reported potency: Submicromolar in sensitive AML contexts (genotype-dependent).
- Key refs: Weisberg et al., Leukemia 2010.

8) Pazopanib (multi-kinase; VEGFR/PDGFR; reported FLT3 activity)
- Rationale: Reported to inhibit FLT3 signaling and AML proliferation in preclinical studies.
- Reported potency: Submicromolar in FLT3-ITD AML lines in some studies.
- Key refs: Kampa-Schittenhelm et al., PLoS One 2013.

RAS/MAPK, mTOR, and JAK pathway inhibitors
9) Trametinib (MEK1/2)
- Rationale: FLT3-ITD signals via RAS/MAPK; MEK inhibition reduces AML proliferation and can sensitize to BCL2 blockade.
- Reported potency: Low- to mid-nanomolar in AML lines with MAPK activation.
- Key refs: Konopleva et al., Cancer Discov 2014 (MEK pathway targeting in AML).

10) Cobimetinib (MEK1)
- Rationale: Similar to trametinib; preclinical AML activity and combinations reported.
- Reported potency: Tens to hundreds of nM in AML lines.
- Key refs: Tyner et al., Cancer Res 2013 (functional screens in AML).

11) Everolimus or Temsirolimus (mTORC1 inhibitors)
- Rationale: mTOR signaling supports AML growth; mTOR inhibitors suppress proliferation.
- Reported potency: Tens of nM for pathway inhibition; cell viability effects often in submicromolar range depending on context.
- Key refs: Recher et al., Blood 2005; Perl et al., Clin Cancer Res 2009.

12) Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2)
- Rationale: JAK/STAT contributes to AML survival in subsets; activity can be context-specific and synergistic with FLT3/BCL2 inhibitors.
- Reported potency: Typically submicromolar to low micromolar in sensitive AML lines.
- Key refs: Tyner et al., Cancer Res 2013; Koppikar et al., Cancer Cell 2012.

Epigenetic and nuclear export inhibitors
13) Panobinostat (HDAC inhibitor)
- Rationale: HDAC inhibition induces apoptosis/differentiation in AML, including FLT3-ITD lines.
- Reported potency: Low-nanomolar in AML viability assays (often ~10–50 nM).
- Key refs: Fiskus et al., Cancer Res 2009; Fiskus et al., Leukemia 2012.

14) Vorinostat/SAHA (HDAC inhibitor)
- Rationale: Broad anti-AML activity preclinically; used clinically in CTCL with repurposing interest in AML.
- Reported potency: Typically submicromolar to low micromolar in AML lines.
- Key refs: Rao et al., Blood 2008; Heidel et al., Leukemia 2011.

15) Romidepsin (HDAC inhibitor)
- Rationale: Potent class I HDAC inhibitor; induces apoptosis in AML preclinical models.
- Reported potency: Tens of nM in AML cell line viability assays.
- Key refs: Lee et al., Clin Cancer Res 2012.

16) Selinexor (XPO1 inhibitor)
- Rationale: Blocks nuclear export; robust preclinical AML activity including MOLM-13 and MV4-11.
- Reported potency: Typically ~0.1–0.5 μM in AML cell viability.
- Key refs: Etchin et al., Leukemia 2013; Etchin et al., J Hematol Oncol 2016.

Proteasome inhibitors
17) Bortezomib (26S proteasome)
- Rationale: Proteasome inhibition triggers AML apoptosis; strong activity in FLT3-ITD lines (MOLM-13/MV4-11).
- Reported potency: Low-nanomolar (often ~5–20 nM) in AML cell viability assays.
- Key refs: Niewerth et al., Leuk Res 2013; Miller et al., Blood 2007.

18) Carfilzomib (proteasome)
- Rationale: Irreversible proteasome inhibitor with anti-AML activity preclinically.
- Reported potency: Low-nanomolar in AML lines.
- Key refs: Niewerth et al., Leuk Res 2013.

Mitochondrial/oxidative stress and other repurposed agents
19) Tigecycline (antibiotic; mitochondrial translation inhibitor)
- Rationale: AML (including leukemic stem/progenitor cells) relies on mitochondrial translation/OXPHOS; tigecycline impairs this.
- Reported potency: Submicromolar (often ~0.1–1 μM) in AML cell lines and primary blasts.
- Key refs: Skrtic et al., Sci Transl Med 2011; Jones et al., Cancer Cell 2008.

20) Auranofin (rheumatoid arthritis; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor)
- Rationale: Induces oxidative stress and apoptosis in AML cells; reported activity in FLT3-ITD models.
- Reported potency: Submicromolar to low micromolar in AML lines.
- Key refs: Raninga et al., Blood 2015; Fiskus et al., Cancer Res 2014.

Additional repurposing candidates with AML activity reported (choose based on your interest and literature support)
- Niclosamide (anthelmintic; Wnt/STAT3/oxidative phosphorylation effects) – submicromolar in AML lines (e.g., Li et al., Oncotarget 2014).
- Ciclopirox olamine (antifungal; iron chelation/eIF5A hypusination) – submicromolar to low micromolar in AML lines (e.g., Zhou et al., PLoS One 2010).
- Disulfiram ± copper (ALDH1/XIAP/NF-κB pathways) – submicromolar with copper in AML cells (e.g., Triscott et al., Oncotarget 2012).
- Mebendazole (anthelmintic; microtubules/hedgehog) – submicromolar to micromolar in leukemia models (e.g., Hou et al., Mol Cancer Ther 2015).

Notes and how to verify/choose concentration ranges
- Potencies depend on assay format, serum, time, and cell density; reported IC50s are a starting point, not universal. For MOLM-13-specific values and exact experiment details, consult the primary papers and large pharmacogenomic resources:
  - Zarrinkar et al., Blood 2009 (comprehensive FLT3 inhibitor profiling).
  - Tyner et al., Cancer Research 2013 (functional screening across AML).
  - DepMap PRISM repurposing dataset (Corsello et al., Nat Med 2020) includes MOLM-13; you can query drug sensitivities systematically.
  - GDSC/Genomics of Drug Sensitivity in Cancer and CTRP/CTD2 datasets also report IC50/AUC values for MOLM-13 across many approved drugs.
