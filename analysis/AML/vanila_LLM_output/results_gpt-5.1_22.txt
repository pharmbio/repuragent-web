=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 approved/late‑stage drugs with evidence of activity in AML (especially FLT3‑ITD–positive lines like MOLM‑13) or strong mechanistic rationale. For each, I include:

- **Main known target(s)/rationale in AML**
- **Suggested concentration range for in‑vitro IC50 determinations in MOLM13** (final concentration in culture medium, 72‑h viability assay such as CellTiter-Glo).  
  Use serial dilutions (e.g., 10‑ or 3‑fold) across the indicated range.

**General note for all compounds**

- Prepare **10–1000× stocks** in DMSO, filter if needed.
- Keep final DMSO ≤0.1% (≤0.3% max) in all wells, including vehicle controls.
- 72 h exposure is standard for proliferation/viability in MOLM13; 48 h can be used for more acute signaling readouts.
- For each drug, start with a **broad range** that clearly spans from “no effect” to “near‑maximal killing,” then refine once you know approximate IC50.

## 1. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Type**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, PKC); approved for FLT3‑mut AML.
- **Rationale**: Directly targets FLT3‑ITD, the driver in MOLM13.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 10 µM**  
  Practical starting series: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

## 2. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)
- **Type**: FLT3/AXL inhibitor; approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mut AML.
- **Rationale**: Very potent against FLT3‑ITD; strong activity in MOLM13 reported.
- **Test range**: **0.01 nM – 1 µM**  
  Suggested: 0.01, 0.03, 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1 µM.

## 3. Quizartinib (AC220)
- **Type**: Highly selective FLT3 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: One of the most potent FLT3 inhibitors in FLT3‑ITD+ lines.
- **Test range**: **0.01 nM – 1 µM**  
  Same series as gilteritinib.

## 4. Sorafenib
- **Type**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR); used off‑label in FLT3‑ITD AML.
- **Rationale**: Inhibits FLT3 and downstream MAPK; active in MOLM13.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 20 µM**  
  Suggested: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10, 20 µM.

## 5. Crenolanib
- **Type**: FLT3/PDGFR inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Inhibits FLT3 including some TKD mutants; preclinical activity in MOLM13.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**  
  Suggested: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3 µM.

## 6. Dasatinib
- **Type**: SRC/ABL multi‑kinase inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Inhibits SRC family kinases and some KIT/FLT3 signaling; reported anti‑proliferative effects in AML lines.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 10 µM**  
  Suggested: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

## 7. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Type**: BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved for AML in combination.
- **Rationale**: Promotes apoptosis in AML cells dependent on BCL‑2; MOLM13 is sensitive, especially with co‑treatments.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 10 µM**  
  Suggested: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

## 8. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)
- **Type**: BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Broader anti‑apoptotic blockade; many AML lines responsive.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 10 µM** (as above).

## 9. Panobinostat (LBH589)
- **Type**: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Epigenetic reprogramming, induces differentiation/apoptosis; potent across AML panels.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**  
  Suggested: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3 µM.

## 10. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Type**: HDAC inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Alters acetylation state of histones and non‑histone proteins; active in AML preclinical studies.
- **Test range**: **10 nM – 10 µM**  
  Suggested: 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

## 11. Decitabine
- **Type**: DNA methyltransferase inhibitor (hypomethylating agent).
- **Rationale**: Approved in AML/MDS; induces DNA hypomethylation and cell death; activity in MOLM13 with longer exposure.
- **Test range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**  
  Use 72–96 h; suggested: 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

## 12. Azacitidine
- **Type**: Hypomethylating agent.
- **Rationale**: Similar to decitabine, widely used in AML.
- **Test range**: **10 nM – 30 µM**  
  Suggested: 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10, 30 µM (72–96 h).

## 13. Ruxolitinib
- **Type**: JAK1/2 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: JAK/STAT pathway frequently activated in AML; combinatorial benefit shown.
- **Test range**: **1 nM – 20 µM**  
  Suggested: 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10, 20 µM.

## 14. Ibrutinib
- **Type**: BTK inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: BTK and related B‑cell receptor–like signaling contribute to AML survival in some contexts; data show modest direct effects but interesting combinations.
- **Test range**: **10 nM – 30 µM**  
  Suggested: 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10, 30 µM.

## 15. Everolimus
- **Type**: mTORC1 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway often active in AML; everolimus can reduce proliferation and enhance apoptosis.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 10 µM**  
  Suggested: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

## 16. Idelalisib
- **Type**: PI3Kδ inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: PI3K signaling supports AML survival; δ isoform expressed in many hematologic malignancies.
- **Test range**: **1 nM – 20 µM**  
  Suggested: 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10, 20 µM.

## 17. Trametinib
- **Type**: MEK1/2 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Blocks MAPK/ERK pathway downstream of FLT3 and RAS; shown to impair AML cell growth.
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 10 µM**  
  Suggested: 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

## 18. Imatinib
- **Type**: BCR‑ABL/KIT/PDGFR inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Limited direct activity in FLT3‑ITD AML, but relevant in KIT‑driven subsets or for pathway comparison and potential off‑target effects.
- **Test range**: **10 nM – 30 µM**  
  Suggested: 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10, 30 µM.

## 19. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide (choose one primary)
- **Type**: Immunomodulatory drugs (IMiDs).
- **Rationale**: Affect cereblon‑mediated degradation, NF‑κB, microenvironment interactions; direct cytotoxicity in AML is variable but worth testing.
- **Test range**:  
  - **Lenalidomide**: **10 nM – 50 µM**  
  - **Thalidomide**: **0.1 µM – 100 µM**  
  Use: 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10, 30, 50 µM (lenalidomide).

## 20. Auranofin
- **Type**: Oral gold compound (rheumatoid arthritis drug).
- **Rationale**: Inhibits thioredoxin reductase, increases ROS, reported cytotoxic to AML lines including FLT3‑ITD models.
- **Test range**: **1 nM – 20 µM**  
  Suggested: 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10, 20 µM.

### Practical IC50 assay design for MOLM13

- **Cell density**: 3–5 × 10⁴ cells/well (96‑well) in 100 µL RPMI + 10–20% FBS, antibiotics.
- **Drug addition**: 11‑point serial dilutions (at least) in triplicate or quadruplicate.
- **Incubation**: 72 h at 37°C, 5% CO₂.
- **Readout**: Luminescent ATP assay (CellTiter‑Glo) or resazurin; normalize to vehicle control.
- Fit a **4‑parameter logistic curve** to obtain IC50s.
