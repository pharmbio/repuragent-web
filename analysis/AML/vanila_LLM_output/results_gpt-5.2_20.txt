## Practical IC50 assay setup (MOLM-13)
- **Assay**: CellTiter-Glo / Resazurin / IncuCyte confluence, 72 h (MOLM-13 often shows clearer separation at 72 h than 48 h for cytostatic drugs).
- **Seeding**: 5–20k cells/well (96-well), optimize for log-phase growth.
- **Vehicle**: Keep **final DMSO constant** (≤0.1–0.2%).
- **Series**: 10-point, 1:3 (or 1:2) serial dilution.
- **Controls**: Positive control such as **midostaurin** or **quizartinib** (if you have them) for MOLM-13 FLT3-ITD sensitivity; negative control DMSO.

## Suggested plate-ready dilution scheme (example)
For most kinase inhibitors / targeted drugs:
- **Top**: 10 or 20 µM  
- **10-point, 1:3 dilution** gives: 20, 6.67, 2.22, 0.741, 0.247, 0.0823, 0.0274, 0.00914, 0.00305, 0.00102 µM.

For very potent agents (trametinib, bortezomib, venetoclax):
- **Top**: 1 µM (or 10 µM for venetoclax), start lower (0.1–1 nM) and cover up to top.

## Important practical notes (so your IC50s are interpretable)
- **MOLM-13 is FLT3-ITD**, so FLT3-pathway inhibitors (e.g., sorafenib/ponatinib/multi-kinase) often show clearer single-agent effects than in FLT3-WT lines, but this is not guaranteed.
- **Protein binding & serum**: IC50 shifts with FBS %. Keep it consistent (e.g., 10–20% FBS) and report it.
- **Edge effects & evaporation**: use outer wells for buffer/medium or a humidity chamber.
