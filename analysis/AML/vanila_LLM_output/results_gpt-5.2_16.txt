### Notes to make these ranges “experimentally testable”
- For **kinase inhibitors / HDAC inhibitors / proteasome inhibitors**, a **10‑point half‑log curve** (e.g., 0.0003–10 µM) is usually sufficient.
- For **metformin**, you generally need **millimolar** ranges in standard glucose media; consider also testing in **lower glucose** or with OXPHOS‑reliant conditions if relevant.
- For **hypomethylating agents**, consider **longer exposure (96–144 h)** or **media/drug refresh** because effects can be delayed and S‑phase dependent.

## Practical IC₅₀ setup (MOLM‑13) to increase chance of interpretable results
- **Exposure time:** 72 h (standard) plus optional 96 h for azacitidine/decitabine.
- **Curve design:** 10–12 points, 1:3 or 1:2.5 serial dilutions; include vehicle controls.
- **Seeding density:** keep in log growth (commonly 5–20k cells/well in 96‑well; adjust to your lab norms).
- **Readout:** CellTiter‑Glo (ATP) or resazurin; ensure no compound interference (e.g., redox‑active compounds).
- **Controls:**  
  - Positive cytotoxic control: staurosporine (e.g., 1 nM–1 µM) or bortezomib.  
  - Pathway control: quizartinib/gilteritinib for FLT3‑ITD dependence.
- **Confirm mechanism vs general toxicity:** follow-up with apoptosis (Annexin V/PI), cell cycle, and target engagement (e.g., p‑FLT3/p‑STAT5).
