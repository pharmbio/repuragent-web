=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used (or late‑stage) drugs with evidence or strong rationale for repurposing in AML, with emphasis on FLT3‑ITD–positive lines such as MOLM13. For each, I give:

- **Primary known target/mechanism**
- **Key AML/MOLM13 evidence or rationale (brief)**
- **Suggested IC50 assay concentration range** (a log‑spaced screening range you can refine later; values are for in‑vitro cell viability/proliferation assays, e.g., 72 h MTS/CellTiter-Glo on MOLM13).  

Use DMSO ≤0.1% (preferably ≤0.05%) in all wells, including controls.

---

## 1. Midostaurin (PKC412)

- **Type**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, PKC).
- **Rationale**: Approved for FLT3‑mutated AML. Inhibits FLT3‑ITD AML cell lines including MOLM13 at low nM.
- **Test range**:  
 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 – 1000 nM.

---

## 2. Sorafenib

- **Type**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR).
- **Rationale**: Off‑label in FLT3‑ITD AML; strong activity in MOLM13 and MV4‑11.
- **Test range**:  
 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 – 1000 nM.

---

## 3. Sunitinib

- **Type**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, VEGFR).
- **Rationale**: In vitro FLT3 inhibition; reported anti‑proliferative effects in FLT3‑ITD AML models.
- **Test range**:  
 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 – 1000 nM.

---

## 4. Ponatinib

- **Type**: BCR‑ABL/FLT3/FGFR inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Potent FLT3 inhibitor including some TKD mutants; active in FLT3‑ITD AML cell lines.
- **Test range**:  
 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 nM.

---

## 5. Dasatinib

- **Type**: SRC/ABL family kinase inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Reported anti‑leukemic activity in subsets of AML (e.g., SRC‑dependent); some effects in FLT3‑ITD models via downstream signaling.
- **Test range**:  
 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 – 1000 nM.

---

## 6. Nilotinib

- **Type**: BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Shown to inhibit proliferation in some AML cell lines, particularly those with activated KIT/PDGFR pathways.
- **Test range**:  
 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 – 1000 – 3000 nM.

---

## 7. Imatinib

- **Type**: BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Utility in AML subtypes with KIT or PDGFR rearrangements; as a repurposing benchmark it helps discriminate dependency on these kinases in MOLM13.
- **Test range**:  
 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 µM (less potent in FLT3‑ITD AML, so go higher).

---

## 8. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)

- **Type**: BCL‑2 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Approved for AML in combination; MOLM13 is typically BCL‑2‑dependent and quite sensitive.
- **Test range**:  
 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 – 1000 nM.

---

## 9. Ruxolitinib

- **Type**: JAK1/2 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: JAK/STAT pathway contributes to survival in some AML contexts; can reveal JAK‑STAT dependency in MOLM13.
- **Test range**:  
 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 nM.

---

## 10. Tofacitinib

- **Type**: JAK1/3 inhibitor (approved for RA).
- **Rationale**: Modulates cytokine‑driven survival signaling; some JAK‑dependent leukemias show sensitivity.
- **Test range**:  
 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 nM.

---

## 11. Everolimus

- **Type**: mTORC1 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: PI3K/AKT/mTOR is active in AML; mTOR inhibition reduces AML proliferation and synergizes with FLT3/BCL‑2 inhibitors.
- **Test range**:  
 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 nM.

---

## 12. Sirolimus (Rapamycin)

- **Type**: mTORC1 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Classic mTOR inhibitor with known anti‑proliferative effects in AML cell lines.
- **Test range**:  
 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 nM.

---

## 13. Metformin

- **Type**: AMPK activator / mitochondrial complex I inhibitor (antidiabetic).
- **Rationale**: At high micromolar–low millimolar levels, can impair mitochondrial function and proliferation in AML cells.
- **Test range**:  
 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 mM.

---

## 14. Disulfiram (± copper)

- **Type**: ALDH inhibitor; forms Cu‑dependent complexes that generate ROS and inhibit proteasome/NPL4.
- **Rationale**: AML stem/progenitor cells often ALDH‑high; disulfiram + Cu shows activity in AML lines including FLT3‑ITD.
- **Test range** (disulfiram alone, Cu added separately at e.g. 1–5 µM):  
 0.1 – 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 µM.

---

## 15. Valproic acid

- **Type**: HDAC inhibitor (class I/IIa) and anticonvulsant.
- **Rationale**: Differentiation‑inducing and cytostatic effects in AML; used in low‑intensity regimens.
- **Test range**:  
 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 µM.

---

## 16. Vorinostat (SAHA)

- **Type**: HDAC inhibitor (approved for CTCL).
- **Rationale**: Potent anti‑proliferative and pro‑apoptotic effects in multiple AML cell lines, including FLT3‑ITD models.
- **Test range**:  
 0.1 – 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 µM.

---

## 17. Panobinostat

- **Type**: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Very potent; reported low‑nanomolar activity in AML cell lines.
- **Test range**:  
 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 nM.

---

## 18. Chloroquine (or Hydroxychloroquine)

- **Type**: Autophagy/lysosome inhibitor (antimalarial).
- **Rationale**: Autophagy supports AML survival, especially under targeted therapy; CQ/HCQ has shown AML cell line cytotoxicity and synergy with TKIs.
- **Test range**:  
 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 – 1000 µM.

---

## 19. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide (IMiDs)

- **Type**: Cereblon modulators; immunomodulatory, induce degradation of IKZF1/3, etc.
- **Rationale**: Some AML subsets show sensitivity; also modulate microenvironmental support.
- **Test range**:  
 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 – 100 – 300 – 1000 µM.

---

## 20. Niclosamide

- **Type**: Anthelmintic; uncouples oxidative phosphorylation, inhibits Wnt/STAT3/NF‑κB.
- **Rationale**: Published anti‑leukemic and anti‑stem‑cell data in AML; affects mitochondrial metabolism and survival signaling.
- **Test range**:  
 0.1 – 0.3 – 1 – 3 – 10 – 30 µM.

---

### Practical notes for IC50 assays in MOLM13

- **Assay setup**: 72 h exposure, 2–5 × 10³ cells/well in 96‑well plates is typical for MOLM13; readout via ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or resazurin/MTS.
- **Dilution scheme**: Use at least 7–8 concentrations per drug in 3‑fold serial dilutions around the ranges above; include DMSO‑only controls.
- **Data**: Fit a 4‑parameter logistic curve (Hill slope variable) to calculate IC50. Confirm with at least 2 biological repeats.
- **Controls**: Including a “positive control” FLT3 inhibitor (e.g., midostaurin or sorafenib) on each plate will help benchmark MOLM13 sensitivity and assay quality (Z’).
