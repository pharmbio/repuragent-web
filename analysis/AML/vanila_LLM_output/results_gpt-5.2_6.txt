## Practical IC50 assay concentration design (works for all 20 drugs)
For MOLM‑13 (FLT3‑ITD+) and most AML lines, a robust starting design:

- **8–10 point, 3-fold serial dilution**
- **Top concentration** chosen by drug class (below)
- **DMSO ≤0.1%**, constant across wells
- Readout at **72 h** (common for MOLM‑13) using CellTiter‑Glo or resazurin
- Include positive controls: e.g., **midostaurin** or **quizartinib** (if available) for FLT3‑ITD sensitivity benchmarking

### Top concentrations by “repurposed” drug category (to avoid solubility/artifacts)
- **Targeted kinase inhibitors (non-oncology too):** top **10 µM** (often enough)
- **Oncology small molecules (already potent):** top **1–3 µM** (to avoid off-target toxicity dominating)
- **Epigenetic/HDAC inhibitors:** top **10 µM**
- **Antimetabolites/antibiotics (chemo-like):** top **10–30 µM** (depending on solubility)
- **Mitochondrial/anthelmintic/antipsychotic “polypharmacology” agents:** top **10–30 µM** (watch for nonspecific toxicity; include counterscreens)

## Notes to make the IC50s interpretable (especially in MOLM‑13)
- **MOLM‑13 is FLT3‑ITD**: include at least one **FLT3 pathway control** (midostaurin/sorafenib) every plate.
- **Serum effects:** some drugs (e.g., niclosamide, mebendazole) are highly protein bound; consider repeating with consistent serum (e.g., 10–20% FBS) and note it in analysis.
- **Counterscreens:** run the same concentration series on a non-malignant line (e.g., PBMCs or CD34+ cells if available) to flag nonspecific toxicity.
- **Solubility checks:** at the top 1–2 concentrations, visually inspect wells or measure turbidity; precipitation can create false potency.
