=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing small molecules (approved or clinical-stage) with good rationale for repurposing or testing in AML — especially in FLT3-ITD AML lines such as MOLM13 — together with practical, experimentally-testable concentration guidance for an IC50/proliferation assay.

General assay recommendations (use with the concentration series below)
- Use a 10‑point, 3‑fold serial dilution series for each compound (gives coverage from the top concentration down to ~50 pM when top = 1 µM). Prepare compound stocks in DMSO and keep final DMSO ≤0.5% (ideally ≤0.1%).
- Plate MOLM13 in 96‑well format as suspension cells (typical starting density 5,000–10,000 cells/well), incubate 72 h (standard) before viability readout (CellTiter‑Glo or comparable). For nucleoside analogues you may also test 96 h or repeat dosing if desired.
- Run at least triplicates and include vehicle (DMSO) and positive controls (e.g., cytarabine and a FLT3 inhibitor).
- Report IC50 from nonlinear curve fit (four‑parameter logistic).

For each drug below I give: mechanism/why relevant to AML (and to MOLM13 where applicable), recommended top concentration for a 10‑point 3‑fold dilution series, and an approximate expected IC50 range (literature/cell-line dependent — use as a guide).

1) Gilteritinib (Xospata)
- Target: FLT3 (and AXL). MOLM13 (FLT3‑ITD) is typically sensitive.
- Top conc (for 10‑pt 3× series): 1 µM (series: 1 µM → ~50 pM)
- Expected IC50 (MOLM13-range): ~1–50 nM

2) Quizartinib (AC220)
- Target: FLT3 (very potent on FLT3‑ITD).
- Top conc: 100 nM (or 1 µM if you want extended range)
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–10 nM

3) Crenolanib
- Target: pan‑FLT3 inhibitor (active vs ITD and some TKD mutants).
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~1–100 nM

4) Midostaurin (PKC412)
- Target: multi‑kinase including FLT3.
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50: ~100 nM–1 µM

5) Sorafenib
- Target: multi‑kinase (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, etc.). Has activity against FLT3‑ITD.
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50: ~10–500 nM

6) Ponatinib
- Target: multi‑kinase (including FLT3 off‑target activity). Heterogeneous effects in AML.
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~1–200 nM

7) Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- Target: BCL‑2. AML lines (including some MOLM lines) can be sensitive, and combination therapy (hypomethylating agents) is clinically effective.
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~10–200 nM (cell‑dependent)

8) Navitoclax (ABT‑263)
- Target: BCL‑2/BCL‑xL. Useful comparator to venetoclax.
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~50–500 nM

9) Selinexor (XPO1 inhibitor)
- Target: nuclear export (XPO1). Active in AML preclinical models.
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~50–500 nM

10) Alvocidib / Flavopiridol
- Target: CDKs (notably CDK9; transcriptional addiction in AML).
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~10–500 nM

11) Palbociclib
- Target: CDK4/6. May inhibit AML proliferation in subsets; useful to test for cell‑cycle dependencies.
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–5 µM (cell dependent)

12) Panobinostat (LBH589)
- Target: pan‑HDAC inhibitor. Active in AML models and in combinations.
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~1–200 nM

13) Bortezomib
- Target: 20S proteasome. Proteasome inhibition can be cytotoxic in AML.
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~1–100 nM

14) Azacitidine
- Target: DNA methyltransferase (hypomethylating agent). In vitro dosing is different from in vivo; may require repeat dosing/time.
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50 (viability assays): often ~0.1–5 µM

15) Decitabine
- Target: DNMT inhibitor (similar notes to azacitidine).
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–5 µM

16) Cytarabine (Ara‑C)
- Target: nucleoside analogue; standard AML chemo. MOLM13 often sensitive.
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50: ~0.01–1 µM

17) Doxorubicin
- Target: DNA intercalator/topoisomerase II poison. Standard anthracycline in AML regimens.
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50: ~10–500 nM

18) Ibrutinib
- Target: BTK (and off‑targets). Some AML subtypes show sensitivity; useful repurposing candidate to test combinations.
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–10 µM

19) OTX015 / MK‑8628 (BET inhibitor)
- Target: BET bromodomain proteins (transcriptional regulators); active in AML models.
- Top conc: 1 µM
- Expected IC50: ~10–500 nM

20) Idasanutlin (RG7388)
- Target: MDM2 inhibitor (activates p53). Effective only in TP53 wild‑type AML — check MOLM13 p53 status before interpretation.
- Top conc: 10 µM
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–2 µM (p53 WT dependent)

Practical notes and variations
- If you prefer a single standardized series, choose top concentrations as above and perform a 10‑point 3× dilution for all drugs. For very potent kinase inhibitors you can reduce the top conc (e.g., 100 nM) to improve resolution in the low‑nM range.
- Some agents (azacitidine/decitabine/cytarabine) are metabolically labile and sometimes require renewed dosing or longer exposure (96 h) to see full effect — consider running both 72 h and 96 h timepoints.
- For MOLM13 (FLT3‑ITD) prioritize FLT3 inhibitors (gilteritinib, quizartinib, crenolanib, midostaurin, sorafenib, ponatinib) and BCL‑2 targeting (venetoclax) when selecting single agents; combinations (FLT3i + venetoclax, FLT3i + HMA, HDAC + BH3 mimetic) are commonly tested.
