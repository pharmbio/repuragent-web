## Practical IC50 assay setup (so the ranges above are usable)
- **Cells:** MOLM‑13 (and optionally MV4‑11 as another FLT3‑ITD comparator).  
- **Readout:** CellTiter‑Glo (ATP) at **72 h** (common for AML proliferation).  
- **Seeding:** often **2,000–10,000 cells/well** in 96‑well (optimize for log growth and no nutrient depletion).  
- **DMSO:** keep constant (e.g., **0.1%**).  
- **Curve design:** 10 points, **3‑fold** dilution; include **top/bottom controls** (DMSO vs a known active control such as midostaurin or sorafenib).  
- **Replicates:** technical triplicates; repeat on ≥2 days.

## Notes/caveats that matter for interpretation
- Some drugs above (e.g., **metformin, valproate**) often require **high concentrations** in vitro that may exceed clinically achievable free plasma levels; they’re still useful as mechanistic probes or for combination screens.
- **Niclosamide, mebendazole, disulfiram** can have **solubility/stability** issues; confirm solubility, avoid precipitation, and consider shorter pre-dilution times.
- For **disulfiram**, activity is frequently **copper‑dependent**; consider testing with and without added copper.
- MOLM‑13 is **FLT3‑ITD** and frequently shows sensitivity to FLT3-pathway suppression; targeted inhibitors often show steep curves and low‑nM IC50s.
