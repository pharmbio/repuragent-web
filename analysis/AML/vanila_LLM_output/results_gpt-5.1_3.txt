=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used (or late‑stage) drugs with evidence or strong rationale for repurposing in AML, especially FLT3‑mutant lines like MOLM‑13. For each, I include:

- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay test range** (final concentration series in 96‑well proliferation/viability assays; e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, resazurin).
- Ranges are chosen to:  
  1) bracket reported in‑vitro potencies where available, and  
  2) account for solubility and on‑target plasma levels seen in patients.

Use a 10‑point half‑log or log dilution series across these ranges (e.g., 0.1 nM–1 µM, 1 nM–10 µM, etc.). All concentrations are **final** in culture.

---

## 1. Midostaurin  
- **Type:** Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PKC), approved for FLT3‑mut+ AML.  
- **AML relevance:** Inhibits FLT3‑ITD; active in MOLM‑13 and MV4‑11.  
- **Suggested test range:** **0.5 nM – 5 µM**  
  - Start: 0.5 nM, then 1.5, 4.5, 13.5, 40.5, 121, 364, 1,092, 3,276, 5,000 nM (for example).  

## 2. Sorafenib  
- **Type:** Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR).  
- **AML relevance:** Widely used off‑label in FLT3‑ITD AML; strong data in MOLM‑13.  
- **Suggested test range:** **1 nM – 10 µM**  

## 3. Sunitinib  
- **Type:** Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, VEGFR).  
- **AML relevance:** Reported activity in FLT3‑mutant AML cell lines.  
- **Suggested test range:** **1 nM – 10 µM**  

## 4. Dasatinib  
- **Type:** Src/Abl kinase inhibitor (CML drug).  
- **AML relevance:** Inhibits multiple kinases relevant in AML; activity reported in some FLT3‑mutant lines and other AML subsets.  
- **Suggested test range:** **0.5 nM – 5 µM**  

## 5. Nilotinib  
- **Type:** BCR‑ABL / KIT inhibitor.  
- **AML relevance:** Inhibits KIT and may target some oncogenic tyrosine kinases implicated in AML subtypes.  
- **Suggested test range:** **1 nM – 10 µM**  

## 6. Imatinib  
- **Type:** BCR‑ABL / KIT / PDGFR inhibitor.  
- **AML relevance:** Activity in AML with KIT or PDGFR rearrangements; repurposing interest for specific genetic contexts.  
- **Suggested test range:** **10 nM – 20 µM**  

## 7. Venetoclax  
- **Type:** BCL‑2 inhibitor, approved for AML (in combination) and CLL.  
- **AML relevance:** Strong activity in many AML lines, including FLT3‑mutants; synergistic with FLT3 inhibitors.  
- **Suggested test range:** **0.1 nM – 2 µM**  

## 8. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)  
- **Type:** BCL‑2/BCL‑XL/BCL‑W inhibitor.  
- **AML relevance:** Potent apoptosis inducer in AML cell lines; tool and repurposing candidate.  
- **Suggested test range:** **0.5 nM – 5 µM**  

## 9. Ruxolitinib  
- **Type:** JAK1/2 inhibitor (myelofibrosis, PV).  
- **AML relevance:** JAK‑STAT pathway is activated in subsets of AML; some preclinical data in AML lines.  
- **Suggested test range:** **1 nM – 10 µM**  

## 10. Tofacitinib  
- **Type:** JAK inhibitor (rheumatoid arthritis).  
- **AML relevance:** JAK/STAT signaling contributes to survival in some AML contexts; candidate for combinatorial or repurposing screens.  
- **Suggested test range:** **10 nM – 20 µM**  

## 11. Ibrutinib  
- **Type:** BTK inhibitor (CLL, MCL).  
- **AML relevance:** Off‑target kinome profile hits multiple kinases in myeloid cells; some reports of activity in myeloid malignancies and AML stem‑cell–like populations.  
- **Suggested test range:** **10 nM – 20 µM**  

## 12. Everolimus  
- **Type:** mTORC1 inhibitor (solid tumors, TSC).  
- **AML relevance:** mTOR signaling is often hyperactive in AML; everolimus has shown antiproliferative effects in AML cell lines.  
- **Suggested test range:** **0.5 nM – 5 µM**  

## 13. Temsirolimus  
- **Type:** mTOR inhibitor (renal cell carcinoma).  
- **AML relevance:** Similar rationale as everolimus; reported activity in AML cells, including combination strategies.  
- **Suggested test range:** **1 nM – 10 µM**  

## 14. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- **Type:** HDAC inhibitor (CTCL).  
- **AML relevance:** HDAC inhibition promotes differentiation and apoptosis in AML cells; preclinical and early clinical AML data.  
- **Suggested test range:** **10 nM – 20 µM**  

## 15. Panobinostat  
- **Type:** Pan‑HDAC inhibitor (multiple myeloma).  
- **AML relevance:** Very potent; strong epigenetic effects in AML cell lines, synergizes with other agents.  
- **Suggested test range:** **1 nM – 5 µM**  

## 16. Decitabine  
- **Type:** DNA hypomethylating agent (MDS, AML).  
- **AML relevance:** Standard AML therapy; useful as a benchmark and combinatorial partner; can reduce proliferation in MOLM‑13 with longer exposure (≥48–72 h).  
- **Suggested test range:** **10 nM – 10 µM**  

## 17. Azacitidine  
- **Type:** DNA/RNA hypomethylating agent (MDS, AML).  
- **AML relevance:** Established AML drug; again a benchmark and combination partner.  
- **Suggested test range:** **50 nM – 20 µM**  

## 18. Bortezomib  
- **Type:** Proteasome inhibitor (multiple myeloma).  
- **AML relevance:** Induces apoptosis in AML cell lines, including FLT3‑mutant; affects NF‑κB and protein homeostasis.  
- **Suggested test range:** **0.5 nM – 1 µM**  

## 19. Carfilzomib  
- **Type:** Irreversible proteasome inhibitor.  
- **AML relevance:** Similar mechanistic rationale as bortezomib; reported cytotoxicity in AML preclinical models.  
- **Suggested test range:** **1 nM – 2 µM**  

## 20. Disulfiram (± copper)  
- **Type:** ALDH inhibitor (alcohol aversion drug), also inhibits NF‑κB/proteasome and affects ROS.  
- **AML relevance:** AML, including stem‑like cells, often ALDH‑high; disulfiram + Cu²⁺ has preclinical activity in AML cell lines.  
- **Suggested test range:**  
  - **Disulfiram:** **50 nM – 10 µM**  
  - If using copper supplementation (e.g., CuCl₂): keep Cu²⁺ in a low µM range (∼0.5–2 µM) and constant across wells.

---

### Practical assay notes

- **Exposure time:** For most kinase/anti‑apoptotic/proteasome/HDAC inhibitors, 48–72 h exposure in MOLM‑13 is typical for IC₅₀ determination. For hypomethylating agents (azacitidine, decitabine), 72 h or repeated low‑dose exposure more closely reflects their mechanism.
- **Solvents:**  
  - Stock most small‑molecule inhibitors and HDAC/proteasome/BCL‑2 drugs in DMSO (≤0.1% final DMSO in wells).  
  - Azacitidine/decitabine are usually prepared fresh in aqueous buffer immediately prior to use.
- **Initial screen design:**  
  - Start with the broad ranges above. After a first run, refine to a narrower window around the observed IC₅₀ (typically spanning 1–2 logs around it).  
  - Include a positive control known to kill MOLM‑13 (e.g., midostaurin or sorafenib) and a vehicle control.
