=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 existing drugs (approved or in advanced clinical use for other indications) that have published evidence of activity in AML and/or specifically in MOLM13 or similar FLT3‑ITD+ AML lines. For each, I include:

- Mechanism / why it’s relevant to AML  
- A practical **IC₅₀ assay testing range** for MOLM13 or similar AML cells (a starting point; should be refined empirically).  
- Notes on reported in‑vitro potency when available (approximate; varies by study, assay, and conditions).

**Important**:  
- Use appropriate DMSO and vehicle controls.  
- Final DMSO ≤0.1% (preferably ≤0.05%).  
- Suggested ranges are for 72‑h viability/proliferation assays (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, resazurin) in MOLM13. For shorter exposures (24–48 h) you may need higher top doses.

---

## 1. Sorafenib
- **Class/target**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR). Widely used off‑label in FLT3‑ITD AML.
- **Rationale**: Strong activity in FLT3‑ITD+ cell lines, including MOLM13.
- **Reported IC₅₀ (MOLM13/FLT3‑ITD)**: ~3–20 nM in many studies.
- **Suggested test range**:  
  - 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM (optionally up to 1 µM).

---

## 2. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Class/target**: Multikinase inhibitor, FLT3 and others. Approved for FLT3‑mut AML.
- **Rationale**: Potent FLT3 inhibition, active in MOLM13.
- **Reported IC₅₀ (FLT3‑ITD AML lines)**: ~5–50 nM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1 µM.

---

## 3. Sunitinib
- **Class/target**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, VEGFR).
- **Rationale**: Shown to inhibit proliferation and induce apoptosis in FLT3‑ITD AML cell lines.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in AML cells**: Often ~20–200 nM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3 µM.

---

## 4. Dasatinib
- **Class/target**: SRC/ABL family, also inhibits some KIT/FLT3 signaling.
- **Rationale**: Preclinical AML data; can affect downstream survival pathways.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in AML lines**: Typically ~10–300 nM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1 µM.

---

## 5. Imatinib
- **Class/target**: BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR.
- **Rationale**: Some AML subsets with KIT or PDGFR alterations respond; MOLM13 is not classic imatinib‑sensitive but it’s a relevant negative/low‑effect control or for combination work.
- **Expected IC₅₀**: Often >1–5 µM in non‑BCR‑ABL AML lines.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100 µM.

---

## 6. Nilotinib
- **Class/target**: BCR‑ABL/KIT inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Stronger KIT inhibition than imatinib; some AML subsets show sensitivity.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in KIT‑dependent lines**: ~10–100 nM, but AML heterogenous.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3 µM.

---

## 7. Crizotinib
- **Class/target**: ALK/MET/ROS1 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Reported activity against certain AML subsets harboring ALK or MET pathway alterations; can be tested broadly for repurposing.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in various leukemia lines**: ~0.1–2 µM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

---

## 8. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Class/target**: BCL‑2 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Now standard in AML combinations; highly active in many AML cell lines including MOLM13.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in AML lines**: ~1–50 nM in sensitive lines.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100 nM.

---

## 9. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)
- **Class/target**: BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Broader anti‑apoptotic blockade; active in AML cell panels.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: Often ~0.1–1 µM in myeloid lines.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3, 10 µM.

---

## 10. Ruxolitinib
- **Class/target**: JAK1/2 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: JAK/STAT signaling is frequently activated in AML microenvironment; some cell lines show modest sensitivity.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: Typically ~0.5–5 µM in myeloid cells.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30 µM.

---

## 11. Tofacitinib
- **Class/target**: JAK1/3 inhibitor (approved for RA).
- **Rationale**: Another way to modulate JAK/STAT; of interest for repurposing.
- **Expected IC₅₀ in AML lines**: Likely in low‑micromolar range.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30 µM.

---

## 12. Ibrutinib
- **Class/target**: BTK inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: BTK signaling contributes to survival in some AML subsets; preclinical reports of modest AML activity and synergy with other agents.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in myeloid lines**: Often ~1–10 µM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30 µM.

---

## 13. Everolimus
- **Class/target**: mTORC1 inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway is active in AML; mTOR blockade reduces proliferation.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in AML lines**: ~1–20 nM for mTOR signaling; growth inhibition often in ~10–100 nM range.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100 nM, 300 nM.

---

## 14. Temsirolimus
- **Class/target**: mTOR inhibitor (rapalog).
- **Rationale**: Similar to everolimus; early clinical AML trials conducted.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: Growth inhibition often in tens of nM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100 nM, 300 nM, 1 µM.

---

## 15. Pimozide
- **Class/target**: Antipsychotic; functionally inhibits STAT5 and other signaling nodes.
- **Rationale**: Several reports of pimozide suppressing STAT5‑driven leukemias and AML cell lines.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in leukemia cells**: Often ~1–10 µM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30 µM.

---

## 16. Disulfiram (+/‑ copper)
- **Class/target**: ALDH inhibitor; forms copper complexes that induce ROS and proteasome inhibition.
- **Rationale**: AML, including stem/progenitor cells, can be sensitive to ALDH and redox disruption; synergy with copper is common.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in AML lines**: Typically ~0.1–1 µM (with copper).
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 10, 30, 100, 300 nM, 1, 3 µM.  
  - If using Cu²⁺ (e.g., CuCl₂ 0.5–1 µM), keep conditions consistent.

---

## 17. Metformin
- **Class/target**: AMPK activator, mitochondrial complex I inhibitor (antidiabetic).
- **Rationale**: AML cells can be sensitive to metabolic disruption; metformin shows anti‑leukemic effects at higher concentrations in vitro.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in leukemia lines**: Often 1–10 mM (millimolar).
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30 mM (watch for osmolarity and pH; use appropriate controls).

---

## 18. Chloroquine / Hydroxychloroquine
- **Class/target**: Autophagy inhibitor, lysosomotropic agent.
- **Rationale**: Autophagy supports AML survival; chloroquine derivatives can sensitize AML cells.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in AML lines**: Typically 5–30 µM for proliferation.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 1, 3, 10, 30, 50, 100 µM.

---

## 19. Valproic acid
- **Class/target**: HDAC inhibitor (broad), antiepileptic.
- **Rationale**: Differentiation and apoptosis induction in AML; used clinically in low‑intensity regimens.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in AML lines**: Usually 0.5–3 mM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10 mM.

---

## 20. Bortezomib
- **Class/target**: Proteasome inhibitor.
- **Rationale**: Proteasome inhibition triggers apoptosis in AML cells; synergy with FLT3 inhibitors and HDAC inhibitors reported.
- **Reported IC₅₀ in AML lines**: Often ~5–50 nM.
- **Suggested range**:  
  - 0.5, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100 nM.

---

### Practical IC₅₀ Assay Notes (for MOLM13)

- **Cell density**: Commonly 3–5 × 10³ MOLM13 cells per well in 96‑well plates (optimize to stay in log‑phase over 72 h).  
- **Exposure time**: 72 h recommended for first pass; some TKIs give clear effects by 48 h.  
- **Readouts**:  
  - Primary: Cell viability (CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, or similar).  
  - Optional: Annexin V/PI for apoptosis at selected concentrations.  
- **Curve fitting**: Use 8–10 concentrations, 3–4‑fold serial dilutions, in triplicate wells. Fit a 4‑parameter logistic curve to determine IC₅₀.
