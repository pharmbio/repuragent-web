=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a concise, **hypothesis-generating** list of 20 drugs with published or strongly suggested activity against AML (often including FLT3-ITD–positive lines such as MOLM-13). For each, I give:

- **Typical in vitro IC50 range** in AML cells (approximate, from literature when available or inferred from similar models).
- A **reasonable test range** for a 72‑h proliferation/viability IC50 assay (e.g., CellTiter-Glo, MTT) on MOLM-13.
- **Main target / rationale** in AML.

These are starting points; you should optimize conditions (cell density, serum, exposure time, plate type) for your lab.

---

## 1. Midostaurin (PKC412)  
- **Class/Target**: Multikinase inhibitor, FLT3, PKC, KIT.  
- **Reported IC50(AML, FLT3-ITD)**: ~5–20 nM in MOLM-13 and MV4-11.  
- **Suggested test range**: 0.5 nM – 1 µM (1:3 or 1:5 dilutions).  
- **Rationale**: Approved for FLT3-mutated AML; strong activity in MOLM-13.

---

## 2. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)  
- **Class/Target**: FLT3/AXL inhibitor.  
- **IC50(FLT3-ITD lines)**: ~1–10 nM.  
- **Test range**: 0.1 nM – 500 nM.  
- **Rationale**: Approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3-mut AML; very potent in MOLM-13.

---

## 3. Quizartinib (AC220)  
- **Class/Target**: Highly selective FLT3 inhibitor.  
- **IC50(FLT3-ITD)**: ~1–10 nM in MOLM-13/MV4-11.  
- **Test range**: 0.1 nM – 300 nM.  
- **Rationale**: Potent FLT3 inhibition; benchmark compound in FLT3-ITD AML.

---

## 4. Sorafenib  
- **Class/Target**: Multikinase; FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR.  
- **IC50(AML-FLT3-ITD)**: ~1–50 nM often reported for MOLM‑13/MV4-11.  
- **Test range**: 0.5 nM – 5 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Off-label AML use; strong preclinical data in FLT3-ITD+ AML.

---

## 5. Dasatinib  
- **Class/Target**: BCR-ABL/SRC family, multiple kinases.  
- **IC50(AML cells)**: typically 10–100 nM.  
- **Test range**: 1 nM – 5 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Broad kinase inhibition including SRC, KIT; activity in various AML lines.

---

## 6. Imatinib  
- **Class/Target**: BCR-ABL, KIT, PDGFR.  
- **IC50(AML subsets, esp. KIT-driven)**: often 0.1–5 µM.  
- **Test range**: 0.1 µM – 50 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Useful in KIT-dependent or PDGFR-dependent AML contexts; lower expectation in FLT3-driven lines but worth profiling.

---

## 7. Venetoclax (ABT-199)  
- **Class/Target**: BCL2 inhibitor.  
- **IC50(AML lines)**: ~1–100 nM, depending on BCL2 dependence; MOLM‑13 often in low‑nM to tens of nM.  
- **Test range**: 0.5 nM – 2 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Approved for AML (in combination); strong mechanistic rationale in BCL2-high AML.

---

## 8. Idasanutlin (RG7388)  
- **Class/Target**: MDM2–p53 interaction inhibitor.  
- **IC50(AML with WT p53)**: ~0.1–1 µM common in vitro.  
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Stabilizes p53 and induces apoptosis in p53–wild type AML cell lines (including MOLM-13 if p53 wt).

---

## 9. Panobinostat (LBH589)  
- **Class/Target**: Pan-HDAC inhibitor.  
- **IC50(AML lines)**: ~10–100 nM in many studies.  
- **Test range**: 1 nM – 2 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Epigenetic modulation; synergy with FLT3/BCL2 inhibitors has been reported in FLT3-ITD AML.

---

## 10. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- **Class/Target**: HDAC inhibitor.  
- **IC50(AML)**: ~0.5–5 µM.  
- **Test range**: 0.1 µM – 30 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Widely studied; can induce differentiation and apoptosis in AML cells.

---

## 11. Decitabine  
- **Class/Target**: DNA methyltransferase inhibitor (hypomethylating agent).  
- **Effective in vitro concentrations**: ~0.1–1 µM for demethylation and cytotoxicity in AML lines.  
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM (fresh drug daily or consider medium changes due to instability).  
- **Rationale**: Approved for AML/MDS; alters epigenetic state, sensitizes to other agents.

---

## 12. Azacitidine  
- **Class/Target**: DNMT inhibitor.  
- **Effective in vitro concentrations**: ~0.1–5 µM.  
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 20 µM (similarly, watch for instability).  
- **Rationale**: Standard-of-care hypomethylating agent in AML; modulates differentiation/proliferation.

---

## 13. Ruxolitinib  
- **Class/Target**: JAK1/2 inhibitor.  
- **IC50(JAK-activated AML lines)**: often ~0.1–3 µM.  
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM.  
- **Rationale**: For JAK/STAT-driven signaling or cytokine-driven proliferation in AML.

---

## 14. Everolimus (RAD001)  
- **Class/Target**: mTORC1 inhibitor.  
- **IC50(AML proliferation)**: usually ~0.1–1 µM.  
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM.  
- **Rationale**: PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway frequently hyperactive in AML; can reduce AML growth and sensitize to other drugs.

---

## 15. Temsirolimus  
- **Class/Target**: mTOR inhibitor (rapalog).  
- **IC50(AML lines)**: ~0.1–2 µM.  
- **Test range**: 10 nM – 10 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Similar rationale to everolimus; has preclinical AML data.

---

## 16. Bortezomib  
- **Class/Target**: Proteasome inhibitor (26S).  
- **IC50(AML)**: ~5–50 nM in many AML cell lines.  
- **Test range**: 1 nM – 500 nM.  
- **Rationale**: Disrupts NF-κB, protein homeostasis; has shown apoptosis induction in AML.

---

## 17. Carfilzomib  
- **Class/Target**: Irreversible proteasome inhibitor.  
- **IC50(AML)**: typically ~5–50 nM.  
- **Test range**: 1 nM – 500 nM.  
- **Rationale**: More selective/profound proteasome inhibition; potent in multiple hematologic malignancies.

---

## 18. Chloroquine (or Hydroxychloroquine)  
- **Class/Target**: Autophagy inhibitor (lysosomal).  
- **Effective in vitro concentrations**: ~5–30 µM in AML cells to inhibit autophagy and impact viability.  
- **Test range**: 1 µM – 80 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Autophagy supports AML survival; often used in combination (e.g., with FLT3/BCL2 inhibitors) but can have standalone effects at higher doses.

---

## 19. Metformin  
- **Class/Target**: AMPK activator; inhibits mitochondrial complex I.  
- **Effective in vitro concentrations**: typically high, 1–20 mM for AML cells.  
- **Test range**: 0.1 mM – 30 mM.  
- **Rationale**: Alters cellular metabolism, can impair AML proliferation and leukemia stem cell function in some studies.

---

## 20. Thalidomide (or Lenalidomide as close analog)  
- **Class/Target**: IMiD; modulates cereblon–E3 ligase complex, affects IKZF1/3, cytokines, and microenvironment.  
- **IC50(AML lines)**: variable; often modest direct effects around 1–50 µM.  
- **Test range**: 0.5 µM – 100 µM.  
- **Rationale**: Approved in other hematologic cancers; in AML, most data show microenvironment and immune modulation but some direct anti‑proliferative effects in subsets.

---

### Practical notes for IC50 assays in MOLM-13

- **Cell density**: commonly 5–10 × 10³ cells/well (96‑well plate), 72 h exposure is typical.  
- **Dilution series**: ~8–12 points, 1:3 or 1:5 steps, in 0.1–0.2% DMSO max (keep constant across wells).  
- **Controls**:  
  - Vehicle-only (100% viability).  
  - Positive control inhibitor known to work in MOLM-13 (e.g., gilteritinib or quizartinib).  
- **Readout**: Luminescent ATP-based assays (CellTiter-Glo) usually give cleaner AML data than colorimetric MTT/XTT.
