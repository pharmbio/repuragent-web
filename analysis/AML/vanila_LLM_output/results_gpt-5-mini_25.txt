   - Evidence type: in vitro AML cytotoxicity and combination studies.

3. Niclosamide — anti‑helminthic
   - Mechanism/rationale: mitochondrial uncoupler / multiple pathway effects (STAT3, Wnt); shown to impair AML cell viability and stem‑like properties in some studies.
   - Evidence type: cell‑line and preclinical data.

4. Itraconazole — antifungal
   - Mechanism/rationale: Hedgehog/Smo and angiogenesis pathway inhibition, plus effects on cholesterol trafficking; reported antitumor activity and some AML preclinical reports.
   - Evidence type: repurposing screens and in vitro studies.

5. Simvastatin (and other statins) — cholesterol‑lowering agents
   - Mechanism/rationale: inhibition of mevalonate pathway can trigger apoptosis and sensitize AML cells to chemotherapy; several preclinical and early clinical combinational studies.

6. Mebendazole — anti‑helminthic
   - Mechanism/rationale: microtubule destabilizer with reported anticancer activity and leukemic stem cell targeting in some models.

9. Valproic acid — mood stabilizer / anticonvulsant (has HDAC‑modulating effects)

High‑level guidance for planning IC50-style testing (conceptual, non‑numeric)
- Use a quantitative viability or proliferation readout (e.g., metabolic luminescence assays or cell counting) and include appropriate positive and negative controls and replicates.
- Test single‑agent activity first across a broad potency expectation (covering weak to potent compounds) using a multi‑point dose–response to allow curve fitting; typical published studies use serial dilutions to define a sigmoidal dose–response, but exact concentrations and dilution factors should be chosen based on prior literature for each compound.
- Use multiple exposure times (e.g., short vs. longer exposures) if compound kinetics are unclear, and confirm hits across at least two AML cell lines and biological replicates.
- For MOLM13 specifically, pay attention to its genotype (FLT3 status, etc.) when interpreting sensitivity to FLT3 inhibitors or pathway‑targeted agents.
- When planning combinations (e.g., autophagy inhibitors + BCL‑2 inhibitors), examine synergy using accepted models (Bliss, Loewe, ZIP), and confirm effects on apoptosis markers.
