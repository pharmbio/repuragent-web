=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used / late‑stage drugs with evidence (or strong rationale) for repurposing in AML, particularly FLT3‑ITD–positive lines such as MOLM‑13. For each, I include:

- **Suggested IC50 assay range** (10‑point serial dilutions; DMSO ≤0.1–0.2%)  
- **Key rationale** (brief, with AML/MOLM13 relevance when known)  
- **Typical starting stock**: 10 mM in DMSO unless otherwise noted

For most small molecules, a standard first pass is **0.5 nM – 50 μM**. Where literature suggests the effective range, I narrow it.

---

## 1. Sorafenib
- **Class/Use**: Multikinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR); used in HCC, RCC.  
- **AML rationale**: Potent against FLT3‑ITD; strong growth inhibition in MOLM‑13.  
- **Assay range**:  
  - 10‑point, 1:3 serial dilution from **10 μM down to 5 nM**  
- Expect IC50 for MOLM‑13 roughly in the low‑nanomolar to low‑hundreds‑nM range.

---

## 2. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Class/Use**: FLT3, KIT, VEGFR2 inhibitor; AML drug but repurposable from lymphoma/solid tumors.  
- **AML rationale**: Approved for FLT3‑mut AML; MOLM‑13 is highly sensitive.  
- **Assay range**: **10 μM to 5 nM** (1:3 dilutions).

---

## 3. Sunitinib
- **Class/Use**: Multikinase inhibitor; used in RCC, GIST.  
- **AML rationale**: Inhibits FLT3 & c‑KIT, has anti‑proliferative activity in AML lines including MOLM‑13.  
- **Assay range**: **10 μM to 5 nM**.

---

## 4. Dasatinib
- **Class/Use**: SRC/ABL family TKI; used in CML, Ph+ ALL.  
- **AML rationale**: Inhibits SRC, KIT, and other kinases important in AML survival; reported effects in FLT3‑ITD AML cells.  
- **Assay range**: **5 μM to 2.5 nM** (start slightly lower to avoid off‑target toxicity).

---

## 5. Nilotinib
- **Class/Use**: BCR‑ABL TKI; used in CML.  
- **AML rationale**: Inhibits ABL/KIT, affects AML cells especially with KIT involvement; some reports of FLT3 activity.  
- **Assay range**: **10 μM to 5 nM**.

---

## 6. Imatinib
- **Class/Use**: BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR inhibitor; used in CML, GIST.  
- **AML rationale**: Effective mainly in KIT‑driven AML; useful broadly as a comparator or combination partner.  
- **Assay range**: **20 μM to 10 nM**.

---

## 7. Ponatinib
- **Class/Use**: Pan‑BCR‑ABL and multikinase inhibitor; used in resistant CML/ALL.  
- **AML rationale**: Potent FLT3 inhibitor including resistant mutants; strong activity in MOLM‑13 expected.  
- **Assay range**: **1 μM to 0.5 nM** (very potent; keep top dose lower).

---

## 8. Lestaurtinib (CEP‑701)
- **Class/Use**: Multi‑TKI originally developed for solid tumors.  
- **AML rationale**: FLT3 inhibitor; reported low‑nanomolar IC50 in FLT3‑ITD AML including MOLM‑13.  
- **Assay range**: **5 μM to 2.5 nM**.

---

## 9. Ruxolitinib
- **Class/Use**: JAK1/2 inhibitor; used in myelofibrosis, PV.  
- **AML rationale**: Targets JAK/STAT signaling; some AMLs depend on this axis, and combo with FLT3 inhibitors is of interest.  
- **Assay range**: **10 μM to 5 nM**.

---

## 10. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Class/Use**: BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved in CLL, AML (with HMAs).  
- **AML rationale**: Strong anti‑leukemic effect in multiple AML lines including MOLM‑13; induces apoptosis.  
- **Assay range**: **5 μM to 2.5 nM** (high potency; IC50 often <100 nM).

---

## 11. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)
- **Class/Use**: BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor; used in trials for solid tumors and hematologic malignancies.  
- **AML rationale**: Pro‑apoptotic in AML; broader BCL‑XL targeting can produce stronger in vitro effects.  
- **Assay range**: **10 μM to 5 nM**.

---

## 12. Thalidomide
- **Class/Use**: Immunomodulatory drug (IMiD); used in myeloma and complications of hematologic malignancy.  
- **AML rationale**: Anti‑angiogenic and immunomodulatory; direct effects on AML cells tend to be modest but measurable at higher μM.  
- **Assay range**: **100 μM to 50 nM** (be prepared for IC50 in mid‑ to high‑μM).

---

## 13. Lenalidomide
- **Class/Use**: IMiD; used in myeloma, MDS with del(5q).  
- **AML rationale**: Alters cereblon‑mediated degradation; some AML lines show decreased proliferation.  
- **Assay range**: **50 μM to 25 nM**.

---

## 14. Pomalidomide
- **Class/Use**: IMiD; used in relapsed myeloma.  
- **AML rationale**: More potent analog; can impact AML proliferation and differentiation in some models.  
- **Assay range**: **20 μM to 10 nM**.

---

## 15. Metformin
- **Class/Use**: AMPK activator; antidiabetic.  
- **AML rationale**: Inhibits mitochondrial complex I; can reduce proliferation and induce energetic stress in AML cells at millimolar levels.  
- **Assay range**: **20 mM to 10 μM**  
  - Prepare fresh in aqueous medium; start high because IC50 is often in low‑mM range.

---

## 16. Auranofin
- **Class/Use**: Gold complex; used in rheumatoid arthritis.  
- **AML rationale**: Inhibits thioredoxin reductase; causes ROS‑mediated apoptosis in AML lines including FLT3‑ITD.  
- **Assay range**: **10 μM to 5 nM**.

---

## 17. Disulfiram (+ copper)
- **Class/Use**: ALDH inhibitor; used for alcohol dependence.  
- **AML rationale**: Inhibits ALDH‑positive leukemic stem/progenitor cells; often potentiated by CuCl2.  
- **Assay range**:  
  - Disulfiram: **10 μM to 5 nM**  
  - CuCl2: co‑treat at **0.5–2 μM** (constant), based on literature.  
- Protect solutions from light; prepare fresh.

---

## 18. Valproic acid (sodium valproate)
- **Class/Use**: Antiepileptic; HDAC inhibitor.  
- **AML rationale**: Induces differentiation and apoptosis in AML, including FLT3‑ITD models; synergy with ATRA and demethylating agents.  
- **Assay range**: **5 mM to 2.5 μM**  
  - IC50 often in high‑hundreds‑μM to low‑mM.

---

## 19. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Class/Use**: HDAC inhibitor; used in CTCL.  
- **AML rationale**: Potent epigenetic modulator; induces apoptosis/differentiation in multiple AML lines.  
- **Assay range**: **10 μM to 5 nM** (IC50 typically low‑μM).

---

## 20. Chloroquine or Hydroxychloroquine
- **Class/Use**: Antimalarial; autophagy inhibitor.  
- **AML rationale**: Blocks autophagy; enhances stress and cell death in AML, especially in combination with TKIs or chemo.  
- **Assay range**:  
  - Chloroquine: **100 μM to 50 nM**  
  - Hydroxychloroquine: **200 μM to 100 nM**  
- IC50 often tens of μM in vitro.

---

### General experimental notes for an IC50 assay in MOLM‑13

- **Cell line**: MOLM‑13 (FLT3‑ITD+). Culture in RPMI‑1640 + 10–20% FBS, 1% pen‑strep; keep log‑phase.  
- **Seeding density**: ~5×10³–1×10⁴ cells/well in 96‑well plates (final volume 100 μL).  
- **Treatment time**: 48–72 h for proliferation/viability IC50 (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, WST‑1, MTS).  
- **Vehicle control**: Match highest DMSO used (typically ≤0.1–0.2%).  
- **Data**: Normalize to vehicle, fit a 4‑parameter logistic curve to calculate IC50.
