=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used (or late‑stage) drugs with evidence or strong rationale for repurposing in acute myeloid leukemia (AML), especially FLT3‑ITD–positive lines like MOLM‑13. For each, I include:

- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay test range**: a *starting* concentration series for in‑vitro proliferation/viability assays (e.g., 72‑hr CellTiter‑Glo) in MOLM‑13.  
- These ranges are based on reported in‑vitro potencies (when available) plus typical cell‑based assay practice (log‑scale dilutions, often 8–10 points). Always re‑optimize for your specific setting.

General note for all:  
- Use serial 3‑fold or 10‑fold dilutions across the stated range.  
- Include DMSO control at the highest vehicle concentration used.

---

## 1. Midostaurin  
- **Original use**: Multikinase/FLT3 inhibitor; approved for FLT3‑mut+ AML.  
- **Rationale**: Benchmark FLT3 inhibitor; strong activity in MOLM‑13.  
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**.

## 2. Sorafenib  
- **Original use**: Renal cell carcinoma, HCC, thyroid; multikinase inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Off‑label in FLT3‑ITD AML; potent in MOLM‑13.  
- **Test range**: **0.5 nM – 10 µM**.

## 3. Sunitinib  
- **Original use**: RCC, GIST; multikinase inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Inhibits FLT3 and related kinases; preclinical AML data.  
- **Test range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

## 4. Dasatinib  
- **Original use**: BCR‑ABL/Src inhibitor (CML, Ph+ ALL).  
- **Rationale**: Broader Src/ABL/Kit inhibition; anti‑leukemic activity in some AML models.  
- **Test range**: **0.5 nM – 5 µM**.

## 5. Nilotinib  
- **Original use**: BCR‑ABL inhibitor (CML).  
- **Rationale**: Inhibits some FLT3 and KIT mutants; activity shown in AML cells in vitro.  
- **Test range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

## 6. Ponatinib  
- **Original use**: Pan‑BCR‑ABL inhibitor (including T315I); multikinase inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Potent inhibitor of FLT3‑ITD and other AML‑relevant kinases; strong MOLM‑13 activity reported.  
- **Test range**: **0.05 nM – 1 µM**.

## 7. Ibrutinib  
- **Original use**: BTK inhibitor (CLL, MCL, etc.).  
- **Rationale**: Off‑target inhibition and signaling disruption in myeloid leukemias; some preclinical AML data.  
- **Test range**: **10 nM – 20 µM**.

## 8. Venetoclax  
- **Original use**: BCL‑2 inhibitor (CLL, AML in combination).  
- **Rationale**: Strong pro‑apoptotic effects in AML including MOLM‑13; standard of care in combos.  
- **Test range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**.

## 9. Ruxolitinib  
- **Original use**: JAK1/2 inhibitor (myelofibrosis, PV).  
- **Rationale**: JAK/STAT pathway relevant in subsets of AML; shown to reduce growth in some AML cell lines.  
- **Test range**: **10 nM – 20 µM**.

## 10. Everolimus  
- **Original use**: mTOR inhibitor (RCC, other solid tumors, transplantation).  
- **Rationale**: mTOR pathway important in AML cell survival; everolimus shows anti‑proliferative effects in AML in vitro.  
- **Test range**: **0.5 nM – 5 µM**.

## 11. Temsirolimus  
- **Original use**: mTOR inhibitor (RCC).  
- **Rationale**: Similar to everolimus; tested in AML cell lines and clinical trials.  
- **Test range**: **0.5 nM – 5 µM**.

## 12. Metformin  
- **Original use**: Type 2 diabetes (AMPK activator, mitochondrial complex I inhibitor).  
- **Rationale**: Metabolic stressor; reported anti‑leukemic effects at higher in‑vitro doses (relative to plasma levels).  
- **Test range**: **0.1 mM – 20 mM**.

## 13. Disulfiram (+ Cu²⁺)  
- **Original use**: Alcohol aversion therapy.  
- **Rationale**: ALDH inhibition and copper‑dependent proteasome/NF‑κB effects; preclinical AML activity, including leukemic stem cells.  
- **Test range**:  
  - Disulfiram: **10 nM – 10 µM**  
  - Copper (e.g., CuCl₂ or CuSO₄): fixed low micromolar (e.g., 0.5–1 µM) in all wells.

## 14. Chloroquine / Hydroxychloroquine  
- **Original use**: Antimalarial/antirheumatic; autophagy inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Target survival/autophagy in AML; synergy with other agents reported.  
- **Test range**: **0.5 µM – 50 µM**.

## 15. Celecoxib  
- **Original use**: COX‑2 inhibitor (anti‑inflammatory, pain).  
- **Rationale**: COX‑2–independent PI3K/Akt and ER stress effects; some anti‑leukemia evidence in vitro.  
- **Test range**: **1 µM – 80 µM**.

## 16. Valproic acid  
- **Original use**: Antiepileptic, mood stabilizer; HDAC inhibitor.  
- **Rationale**: Differentiation and growth inhibition in AML; used in clinical differentiation protocols.  
- **Test range**: **0.1 mM – 5 mM**.

## 17. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- **Original use**: HDAC inhibitor (CTCL).  
- **Rationale**: Potent HDACi with strong activity in AML lines including MOLM‑13; used in AML trials.  
- **Test range**: **10 nM – 10 µM**.

## 18. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide  
- **Original use**: Immunomodulatory drugs (MM, MDS‑del(5q)).  
- **Rationale**: Cereblon‑mediated degradation of IKZF1/3 and immune modulation; modest direct AML cytotoxicity but interesting in combinations.  
- **Test range**:
  - Thalidomide: **0.5 µM – 100 µM**  
  - Lenalidomide: **0.1 µM – 30 µM**.

## 19. Auranofin  
- **Original use**: Oral gold compound for rheumatoid arthritis.  
- **Rationale**: Thioredoxin reductase inhibitor; induces ROS and apoptosis in AML cell lines (including FLT3‑ITD); strong preclinical data.  
- **Test range**: **5 nM – 5 µM**.

## 20. Leflunomide / Teriflunomide  
- **Original use**: Immunosuppressant (RA; MS for teriflunomide).  
- **Rationale**: DHODH inhibition; blocks de novo pyrimidine synthesis and differentiation/proliferation in AML models.  
- **Test range**:
  - Teriflunomide: **0.5 µM – 100 µM**  
  - If using leflunomide itself, expect to need mid‑ to high‑µM due to in‑vitro conversion.

### Practical assay notes for MOLM‑13

- **Exposure time**: commonly 48–72 h for proliferation/viability readouts.  
- **Readouts**:  
  - ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or  
  - Metabolic (MTS/XTT) plus confirm by annexin V/PI for apoptosis.  
- **Plate layout**: 8–10‑point serial dilutions (e.g., 1:3) in triplicate for each drug; include medium‑only, DMSO vehicle, and positive‑control inhibitor (e.g., sorafenib or midostaurin).

If you tell me what is already available in your lab (kinase inhibitors vs metabolic, HDAC, etc.), I can narrow this to a shorter, more focused panel with specific dilution schemes.
