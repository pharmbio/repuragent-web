## How to get *assay-ready* IC50 concentration ranges safely (recommended)
Because IC50s vary dramatically with **media**, **serum**, **cell density**, **assay readout (CellTiter‑Glo vs resazurin)**, and **exposure time (24/48/72 h)**, the most accurate way to set test concentrations is to pull *MOLM13-specific* dose–response data from large pharmacogenomic screens and then design your dilution series around those values.

Here are the best sources to query (all include MOLM13 in many cases):
- **DepMap PRISM Repurposing** (Broad): viability AUC/IC50-like metrics for many approved drugs.  
- **CTRP (Cancer Therapeutics Response Portal)**: many compounds with dose–response across lines.  
- **GDSC (Genomics of Drug Sensitivity in Cancer)**: curated IC50s for kinase inhibitors and others.  
- **ChEMBL / PubChem BioAssay**: literature-extracted potency values.  
- Primary papers: search “MOLM13 IC50 [drug]” and check exposure time.
