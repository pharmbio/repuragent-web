=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a curated list of 20 clinically used or late‑stage drugs with published evidence of activity against AML (often including FLT3‑ITD+ lines like MOLM‑13). For each, I summarize key rationale and give a **reasonable starting test concentration range** for an in‑vitro IC50 assay on MOLM13 or similar AML cells.

Use these as **screening ranges**, then refine around hits to get precise IC50 values. All concentrations refer to **final concentrations in culture medium** (DMSO ≤0.1%).

---

## General assay guidance (brief)

- Cell model: MOLM13 (FLT3‑ITD+), 48–72 h proliferation/viability assay (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, resazurin, MTT).
- Plating: 5–10 × 10⁴ cells/well in 96‑well plates (suspension culture).
- Dose range: 8–10‑point half‑log or log dilution series spanning from **sub‑nanomolar to tens of micromolar**.
- Vehicle control: 0.1% DMSO.
- At least three biological replicates per drug.

---

## 1. Sorafenib  
**Type:** Multikinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR, FLT3).  
**Rationale:** Strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML; clinical reports in relapsed AML.  
**Suggested range for IC50 assay:**  
- 0.5 nM – 10 µM (e.g., 0.5, 1.5, 5, 15, 50, 150, 500, 1500, 5000, 10000 nM).  
Published IC50 in FLT3‑ITD lines is typically in low‑nanomolar range.

---

## 2. Sunitinib  
**Type:** Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, VEGFR, PDGFR).  
**Rationale:** Inhibits FLT3‑ITD AML lines including MOLM13.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 1 nM – 20 µM.

---

## 3. Midostaurin (PKC412)  
**Type:** FLT3 and multi‑kinase inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Approved for FLT3‑mutated AML; nanomolar potency on FLT3‑ITD cell lines.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 0.5 nM – 10 µM.

---

## 4. Dasatinib  
**Type:** SRC/ABL inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Shows antiproliferative effects in subsets of AML through SRC family and other kinases.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 1 nM – 20 µM.  

---

## 5. Nilotinib  
**Type:** BCR‑ABL/ABL inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Off‑target activity in some AML models; can be tested as repurposing candidate.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 1 nM – 20 µM.

---

## 6. Imatinib  
**Type:** BCR‑ABL/KIT/PDGFR inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Limited but measurable activity in AML with KIT or PDGFR dependence; often used as control TKI.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 10 nM – 30 µM.

---

## 7. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)  
**Type:** BCL‑2 inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Approved for AML (with HMA or LDAC); robust activity in many AML lines including MOLM13.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 0.1 nM – 5 µM (fine spacing in 0.1–100 nM is useful).

---

## 8. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)  
**Type:** BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Induces apoptosis in AML cell lines; often more toxic due to BCL‑XL but good in vitro tool.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 0.5 nM – 10 µM.

---

## 9. Ruxolitinib  
**Type:** JAK1/2 inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** JAK/STAT signaling contributes to some AML subtypes; synergy with other agents reported.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 10 nM – 20 µM.

---

## 10. Tofacitinib  
**Type:** JAK1/3 inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Modulates cytokine‑dependent signaling; repurposing interest in hematologic malignancies.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 10 nM – 30 µM.

---

## 11. Panobinostat (LBH589)  
**Type:** Pan‑HDAC inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Strong antiproliferative and pro‑apoptotic effects in AML cell lines, including FLT3‑ITD models.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 0.5 nM – 5 µM (many AML IC50 values are in low‑nanomolar range).

---

## 12. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
**Type:** HDAC inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Broad antileukemic activity; tested in AML clinical trials and in combinations.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 50 nM – 20 µM.

---

## 13. Decitabine  
**Type:** DNA hypomethylating agent.  
**Rationale:** Approved for AML/MDS; in vitro, effects often seen at micromolar levels with longer exposure.  
**Suggested range (for 72 h assay):**  
- 0.1 µM – 20 µM.  

---

## 14. Azacitidine  
**Type:** DNA/RNA hypomethylating agent.  
**Rationale:** Standard therapy in older AML patients; in vitro cytostatic/cytotoxic effects.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 0.5 µM – 50 µM.

---

## 15. Bortezomib  
**Type:** Proteasome inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Induces apoptosis in AML cell lines including FLT3‑ITD; strong preclinical evidence.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 0.5 nM – 1 µM.

---

## 16. Carfilzomib  
**Type:** Irreversible proteasome inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Highly potent; induces apoptosis in AML preclinical models.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 0.1 nM – 1 µM.

---

## 17. Auranofin  
**Type:** Gold compound; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Reported selective toxicity in AML (ROS and redox imbalance); repurposing candidate in leukemia.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 10 nM – 20 µM.

---

## 18. Disulfiram (± copper supplementation in vitro)  
**Type:** ALDH inhibitor; forms Cu(DDC)₂ complex with proteasome/ROS effects.  
**Rationale:** Shown to kill AML stem/progenitor cells in vitro; repurposing interest.  
**Suggested range (drug alone):**  
- 0.1 µM – 30 µM.  
If adding Cu²⁺ (e.g., CuCl₂ 0.5–2 µM), keep copper low and constant across wells.

---

## 19. Metformin  
**Type:** AMPK activator / complex I modulator.  
**Rationale:** Anti‑leukemic effects via metabolic stress and mTOR inhibition; usually requires higher micromolar–millimolar levels in vitro.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 0.1 mM – 20 mM (100 µM – 20,000 µM).  
Because of solubility and off‑target issues at very high doses, focus on 0.5–10 mM.

---

## 20. Chloroquine or Hydroxychloroquine  
**Type:** Lysosomotropic autophagy inhibitor.  
**Rationale:** Inhibits autophagy; sensitizes AML cells to other agents and has moderate single‑agent effects at higher µM.  
**Suggested range:**  
- 1 µM – 50 µM.

---

### Practical design for your IC50 screen on MOLM13

For a single 96‑well plate per drug:

- 10 concentrations in duplicate + vehicle control (DMSO) + positive cytotoxic control (e.g., 1 µM staurosporine or 1 µM bortezomib).
- Use **log‑spaced dilutions** from the lower to the upper bound indicated above.
- 48 h and 72 h readouts can reveal time‑dependent effects (especially for hypomethylating agents).
