=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing, clinically used drugs with evidence of anti-AML activity (many with specific activity in FLT3-ITD–positive lines like MOLM-13). For each, I list a practical single-agent concentration range to screen in an IC50 viability/proliferation assay. Use a logarithmic dilution series that spans the full range to capture both highly sensitive and resistant responses; adjust based on solubility and observed curves.

- Sorafenib (multi-kinase; FLT3, RAF): Very active in FLT3-ITD AML (MOLM-13, MV4-11). Test 0.1 nM – 3 µM.
- Ponatinib (pan-ABL/FLT3): Potent vs FLT3-ITD and TKD mutants (including F691L). Test 0.01 nM – 1 µM.
- Sunitinib (multi-kinase; FLT3/KIT/VEGFR): Inhibits FLT3-ITD AML lines. Test 0.5 nM – 10 µM.
- Dasatinib (SRC/ABL family): Anti-proliferative in subsets of AML, including FLT3-ITD contexts. Test 0.5 nM – 10 µM.
- Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2): Variable single-agent AML activity; stronger in JAK/STAT–driven signaling; useful benchmark. Test 10 nM – 30 µM.
- Venetoclax (BCL-2): Many AML lines sensitive; potency varies by BCL2/MCL1 balance; include for MOLM-13. Test 0.05 nM – 5 µM.
- Selinexor (XPO1): Broad activity across AML cell lines, including MOLM-13. Test 1 nM – 10 µM.
- Panobinostat (HDAC): Potent epigenetic inhibitor; strong AML cytotoxicity. Test 0.1 nM – 1 µM.
- Vorinostat/SAHA (HDAC): AML activity across lines; MOLM-13 typically micromolar IC50. Test 10 nM – 30 µM.
- Belinostat (HDAC): Similar AML activity to vorinostat. Test 10 nM – 30 µM.
- Bortezomib (proteasome): Strong anti-AML effects including in FLT3-ITD lines. Test 0.05 nM – 1 µM.
- Carfilzomib (proteasome): Potent; complementary to bortezomib. Test 0.05 nM – 1 µM.
- Everolimus (mTORC1): Cytostatic/cytotoxic activity in AML; test broad due to context-dependence. Test 0.5 nM – 10 µM.
- Trametinib (MEK1/2): Inhibits MAPK-driven AML; some activity in MOLM-13; combine in panels. Test 0.1 nM – 10 µM.
- Auranofin (thioredoxin reductase inhibitor; anti-rheumatic): Consistent low-µM AML activity, including MOLM-13. Test 10 nM – 30 µM.
- Tigecycline (mitochondrial translation inhibitor; antibiotic): Targets leukemic stem/progenitor metabolism; AML IC50 often low µM. Test 0.1 µM – 50 µM.
- Niclosamide (OXPHOS/Wnt/STAT3 modulator; anthelmintic): AML lines often sub-µM sensitive; check solubility. Test 1 nM – 10 µM.
- Simvastatin (HMG‑CoA reductase): Mevalonate pathway dependence in AML; micromolar activity. Test 0.1 µM – 50 µM.
- Arsenic trioxide (APL drug; oxidative stress): Non-APL AML lines show µM-range sensitivity; include as a reference. Test 10 nM – 10 µM.
- Thioridazine (dopamine receptor antagonist; antipsychotic): Selective toxicity to leukemic vs normal HSPCs reported; AML IC50 in low–mid µM. Test 0.1 µM – 50 µM.

Practical notes
- MOLM-13 is FLT3-ITD; FLT3 inhibitors (sorafenib, ponatinib, sunitinib) and BCL2-pathway agents (venetoclax) are often the most potent single agents.
- Use a wide dynamic range initially (e.g., 8–10 points over 4–6 logs); refine around the observed midpoint for follow-ups.
- Account for protein binding and solubility (HDAC inhibitors, niclosamide, and statins can shift potency with serum).
- Include AML comparators like MV4-11 (FLT3-ITD) and a non-FLT3-ITD line to contextualize MOLM-13 sensitivity.
