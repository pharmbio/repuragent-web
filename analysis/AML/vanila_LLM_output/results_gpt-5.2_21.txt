## Notes that matter for making these “experimentally testable”
1. **Solubility/handling**
   - Niclosamide, mebendazole, pimozide can have solubility limits; you may need lower top doses or careful DMSO stocks.
2. **Prodrug/activation caveats**
   - Some drugs show weaker in‑vitro effects unless the active form is present or metabolism is mimicked (less of an issue for most above, but always verify).
3. **MOLM‑13 specifics**
   - MOLM‑13 is typically **FLT3‑ITD** and tends to show strong responses to FLT3 inhibitors (gilteritinib/quizartinib/midostaurin/sorafenib), but resistance can emerge depending on culture conditions.
4. **Choose your curve top dose strategically**
   - For targeted kinase inhibitors: a **top of 0.3–1 µM** often brackets IC50 cleanly while avoiding off‑target toxicity.
   - For “repurposed non‑oncology” agents: **10–30 µM** top is common for initial screening, but watch solubility and DMSO.
