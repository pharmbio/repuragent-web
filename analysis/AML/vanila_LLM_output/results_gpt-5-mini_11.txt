=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing drugs (approved or clinically‑developed) that have credible rationale or published preclinical evidence for activity against AML cell lines — many specifically against FLT3‑ITD lines such as MOLM13. For each I give a short rationale and an experimentally practical IC50 testing scheme (a recommended top concentration and a 10‑point 3‑fold serial dilution that will usually encompass the inhibitory range). I also give an approximate expected IC50 range (based on literature for AML/FLT3‑ITD cells or typical potency) so you can adjust the top concentration if you prefer a narrower window.

Important common assay notes (apply unless you have different SOPs):
- Use vehicle (DMSO) ≤0.5% final. Prepare drugs in DMSO except hydrophilic drugs as appropriate.  
- Standard readout: 72 hr viability/proliferation assay (CellTiter‑Glo or similar) for most small molecules. Some agents (e.g., decitabine/azacitidine) require longer exposure or repeat dosing (see notes).  
- Plate MOLM13 as suspension cells at ~5–20 × 10^3 cells/well in 96‑well plates (optimize for your assay). Use ≥3 technical replicates and biological repeats. Include positive control (e.g., known FLT3 inhibitor) and untreated/vehicle controls.  
- Use 10‑point 3‑fold serial dilutions unless otherwise noted.

1) Gilteritinib (FLT3/AXL inhibitor)  
- Rationale: approved FLT3 inhibitor, active in FLT3‑ITD cell lines (e.g., MOLM13).  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM (1.0 µM → ~0.5 pM).  
- Expected IC50 (MOLM13): ~1–50 nM.

2) Quizartinib (AC220, FLT3 inhibitor)  
- Rationale: highly potent FLT3 inhibitor, strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML lines.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 100 nM.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.5–10 nM.

3) Midostaurin (PKC/FLT3 multi‑kinase inhibitor)  
- Rationale: FLT3 inhibitor approved in AML (in combination therapy), active in FLT3‑ITD lines.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~50–300 nM.

4) Sorafenib (multikinase inhibitor)  
- Rationale: shows activity in FLT3‑ITD AML preclinically and in some clinical settings.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~10–300 nM (line dependent).

5) Venetoclax (BCL2 inhibitor)  
- Rationale: approved for AML combinations; many AML cell lines including some MOLM13 cultures are venetoclax‑sensitive or synergize with other agents.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~5–200 nM (often low nM in dependent lines).

6) Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor)  
- Rationale: proteasome inhibition can trigger apoptosis in AML; studied in trials/combinations.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~5–200 nM.

7) Decitabine (DNA hypomethylating agent)  
- Rationale: standard in AML therapy; in vitro effects require DNA incorporation and longer exposure.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 50 µM. For proliferation IC50 test, treat cells continuously 72–120 hr or use repeated daily dosing.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–5 µM (time‑dependent).

8) Azacitidine (DNA hypomethylating agent)  
- Rationale: as above (in vitro needs appropriate exposure).  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 50 µM; treat 72–120 hr or daily.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–10 µM (time‑dependent).

9) Vorinostat (SAHA, HDAC inhibitor)  
- Rationale: HDAC inhibition induces differentiation/apoptosis in AML; investigated in combinations.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 10 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.3–5 µM.

10) Panobinostat (HDAC inhibitor)  
- Rationale: potent HDAC inhibitor with AML activity preclinically and in combination trials.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~5–200 nM.

11) Dinaciclib (broad CDK inhibitor: CDK1/2/5/9)  
- Rationale: CDK9/other CDK inhibition downregulates anti‑apoptotic proteins (e.g., MCL1) and is active in AML.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–100 nM.

12) Palbociclib (CDK4/6 inhibitor)  
- Rationale: cell‑cycle targeting; activity in subset of AMLs and potential combinations.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 10 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.2–5 µM (often less potent in AML).

13) Trametinib (MEK1/2 inhibitor)  
- Rationale: MAPK pathway involvement in AML; MEK inhibition can be active in some AML contexts.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–50 nM (line dependent).

14) Everolimus (mTORC1 inhibitor)  
- Rationale: PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway is commonly activated in AML; mTOR inhibitors show activity in combinations.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 10 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~10–1000 nM.

15) Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2 inhibitor)  
- Rationale: JAK/STAT signaling contributes in some AML subsets; ruxolitinib active in certain contexts or combinations.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~50–500 nM.

16) 17‑AAG / Tanespimycin (HSP90 inhibitor)  
- Rationale: HSP90 inhibition destabilizes multiple oncogenic client proteins (FLT3, etc.).  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 1.0 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~50–500 nM.

17) Etoposide (topoisomerase II inhibitor)  
- Rationale: topoisomerase inhibitors have cytotoxic activity in AML (used clinically).  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 50 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~1–20 µM.

18) Simvastatin (HMG‑CoA reductase inhibitor)  
- Rationale: statins can induce apoptosis and potentiate other agents in AML in vitro; repurposing candidate.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 20 µM. (Simvastatin is a prodrug—use active form or confirm activation protocol.)  
- Expected IC50: ~5–20 µM (context dependent).

19) Niclosamide (antihelminthic)  
- Rationale: multiple anticancer mechanisms reported (Wnt/STAT/mitochondria); active in some leukemia models.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 10 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.5–5 µM.

20) Mebendazole (antihelminthic)  
- Rationale: reported microtubule‑targeting and anti‑leukemia activity in preclinical studies; good repurposing candidate.  
- Dilution scheme: 10‑point 3‑fold from 10 µM.  
- Expected IC50: ~0.5–5 µM.

Additional practical tips
- If you expect very potent activity (low nM), start the top concentration at 1 µM or 100 nM to avoid wasting compound and to ensure the assay captures the sigmoidal region. If activity is weak/unknown, start higher (10 µM or 50 µM for nucleoside/hypomethylating agents).  
- For BH3 mimetics (venetoclax), consider co‑testing with MCL1 inhibitors or measure BCL2 family dependencies (BH3 profiling) if you encounter resistance.  
- For decitabine/azacitidine, because they are S‑phase dependent and require incorporation, longer exposure (72–120 hr) and possibly daily medium/dose refresh gives more interpretable IC50s.  
- For combination studies (e.g., FLT3 inhibitor + venetoclax), run matrix dose–response or fixed‑ratio combination assays to look for synergy.

Caveats
- These are suggestions for in vitro screening; activity in MOLM13 in vitro does not imply clinical efficacy. Some listed agents are approved for other indications and some are investigational/clinical agents (use appropriate handling and approvals).  
- MOLM13 is FLT3‑ITD positive, so FLT3 inhibitors will often be the most potent. If you want a focused screen, prioritize FLT3 inhibitors, venetoclax, proteasome, CDK9 (dinaciclib), HDAC inhibitors, and HSP90 inhibitors.
