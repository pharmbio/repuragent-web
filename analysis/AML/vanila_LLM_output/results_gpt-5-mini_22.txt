General notes and suggestions
- For potent targeted drugs (expected nM IC50), starting at 1 µM (or 100 nM for extremely potent agents) with 10‑point 3‑fold dilutions should reliably include the IC50. For compounds known to require µM–tens‑µM (or mM) concentrations, start proportionally higher (10–20 µM or 20 mM for metformin).
- If you want explicit concentration series (exact µM or nM values for each of the 10 wells for any specific drug), tell me which drug(s) and I will expand the list into the exact concentrations.
- Solubility: adjust top concentration depending on solubility limits in your assay solvent (most kinase inhibitors/DMSO‑soluble drugs handle 1–10 µM; for drugs with poor solubility you may need lower top concentrations or co‑solvents). Keep final DMSO % constant across wells (commonly ≤0.5–1%).
- For agents that require cofactors (e.g., disulfiram with copper), test both with and without the cofactor as separate treatment arms.
- Combination testing: many of these drugs are most active in combinations (e.g., venetoclax + hypomethylating agent), so single‑agent IC50s can underestimate therapeutic potential. Consider follow‑up combination matrices after single‑agent profiling.
