=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Repurposing existing drugs for the treatment of acute myeloid leukemia (AML) is a promising approach that can accelerate the development of new therapies. The following is a list of drugs that have shown potential in preclinical or clinical studies for AML, including the MOLM-13 cell line. The suggested concentrations for IC50 assays are based on available data from literature and might require further optimization:

1. **Venetoclax**: Initially approved for chronic lymphocytic leukemia, this BCL-2 inhibitor shows activity in AML. Suggested concentration range for IC50: 10 nM - 1 µM.

2. **Sorafenib**: A multi-kinase inhibitor initially approved for renal and liver cancer, has shown potential in AML, particularly FLT3-ITD mutated cases. IC50 range: 1 nM - 10 µM.

3. **Quizartinib (AC220)**: A FLT3 inhibitor, used in relapsed/refractory AML with FLT3 mutations. IC50 range: 0.1 nM - 1 µM.

4. **Midostaurin (PKC412)**: A broad-spectrum kinase inhibitor effective in FLT3-mutated AML. IC50 range: 100 nM - 1 µM.

5. **Dasatinib**: A tyrosine kinase inhibitor initially used for CML, under investigation for AML. IC50 range: 1 nM - 10 µM.

6. **Ibrutinib**: A Bruton's tyrosine kinase inhibitor used in B-cell malignancies, shows potential in AML. IC50 range: 10 nM - 2 µM.

7. **Ruxolitinib**: A JAK1/JAK2 inhibitor, approved for myelofibrosis and polycythemia vera, investigated for AML. IC50 range: 100 nM - 10 µM.

8. **Lenalidomide**: Approved for multiple myeloma, studied for its effects on AML blast differentiation. IC50 range: 0.1 µM - 10 µM.

9. **Bortezomib**: A proteasome inhibitor used in multiple myeloma, studied in AML for cell cycle arrest. IC50 range: 1 nM - 100 nM.

10. **Arsenic Trioxide**: Used primarily in APL, it can induce apoptosis in AML cell lines. IC50 range: 0.1 µM - 2 µM.

11. **Metformin**: An antidiabetic agent with data suggesting anti-leukemic effects through AMPK activation. IC50 range: 1 mM - 5 mM.

12. **Valproic Acid**: A histone deacetylase (HDAC) inhibitor with potential differentiation-inducing effects in AML. IC50 range: 0.5 mM - 5 mM.

13. **Thalidomide**: Used for multiple myeloma, has immunomodulatory effects in AML. IC50 range: 1 µM - 10 µM.

14. **Aprepitant**: Originally an antiemetic, acts as an NK-1 receptor antagonist, inhibiting AML cell proliferation. IC50 range: 1 µM - 10 µM.

15. **Mosunetuzumab**: A bispecific antibody that engages T cells to kill AML cells. IC50 range (for cell-based assays, since it's an antibody): 0.1 nM - 1 µM.

16. **Nicotinamide**: A form of vitamin B3, shows potential in inducing differentiation of AML cells. IC50 range: 1 mM - 10 mM.

17. **Clofarabine**: A purine nucleoside analog for pediatric ALL, shows activity in AML. IC50 range: 10 nM - 1 µM.

18. **Disulfiram**: An alcohol aversion agent with potential anti-cancer properties through inhibition of aldehyde dehydrogenase. IC50 range: 0.5 µM - 5 µM.

19. **Clarithromycin**: Known for antibacterial activity, affecting AML cells through modulation of autophagy. IC50 range: 10 µM - 100 µM.

20. **Simvastatin**: HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor, shows inhibition of AML cell lines via cholesterol synthesis disruption. IC50 range: 1 µM - 10 µM.

For each drug, it is crucial to verify its effects on a specific AML cell line, such as MOLM13, and optimize the concentrations based on the type of assay and the cell line characteristics. Always consult peer-reviewed studies or conduct preliminary experiments to fine-tune the concentrations further.
