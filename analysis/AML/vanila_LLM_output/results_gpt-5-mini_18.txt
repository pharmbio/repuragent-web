=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing drugs (approved or clinical-stage) that have published or plausible activity against AML cell proliferation — with emphasis on agents known to be active against FLT3-ITD AML models (MOLM‑13 or related lines) or that have shown single‑agent activity in AML screens. For each drug I give (A) short rationale, (B) a recommended concentration series for an IC50/proliferation assay (a 10‑point 3‑fold dilution is suggested for most compounds), and (C) an approximate expected IC50 range you can use to center the curve. These are suggestions to generate experimentally testable curves; actual IC50 values depend on assay format, serum, exposure time and cell density.

Common assay notes (recommended):
- Use a 10‑point, 3‑fold serial dilution (C0, C0/3, C0/32, … C0/3^9) unless otherwise noted. Prepare concentrated stocks in DMSO (or water for hydrophilic drugs) and keep final DMSO ≤0.1% in wells. Include vehicle controls.
- Typical readout: 72‑hour viability/proliferation (CellTiter‑Glo or equivalent) for MOLM‑13 suspension cells. Seeding: ~20–50k cells/well in 96‑well plates (optimize).
- For drugs requiring cofactors (e.g., disulfiram), include the cofactor (Cu2+) at a low fixed concentration if testing that mechanism.
- For drugs needing very high concentrations (metformin), use a wider top concentration and be mindful of osmolarity and vehicle effects.

Practical tips for running the curves
- Centering the curve: choose the top concentration so the expected IC50 is ~1/3 to 1/30 of the top—this ensures you capture the slope. If you have no prior data, a broad top (e.g., 10 µM for many drugs, 30 µM for repurposed non‑oncotics, 20 mM for metformin) is conservative.
- Replicates: use at least triplicate wells per concentration and include vehicle-only and positive‑control cytotoxic compounds (e.g., staurosporine or benzethonium chloride) to verify assay performance.
- Exposure time: 72 h is commonly used for MOLM‑13 proliferation; some drugs (e.g., venetoclax) may show earlier effects (24–48 h) and others require longer.
- Solubility/toxicity: watch for precipitation at high concentrations; include a DMSO control at the highest vehicle concentration used.
