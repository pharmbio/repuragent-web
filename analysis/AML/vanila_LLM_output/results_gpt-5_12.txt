=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 approved, “existing” drugs with published anti-AML activity (many with activity in FLT3-ITD models such as MOLM13). For each, I list a practical screening range for an in vitro IC50 assay that spans 3–4 logs around typical reported potencies in serum-containing media. These are starting points—optimize based on solubility, protein binding, and observed curve shape.

FLT3/MAPK/mTOR and other kinase inhibitors (highly relevant to MOLM13, which is FLT3-ITD)
- Sorafenib (HCC/RCC; multikinase including FLT3): Suggested range 0.1 nM to 3 µM. FLT3-ITD AML often shows low-nM sensitivity.
- Ponatinib (CML/ALL; ABL/FLT3): 0.05 nM to 1 µM. Frequently low-nM in FLT3-ITD lines (including MOLM13).
- Cabozantinib (RCC/thyroid; MET/FLT3/VEGFR): 0.5 nM to 5 µM. Reported activity in FLT3-ITD AML at low–mid nM.
- Sunitinib (RCC; FLT3/VEGFR): 0.5 nM to 5 µM. Commonly tens of nM in FLT3-ITD AML.
- Regorafenib (CRC/HCC; multikinase incl. FLT3): 1 nM to 10 µM. Typically mid‑nM to sub‑µM in FLT3-ITD contexts.
- Dasatinib (CML/ALL; Src/ABL; some FLT3 inhibition): 1 nM to 10 µM. Often tens of nM to sub‑µM in AML.
- Osimertinib (NSCLC; EGFR TKI; also inhibits FLT3, incl. D835 mutants): 0.5 nM to 5 µM. Frequently ~10–100 nM in FLT3‑ITD AML (MOLM13/MV4‑11).
- Trametinib (melanoma; MEK1/2): 0.5 nM to 5 µM. RAS/MAPK‑driven AML lines show ~10–300 nM single‑agent sensitivity; often synergizes with FLT3 inhibitors.
- Everolimus (RCC; mTORC1 inhibitor): 1 nM to 10 µM. AML lines commonly show 10–300 nM growth inhibition, context‑dependent.
- Bortezomib (myeloma/MCL; proteasome inhibitor): 0.5 nM to 1 µM. Many AML lines respond in the 5–50 nM range.
- Selinexor (myeloma/DLBCL; XPO1 inhibitor): 1 nM to 5 µM. AML lines (incl. MOLM13) often 10–200 nM.

Redox/mitochondrial/lysosomal and other repurposed agents with AML activity
- Auranofin (rheumatoid arthritis; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor): 10 nM to 10 µM. AML IC50s frequently 0.3–3 µM.
- Tigecycline (antibiotic; mitochondrial translation inhibitor): 0.1 µM to 100 µM. AML blasts/lines often 1–20 µM; targets leukemia stem cell metabolism.
- Pimozide (antipsychotic; STAT5 pathway inhibition): 0.05 µM to 20 µM. FLT3‑ITD AML (STAT5‑dependent) typically 1–10 µM.
- Thioridazine (antipsychotic; LSC‑active, dopamine receptor antagonist/ion channel effects): 0.1 µM to 30 µM. Often low‑ to mid‑µM in AML.
- Mebendazole (anthelmintic; microtubules/other): 0.01 µM to 30 µM. AML IC50s commonly ~0.1–5 µM.
- Niclosamide (anthelmintic; OxPhos/Wnt/STAT): 0.01 µM to 10 µM. Many leukemia models ~0.1–2 µM.
- Mefloquine (antimalarial; lysosomal permeabilization): 0.1 µM to 50 µM. AML lines including LSCs often 1–10 µM.
- Nelfinavir (antiretroviral; proteostasis/ER stress/Akt): 0.5 µM to 50 µM. AML IC50s typically 3–15 µM.
- Ciclopirox (topical antifungal; iron chelation/EIF5A hypusination block): 0.1 µM to 50 µM. Leukemia IC50s commonly 0.5–5 µM.

Practical notes
- Use a 10–12 point, ~3-fold serial dilution across the suggested range; widen or shift if the curve does not bracket 0–100% effect.
- Upper bounds reflect common solubility/protein-binding limits in 10% FBS; keep vehicle constant across doses.
- For MOLM13 (FLT3-ITD), FLT3-active TKIs (ponatinib, sorafenib, cabozantinib, sunitinib, regorafenib, osimertinib) are expected to be among the most potent. Consider including a FLT3‑WT AML line (e.g., HL‑60) as a selectivity control.
