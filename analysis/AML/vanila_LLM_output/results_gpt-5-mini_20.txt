High‑level guidance for selecting concentration windows for IC50/dose–response testing
- Use the literature‑reported potency classes above to choose a concentration window that spans several orders of magnitude around expected potency. For example:
  - If expected potency is low nM, include concentrations that cover sub‑nanomolar to low micromolar.
  - If expected potency is in the μM range, include concentrations from low‑μM up to high‑μM (and beyond if literature suggests).
- Use a logarithmic dilution series (broader, multi‑point curves are more informative than few points).
- Include vehicle controls and multiple independent replicates; compare results across at least two AML cell lines (MOLM13 plus one or two non‑FLT3 lines) to distinguish FLT3‑dependent from general cytotoxic effects.
- Because some agents (e.g., disulfiram) require cofactors (copper) or metabolic activation, consult the literature for any adjuvants or assay conditions that influence activity.
