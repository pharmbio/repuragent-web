=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a **shortlist of 20 clinically used drugs** that have published activity against AML (many with data in MOLM-13 or very similar FLT3-ITD AML lines). I’ll also suggest **experimentally testable concentration ranges** for in‑vitro IC₅₀ determination in MOLM‑13 proliferation/viability assays (e.g., CellTiter-Glo, MTS, resazurin).

Notes and caveats first (important for design):
- Use **log10 serial dilutions** (e.g., 8–10 points) spanning at least **3–4 orders of magnitude** to bracket the expected IC₅₀.
- Suggested ranges are **starting ranges**, not exact IC₅₀ values. They’re based on typical in‑vitro potencies reported for AML or related cell lines, plus safety margins.
- Work in **0.1% DMSO or less** in all wells, including vehicle controls.
- For MOLM‑13, **48–72 h** drug exposure is standard for IC₅₀.

---

## 1. Sorafenib  
Multi-kinase inhibitor (including FLT3); widely reported active in FLT3-ITD AML, including MOLM‑13.

- Suggested IC₅₀ assay range: **0.5 nM – 10 μM**  
  (e.g., 0.5, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000, 3000, 10,000 nM)

---

## 2. Sunitinib  
Multi-kinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, VEGFR).

- Suggested range: **1 nM – 20 μM**

---

## 3. Dasatinib  
SRC/ABL family inhibitor with reported activity in some AML models.

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 10 μM**

---

## 4. Nilotinib  
Second-generation ABL inhibitor; some data for FLT3 and KIT-driven AML cells.

- Suggested range: **1 nM – 20 μM**

---

## 5. Imatinib  
First-generation BCR-ABL inhibitor; weaker but sometimes used as reference.

- Suggested range: **10 nM – 50 μM**

---

## 6. Midostaurin (PKC412)  
Clinically used FLT3 inhibitor; directly relevant in FLT3‑ITD AML (including MOLM‑13).

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 10 μM**

---

## 7. Quizartinib (AC220)  
Highly potent and selective FLT3 inhibitor; strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML cell lines.

- Suggested range: **0.1 nM – 1 μM**  
  (you can extend to 3–10 μM for a full curve)

---

## 8. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)  
Clinically approved FLT3 inhibitor; active in MOLM‑13 and other FLT3‑ITD lines.

- Suggested range: **0.1 nM – 2 μM**

---

## 9. Ponatinib  
Potent multi-kinase inhibitor (BCR-ABL, FLT3, FGFR, etc.) with strong in‑vitro AML activity.

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 5 μM**

---

## 10. Venetoclax (ABT-199)  
BCL-2 inhibitor; strong synergistic and single-agent activity in many AML lines including FLT3‑ITD models.

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 5 μM**

---

## 11. Ruxolitinib  
JAK1/2 inhibitor; can impair proliferation in cytokine-dependent AML/myeloproliferative contexts.

- Suggested range: **10 nM – 20 μM**

---

## 12. Trametinib  
MEK1/2 inhibitor; targets MAPK signaling often activated in AML.

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 10 μM**

---

## 13. Selumetinib (AZD6244)  
Another MEK1/2 inhibitor; widely used in hematologic signaling studies.

- Suggested range: **1 nM – 20 μM**

---

## 14. Everolimus (RAD001)  
mTOR inhibitor; PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway is frequently active in AML.

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 10 μM**

---

## 15. Temsirolimus  
mTOR inhibitor with clinical use in other cancers; reported anti‑proliferative effects in AML cells.

- Suggested range: **1 nM – 20 μM**

---

## 16. Bortezomib  
Proteasome inhibitor; induces apoptosis in AML cell lines, often at low‑nanomolar concentrations.

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 500 nM**  
  (may extend to 1–2 μM for full curve)

---

## 17. Carfilzomib  
Second-generation proteasome inhibitor; also cytotoxic against AML blasts and lines.

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 500 nM**  
  (extend to 1–2 μM if needed)

---

## 18. Panobinostat (LBH589)  
HDAC inhibitor; strong epigenetic modulation and cytotoxicity in AML cell lines.

- Suggested range: **0.5 nM – 5 μM**

---

## 19. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
Another HDAC inhibitor; broad anti‑proliferative activity in leukemia models.

- Suggested range: **5 nM – 30 μM**

---

## 20. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide (pick one for initial tests)  
Immunomodulatory drugs; reported anti‑leukemic and differentiation effects, especially in subsets of AML/MDS. Activity in MOLM‑13 may be modest but worth screening in a repurposing panel.

- For **lenalidomide**: **10 nM – 50 μM**  
- For **thalidomide**: **100 nM – 100 μM**

### Practical assay design (brief)

- **Plate type**: 96‑ or 384‑well, white or clear-bottom depending on readout.  
- **Cell density (MOLM‑13)**: typically **5–10 × 10³ cells/well (96‑well)** or **2–5 × 10³ cells/well (384‑well)** in 100 or 50 μL.  
- **Exposure time**: **48 h** (often enough) and/or **72 h** (to capture delayed responses).  
- **Controls**:
  - Vehicle (0.1% DMSO).
  - Positive control drug with known activity in MOLM‑13 (e.g., quizartinib, sorafenib, or venetoclax at ~100 nM–1 μM).
- **Readout**: luminescent ATP (CellTiter-Glo) or similar metabolic assay; normalize to vehicle.

If you want, I can prioritize a smaller panel (e.g., 6–8 drugs) optimized specifically for FLT3‑ITD/MCL1/BCL2 biology in MOLM‑13 and suggest combination pairs and synergy-testing layouts.
