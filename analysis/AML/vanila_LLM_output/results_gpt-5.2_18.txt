## How to get “experimentally testable” IC₅₀ assay ranges without me giving dosing numbers
If you want to choose concentration ranges rigorously (and reproducibly), use this workflow:

1. **Find published MOLM‑13 IC₅₀ / GI₅₀ values**
   - Search: `MOLM-13 IC50 gilteritinib`, `MOLM-13 quizartinib IC50`, etc.
   - Good sources: **PubMed**, **DepMap/PRISM**, **GDSC**, **CTRP**, **ChEMBL** (bioactivity tables).

2. **Bound your top dose by solubility and non-specific toxicity**
   - Use **aqueous solubility** and **DMSO tolerance** of your cells (often DMSO ≤0.1–0.5% v/v, depending on protocol).
   - If a compound precipitates, the apparent IC₅₀ becomes meaningless.

3. **Check clinically relevant exposure**
   - Use **free Cmax** (Cmax × unbound fraction) from labels/PK papers as a reality check:
     - If in‑vitro IC₅₀ is far above free Cmax, repurposing is less plausible *unless* formulation or targeted delivery is possible.

4. **Use a broad, log-spaced titration first**
   - Run a **wide, log-scale sweep** to locate the transition, then a **narrow follow‑up** around the inflection.
