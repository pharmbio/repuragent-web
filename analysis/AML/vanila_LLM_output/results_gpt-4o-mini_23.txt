### Experimental Considerations:

1. **Cell Line Validation:** Use the MOLM13 cell line for testing. Confirm that the cell line is authentically characterized, as variations in cell line strains can lead to different responses to drugs.

2. **Assay Conditions:** Establish your IC50 assays under consistent conditions — including cell density, time of exposure, and media composition.

3. **Dose-Response Curves:** Generate dose-response curves for each drug. Determine IC50 values by fitting the data to a suitable model, typically a sigmoidal dose-response curve.

4. **Replicates and Controls:** Include appropriate positive and negative controls for validation purposes, along with biological replicates to ensure reproducibility of results.

5. **Mechanistic Studies:** Following initial screening, consider conducting mechanistic studies to understand how these drugs affect AML cell pathways.

6. **Safety and Pharmacokinetics:** Consider the safety profiles and pharmacokinetics of these drugs, especially if there’s potential for combined therapies.

7. **Ethical Considerations and Approvals:** Ensure all experimental protocols are approved by your institution's ethical review board and comply with regulatory standards.

Before starting any experiments, consult the latest literature on the drugs listed and perform preliminary studies to assess their efficacy and safety in the specific context of AML.
