=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a **shortlist of 20 existing drugs** with literature support or mechanistic rationale for **repurposing against AML**, especially **FLT3-ITD–positive lines like MOLM‑13**. For each, I also give a **reasonable starting concentration range for in‑vitro IC₅₀ determinations** (e.g., 72‑h CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, or similar).

Use these as **screening ranges**, then narrow around the observed activity. Values are based on reported IC₅₀s (when available), known pharmacology, and typical in‑vitro testing windows for small molecules.

---

## General assay notes (for all compounds)

- Cell line: MOLM‑13 (FLT3‑ITD–mutant AML), ± a second line (e.g., MV4‑11 or THP‑1) for comparison.  
- Assay window: 72 h treatment is common for proliferation/viability.  
- Assay readout: ATP‑based or resazurin/MTS.  
- Concentration design:  
  - **Primary screen**: 8–10‑point half‑log dilution from the upper limit given below.  
  - **Vehicle control**: identical DMSO concentration (commonly ≤0.1%).  

Where “expected IC₅₀” is included, it’s approximate and line‑dependent; use it only to guide the range.

---

## 1. Nilotinib  
- Class: BCR‑ABL / KIT / PDGFR inhibitor; has activity against FLT3 in some studies.  
- Rationale: Multiple reports show anti‑proliferative effects in AML, including FLT3‑mutated contexts.  
- Suggested range: **0.001–10 µM**  
- Expect many AML cells to respond around **0.05–1 µM**.

## 2. Dasatinib  
- Class: SRC/ABL family TKI.  
- Rationale: Inhibits multiple kinases involved in AML signaling; reported activity in AML lines.  
- Range: **0.001–10 µM**  
- Expect IC₅₀ around **0.01–1 µM** in sensitive cells.

## 3. Sorafenib  
- Class: Multikinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR, FLT3).  
- Rationale: Widely reported activity against FLT3‑ITD AML lines (MOLM‑13, MV4‑11).  
- Range: **0.001–10 µM**  
- Many studies report IC₅₀ in FLT3‑ITD AML around **1–10 nM**; include low‑nM doses.

## 4. Sunitinib  
- Class: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, VEGFR, PDGFR, etc.).  
- Rationale: Known to inhibit FLT3 and suppress growth of FLT3‑mutant AML lines.  
- Range: **0.001–10 µM**  
- Likely IC₅₀ in **10–200 nM** range in sensitive AML lines.

## 5. Ponatinib  
- Class: Pan‑BCR‑ABL / multikinase inhibitor (including FLT3).  
- Rationale: Strong FLT3‑ITD inhibition; potent in MOLM‑13 and MV4‑11.  
- Range: **0.0001–1 µM**  
- Expected IC₅₀ for FLT3‑ITD AML often **1–20 nM**; include sub‑nM to 100 nM.

## 6. Imatinib  
- Class: BCR‑ABL / KIT / PDGFR inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Less active than newer TKIs but may inhibit KIT/PDGFR‑driven subsets; useful comparator.  
- Range: **0.01–50 µM**  
- AML responses are often weaker; expect IC₅₀ in the **5–20 µM** region if any.

## 7. Midostaurin  
- Class: Multikinase inhibitor; clinically used in FLT3‑mut AML.  
- Rationale: Established activity in FLT3‑ITD AML; MOLM‑13 is frequently used in midostaurin studies.  
- Range: **0.001–10 µM**  
- Many FLT3‑ITD lines show IC₅₀ **~10–100 nM**.

## 8. Crenolanib  
- Class: FLT3 inhibitor (type I).  
- Rationale: Potent FLT3 inhibitor with strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML lines including MOLM‑13.  
- Range: **0.0003–3 µM**  
- Often reported IC₅₀ **< 50 nM**; include 0.3–30 nM data points.

## 9. Quizartinib (AC220)  
- Class: FLT3 inhibitor (type II).  
- Rationale: Very potent vs FLT3‑ITD; MOLM‑13 is a standard sensitive line.  
- Range: **0.0001–1 µM**  
- Many reports show IC₅₀ in **1–10 nM** range.

## 10. Ibrutinib  
- Class: BTK inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Off‑target inhibition of other kinases and signaling mediators; some AML lines show sensitivity.  
- Range: **0.01–20 µM**  
- Expect IC₅₀ (if present) usually **1–10 µM** in AML.

## 11. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)  
- Class: BCL‑2 inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Very active in AML (including FLT3‑ITD); MOLM‑13 is typically highly sensitive.  
- Range: **0.0003–3 µM**  
- Expected IC₅₀ often **1–50 nM** in BCL‑2–dependent AML lines.

## 12. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)  
- Class: BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Pre‑venetoclax BH3 mimetic; shows anti‑leukemic activity in several AML lines.  
- Range: **0.001–10 µM**  
- AML IC₅₀ frequently **10–200 nM** in sensitive cells.

## 13. Disulfiram (± copper)  
- Class: ALDH inhibitor; proteasome‑related and NF‑κB activity when complexed with Cu²⁺.  
- Rationale: Multiple reports of anti‑leukemic effects; AML stem‑cell targeting proposed.  
- Range (drug): **0.05–5 µM** disulfiram  
- If using Cu²⁺ supplementation: 0.5–2 µM CuCl₂; test combination matrix.  
- Expect IC₅₀ roughly **0.1–2 µM** in responsive AML lines.

## 14. Metformin  
- Class: Antidiabetic; AMPK activator, mitochondrial complex I modulator.  
- Rationale: Reported anti‑proliferative effects in AML via metabolic stress, though often at higher doses.  
- Range: **0.1–20 mM**  
- IC₅₀ typically in the **1–10 mM** range in vitro.

## 15. Chloroquine / Hydroxychloroquine  
- Class: Antimalarial; autophagy inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Autophagy blockade can reduce AML cell survival; used alone or in combination.  
- Range: **1–50 µM**  
- AML IC₅₀ often **10–30 µM** in vitro.

## 16. Atorvastatin (or Simvastatin)  
- Class: HMG‑CoA reductase inhibitor (statins).  
- Rationale: Disrupts prenylation and membrane signaling; several statins show AML cell growth inhibition.  
- Range: **0.1–50 µM**  
- IC₅₀ often **1–10 µM** for simvastatin/atorvastatin in AML lines.

## 17. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide  
- Class: Immunomodulatory drugs (IMiDs).  
- Rationale: Complex immunomodulatory and direct anti‑leukemic actions; data in AML is mixed but present.  
- Range: **0.1–50 µM**  
- Direct IC₅₀ in AML lines, if present, usually **5–30 µM**.

## 18. Bortezomib  
- Class: Proteasome inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Induces apoptosis in AML cells via proteasome inhibition and NF‑κB pathway blockade.  
- Range: **0.0003–0.3 µM**  
- AML IC₅₀ frequently **5–50 nM**.

## 19. Carfilzomib  
- Class: Irreversible proteasome inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Potent apoptosis induction in AML cell lines, including FLT3‑mutant subsets.  
- Range: **0.0001–0.3 µM**  
- IC₅₀ often **5–30 nM**.

## 20. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- Class: HDAC inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Epigenetic modulation; induces apoptosis and differentiation in multiple AML lines (including MOLM‑13 in many studies).  
- Range: **0.01–10 µM**  
- Common IC₅₀ range **0.5–5 µM**.

### Practical experimental design suggestion

For each drug, you can standardize an initial 8‑point, 3‑fold dilution series, for example (adjust starting point per ranges above):

- Example for potent TKIs (e.g., quizartinib, ponatinib):  
  100 nM → 12.3 nM → 1.5 nM → 0.19 nM, etc., plus one higher concentration (300 nM–1 µM).
- Example for weaker agents (e.g., metformin, statins):  
  20 mM → 6.7 mM → 2.2 mM → 0.74 mM, etc.
