=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used / late‑stage drugs with published or strong preclinical evidence of activity in AML, many with data in FLT3‑ITD–positive lines such as MOLM‑13. For each, I give:

- **Primary target / rationale for AML**
- **Example potency range (IC50) in AML lines** where reported or reasonably inferred from close analogs
- **Suggested test concentration range for a 72‑h MOLM‑13 IC50 assay** (in vitro)

**Important notes**

- All concentrations are for *in vitro* cell viability/proliferation assays (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, resazurin, etc.).
- Ranges are chosen to span ≥2–3 logs around the expected IC50 to allow good curve fitting.
- Always verify solubility (DMSO stock), light sensitivity, and stability in your specific medium.
- Maintain final DMSO ≤0.1–0.2%.

---

## 1. Sorafenib
- **Type/target**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR); used in solid tumors but widely tested off‑label in FLT3‑ITD AML.
- **Reported AML activity**: IC50 in FLT3‑ITD lines (e.g., MOLM‑13, MV4‑11) typically in the **5–50 nM** range for viability.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.5 nM – 10 µM** (e.g., 0.5, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300 nM; 1, 3, 10 µM).

## 2. Sunitinib
- **Type/target**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, VEGFR, PDGFR).
- **Reported AML activity**: Low‑ to mid‑nanomolar IC50 in FLT3‑ITD AML.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 20 µM**.

## 3. Dasatinib
- **Type/target**: SRC/ABL family inhibitor; approved for CML/Ph+ ALL; SRC, LYN, KIT relevant in AML.
- **Reported AML activity**: Often **10–100 nM** in sensitive AML lines.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

## 4. Nilotinib
- **Type/target**: BCR‑ABL / KIT inhibitor; some activity in KIT‑ or PDGFR‑dependent AML subsets.
- **Reported AML activity**: Usually **0.1–5 µM** depending on mutational context.
- **Suggested assay range**: **10 nM – 30 µM**.

## 5. Imatinib
- **Type/target**: BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR inhibitor.
- **Rationale in AML**: Effective in KIT‑mutant core‑binding‑factor AML and PDGFR‑rearranged AML; less potent in FLT3‑driven cells, but useful as a benchmark.
- **Reported AML activity**: **1–10 µM** in responsive subsets.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 – 50 µM**.

## 6. Ponatinib
- **Type/target**: Pan‑BCR‑ABL inhibitor (incl. T315I), also potently inhibits FLT3 and KIT.
- **Reported AML activity**: Very potent in FLT3‑ITD AML (MOLM‑13, MV4‑11) with IC50 ~**1–10 nM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**.

## 7. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Type/target**: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR); approved for FLT3‑mutant AML.
- **Reported AML activity**: FLT3‑ITD cell IC50 typically **10–100 nM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

## 8. Quizartinib (AC220)
- **Type/target**: Highly selective FLT3 inhibitor; approved/late‑stage in FLT3‑ITD AML.
- **Reported AML activity**: IC50 in MOLM‑13 / MV4‑11 commonly **1–10 nM** for proliferation.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 nM – 1 µM**.

## 9. Gilteritinib
- **Type/target**: FLT3/AXL inhibitor; approved in relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mutant AML.
- **Reported AML activity**: IC50 often **1–20 nM** in FLT3‑ITD AML lines.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**.

## 10. Crenolanib
- **Type/target**: Type I FLT3 inhibitor; active on ITD and many TKD mutations.
- **Reported AML activity**: **~1–20 nM** in FLT3‑ITD lines.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 nM – 3 µM**.

## 11. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Type/target**: BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved in AML in combination regimens.
- **Reported AML activity**: IC50 in AML cell lines including MOLM‑13 often **10–100 nM** (BCL‑2–dependent lines).
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

## 12. Ruxolitinib
- **Type/target**: JAK1/2 inhibitor; approved for myelofibrosis, polycythemia vera.
- **Rationale in AML**: JAK/STAT signaling contributes to survival/proliferation in subsets; can sensitize to other agents.
- **Reported AML activity**: Typically **0.5–5 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **50 nM – 30 µM**.

## 13. Idelalisib
- **Type/target**: PI3Kδ inhibitor; approved for B‑cell malignancies.
- **Rationale in AML**: PI3K/AKT pathway is frequently hyperactive; δ isoform contributes to survival in some AML lines.
- **Reported AML activity**: ~**1–10 µM** in myeloid lines.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 – 50 µM**.

## 14. Ibrutinib
- **Type/target**: BTK inhibitor; approved for CLL/MCL; also hits TEC family kinases.
- **Rationale in AML**: BTK is expressed and can contribute to AML signaling, particularly in monocytic phenotypes; combinatorial potential.
- **Reported AML activity**: Typically **1–10 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 – 50 µM**.

## 15. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Type/target**: HDAC inhibitor; approved for CTCL.
- **Reported AML activity**: AML IC50 values generally **0.5–2 µM** for viability.
- **Suggested assay range**: **50 nM – 20 µM**.

## 16. Panobinostat
- **Type/target**: Pan‑HDAC inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma.
- **Reported AML activity**: Very potent; IC50 often **5–50 nM** in AML lines.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 1 µM**.

## 17. Azacitidine
- **Type/target**: DNA methyltransferase inhibitor (hypomethylating agent); approved for MDS/AML.
- **Reported AML activity**: Requires longer exposure; in 72‑h assays IC50 often **1–10 µM**, but demethylation biology is more complex than classical cytotoxicity.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 – 50 µM** (consider 96–120 h exposure if feasible).

## 18. Decitabine
- **Type/target**: DNMT inhibitor; approved for MDS/AML.
- **Reported AML activity**: Similar considerations as azacitidine; IC50 usually **0.1–5 µM** in prolonged exposures.
- **Suggested assay range**: **10 nM – 20 µM** (longer exposure preferable).

## 19. Bortezomib
- **Type/target**: Proteasome inhibitor; approved for myeloma and MCL.
- **Reported AML activity**: AML cell IC50 typically **5–50 nM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 1 µM**.

## 20. Carfilzomib
- **Type/target**: Irreversible proteasome inhibitor.
- **Reported AML activity**: IC50 usually **5–50 nM** in sensitive AML cell lines.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 1 µM**.

### Practical IC50 assay considerations for MOLM‑13

- **Exposure time**: 72 h is standard for proliferation/viability readouts; for hypomethylating agents you may want ≥96 h.
- **Cell density**: Common starting point is 5×10³–1×10⁴ cells per well in 96‑well plates to stay in log‑phase growth.
- **Readouts**: ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo), resazurin (alamarBlue), or MTS/WST‑1 are widely used.
- **Curve fitting**: Use at least 8–10 concentrations per drug, spaced ~0.5‑log apart, and fit a 4‑parameter logistic curve.
