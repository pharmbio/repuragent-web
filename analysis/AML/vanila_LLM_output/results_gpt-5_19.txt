=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing, repurposable drugs with preclinical evidence of antiproliferative activity in AML cell lines (including FLT3-ITD–positive MOLM-13), plus practical concentration ranges to span likely IC50 values in a standard viability/proliferation readout. Use a log-scale concentration series that covers each range.

FLT3 and multikinase inhibitors (MOLM-13 is FLT3-ITD and typically very sensitive)
- Gilteritinib (FLT3/AXL inhibitor): 0.03–300 nM
- Quizartinib/AC220 (FLT2/FLT3 inhibitor): 0.01–100 nM
- Crenolanib (FLT3 inhibitor, including D835 mutants): 0.1–1000 nM
- Midostaurin (multikinase incl. FLT3/PKC): 1–10,000 nM (0.001–10 μM)
- Sorafenib (multikinase incl. FLT3/VEGFR/RAF): 0.1–1000 nM
- Ponatinib (broad TK inhibitor incl. FLT3): 0.01–300 nM
- Sunitinib (multikinase): 1–10,000 nM (0.001–10 μM)

Apoptosis/protein homeostasis
- Venetoclax (BCL-2 inhibitor): 1–10,000 nM (0.001–10 μM)
- Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor): 0.5–100 nM
- Carfilzomib (proteasome inhibitor): 0.5–100 nM

Epigenetic/translation/export
- Panobinostat (HDAC inhibitor): 0.5–100 nM
- Omacetaxine/homoharringtonine (protein translation inhibitor): 1–100 nM
- Selinexor (XPO1 inhibitor): 10–1000 nM

MAPK/mTOR/JAK pathway
- Trametinib (MEK1/2 inhibitor): 0.1–1000 nM
- Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2 inhibitor): 0.1–30 μM
- Everolimus (mTORC1 inhibitor): 0.1–1000 nM

Repurposed anti-infectives/metabolism-modulating agents with AML activity
- Tigecycline (mitochondrial translation inhibitor): 0.05–50 μM
- Auranofin (thioredoxin reductase inhibitor): 0.05–20 μM
- Niclosamide (OXPHOS/Wnt/STAT3 modulator): 0.05–10 μM
- Disulfiram (aldehyde dehydrogenase inhibitor; copper-dependent cytotoxicity): Disulfiram 0.01–10 μM; activity is enhanced by low-μM Cu2+ (e.g., 0.5–1 μM)

Additional classic agent with AML activity
- Arsenic trioxide (ROS modulation, PML-RARA targeting in APL; broader cytotoxicity at higher doses): 0.05–10 μM

Notes
- The ranges above are intended to bracket typical cellular potencies reported for AML lines; exact IC50s can vary with assay format and serum conditions. 
- Compounds like niclosamide and disulfiram have solubility/stability nuances; ensure appropriate solvent and freshness when preparing stocks.
- MOLM-13 (FLT3-ITD) is often most responsive to FLT3 inhibitors (gilteritinib, quizartinib, crenolanib, sorafenib, ponatinib); include those among your first-pass set.
