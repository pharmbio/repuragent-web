=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a **shortlist of 20 clinically used drugs** with reported or plausible anti‑AML activity (many with data in MOLM‑13 or closely related FLT3‑ITD AML lines). For each, I include:

- **Proposed 10‑point IC₅₀ assay range** (final concentrations, in standard 96‑well viability assays, 72 h typical).
- **Notes** focused on MOLM‑13/AML relevance.

Use DMSO ≤0.1% v/v, 2–3 technical replicates per plate, at least 3 independent experiments. A typical dilution scheme is 1:3 or 1:2.5 serial dilutions across the range.

---

## 1. Sorafenib  
- **Range**: 0.5 nM – 10 μM  
  (0.5, 1.5, 4.5, 13.5, 40, 120, 360, 1080, 3240, 9720 nM)  
- **Notes**: Multi‑kinase inhibitor; strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML including MOLM‑13.

## 2. Midostaurin (PKC412)  
- **Range**: 0.5 nM – 10 μM  
- **Notes**: FLT3 inhibitor approved for FLT3‑mut AML; MOLM‑13 is a standard responsive line.

## 3. Gilteritinib  
- **Range**: 0.1 nM – 2 μM  
  (0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000, 2000 nM)  
- **Notes**: Highly potent FLT3/AXL inhibitor; MOLM‑13 IC₅₀ often in low‑nM.

## 4. Quizartinib (AC220)  
- **Range**: 0.05 nM – 1 μM  
  (0.05, 0.15, 0.45, 1.35, 4, 12, 36, 108, 324, 972 nM)  
- **Notes**: Very potent FLT3 inhibitor; MOLM‑13 usually sub‑nanomolar to low‑nM.

## 5. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)  
- **Range**: 1 nM – 20 μM  
- **Notes**: BCL‑2 inhibitor; strong single‑agent and combo activity in AML lines including MOLM‑13.

## 6. Ruxolitinib  
- **Range**: 10 nM – 30 μM  
- **Notes**: JAK1/2 inhibitor; moderate anti‑proliferative effects, may synergize with FLT3 or BCL‑2 inhibition.

## 7. Dasatinib  
- **Range**: 0.5 nM – 10 μM  
- **Notes**: SRC/ABL inhibitor with off‑target activity in AML; some MOLM‑13 sensitivity reported.

## 8. Imatinib  
- **Range**: 0.1 μM – 100 μM  
  (0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000, 10000 μM – you can truncate if toxicity obvious)  
- **Notes**: Limited FLT3 activity; mainly exploratory/negative control in FLT3‑ITD AML.

## 9. Nilotinib  
- **Range**: 1 nM – 20 μM  
- **Notes**: More potent than imatinib; some reported activity in FLT3‑mutant AML at μM range.

## 10. Ponatinib  
- **Range**: 0.1 nM – 5 μM  
- **Notes**: Broad tyrosine kinase inhibitor (including FLT3); strong activity on FLT3‑ITD AML.

## 11. Tofacitinib  
- **Range**: 10 nM – 30 μM  
- **Notes**: JAK inhibitor used for autoimmunity; modest direct antiproliferative effects, useful in combinations.

## 12. Ibrutinib  
- **Range**: 10 nM – 20 μM  
- **Notes**: BTK inhibitor; AML cells may show variable BTK dependence; consider for repurposing screens.

## 13. Everolimus  
- **Range**: 0.5 nM – 10 μM  
- **Notes**: mTORC1 inhibitor; AML, including MOLM‑13, often mTOR‑dependent; IC₅₀ usually low‑nM to low‑μM.

## 14. Sirolimus (Rapamycin)  
- **Range**: 0.5 nM – 10 μM  
- **Notes**: Classic mTOR inhibitor; often cytostatic; use 72–96 h to see effects on cell number.

## 15. Metformin  
- **Range**: 0.1 mM – 50 mM  
  (0.1, 0.3, 1, 3, 10, 15, 20, 30, 40, 50 mM)  
- **Notes**: Indirect AMPK activator; in vitro effects generally at high mM; test for metabolic vulnerability.

## 16. Chloroquine (or Hydroxychloroquine)  
- **Range**: 1 μM – 100 μM  
- **Notes**: Autophagy inhibitor; may sensitize AML cells to other agents; alone often μM‑range IC₅₀.

## 17. Disulfiram  
- **Range**: 10 nM – 20 μM  
- **Notes**: ALDH inhibitor; AML stem‑like cells rely on ALDH; reported nanomolar–μM activity in AML lines.

## 18. Niclosamide  
- **Range**: 10 nM – 10 μM  
- **Notes**: Anthelmintic; targets multiple signaling pathways (Wnt, STAT3, mTOR); AML data suggest low‑μM IC₅₀.

## 19. Mebendazole  
- **Range**: 0.1 μM – 50 μM  
- **Notes**: Microtubule inhibitor; some preclinical AML data with μM IC₅₀s.

## 20. Valproic Acid  
- **Range**: 0.1 mM – 10 mM  
- **Notes**: HDAC inhibitor used as antiepileptic; induces differentiation and growth arrest in AML, but requires mM levels.

### Practical assay setup (brief)

- **Cells**: MOLM‑13, 3–5 × 10³ cells/well in 96‑well plates.  
- **Duration**: 72 h for IC₅₀; 48 h if very potent compounds.  
- **Readout**: CellTiter‑Glo, resazurin, or similar.  
- **Controls**:  
  - Vehicle (0.1% DMSO) as 100% viability.  
  - Positive control: a known potent FLT3 inhibitor (e.g., quizartinib) on every plate.  

If you tell me which subset (e.g., only FDA‑approved non‑oncology drugs, or only oral agents) you care most about, I can refine this list and narrow the ranges more tightly around likely IC₅₀s for MOLM‑13.
