=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing drugs (approved or well‑characterized investigational agents) that have either documented activity against AML cell lines or strong mechanistic rationale for activity in FLT3‑ITD / MLL‑rearranged AML models such as MOLM13. For each I give: short rationale, a practical IC50 testing plan (recommended top concentration and a simple 10‑point serial dilution scheme that will generally cover the expected potency), an expected IC50 range (from literature/typical cell‑line data or mechanism-based expectation), and short assay notes (solubility, cofactor requirements, caveats).

General assay recommendations (apply to all compounds)
- Cell line: MOLM13 (plate cells at exponential growth phase). Typical starting density for 72 h viability: 2–5 × 10^4 cells/mL (optimize for your assay).
- Incubation: 72 hours is standard for proliferation/viability IC50. You can also run 48 and 96 h for time dependence.
- Readout: CellTiter‑Glo (luminescence) or alamarBlue/MTT; include vehicle (DMSO) controls and no‑cell blanks.
- Serial dilution: 10‑point 3‑fold serial dilution is recommended for most compounds (covers ~3 logs). Example: top concentration C0 then C0/3, C0/9 ... C0/6561. If compound potency/solubility requires, use 2‑fold dilution.
- Vehicle: keep final DMSO ≤0.5% (ideally ≤0.1–0.2%).
- Replicates: at least n = 3 biological replicates with technical duplicates.
- Special reagents: some drugs need cofactors (e.g., disulfiram requires copper for robust activity); see notes for each.

1) Venetoclax (BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved)
- Rationale: potent BCL‑2 dependence in many AML lines; synergy with FLT3 inhibitors; MOLM13 is often sensitive.
- Test plan: top conc 10 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution (10 µM → 1.52 nM).
- Expected IC50 (MOLM13): ~10–200 nM (often low‑ to mid‑nM).
- Notes: DMSO soluble; avoid long preincubation at high DMSO.

2) Gilteritinib (FLT3 inhibitor; approved for FLT3‑mutant AML)
- Rationale: potent FLT3 kinase inhibition; MOLM13 (FLT3‑ITD) is very sensitive.
- Test plan: top conc 1 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution (1 µM → 0.152 nM).
- Expected IC50: ~1–20 nM.
- Notes: DMSO soluble; very potent—use low top conc to avoid hook effects.

3) Quizartinib (FLT3 inhibitor)
- Rationale: highly potent against FLT3‑ITD cells like MOLM13.
- Test plan: top conc 1 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: sub‑ to low‑nM (0.1–10 nM).
- Notes: potent; monitor for solubility at higher concentrations.

4) Midostaurin (multikinase/FLT3 inhibitor)
- Rationale: FLT3 inhibition and broader kinase targeting.
- Test plan: top conc 1 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~10–200 nM.
- Notes: DMSO soluble.

5) Venetoclax + FLT3 inhibitor combo (simple test suggestion)
- Rationale: clinical & preclinical synergy in FLT3‑mutant AML. Test single agents and fixed‑ratio combos.
- Test plan: evaluate single‑agent curves as above, and combos at fixed ratio or matrix (e.g., venetoclax 100:1 gilteritinib).
- Expected combination effect: left shift of IC50; test synergy metrics (Bliss/ZIP).

6) JQ1 (BET bromodomain inhibitor; tool compound)
- Rationale: BET proteins regulate MYC and other oncogenic transcription; MOLM13 often sensitive.
- Test plan: top conc 10 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~100–1000 nM.
- Notes: DMSO soluble; transient effects on transcription require 48–72 h readout.

7) OTX015 / MK‑8628 (BET inhibitor; clinical candidate)
- Rationale: BET inhibition similar to JQ1, clinically relevant agent.
- Test plan: top conc 10 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~100–1000 nM.
- Notes: DMSO soluble.

8) Alvocidib (flavopiridol; CDK9/CDK inhibitor)
- Rationale: transcriptional CDK inhibition reduces anti‑apoptotic transcripts; active in AML models.
- Test plan: top conc 1 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~10–200 nM.
- Notes: very potent; consider shorter exposures for mechanistic readouts.

9) Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor; approved)
- Rationale: disrupts protein homeostasis, induces apoptosis in some AML lines.
- Test plan: top conc 1 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~1–100 nM (line dependent).
- Notes: water soluble in formulation; often tested pulsed exposures.

10) 17‑AAG (tanespimycin; HSP90 inhibitor)
- Rationale: degrades oncogenic kinases (e.g., FLT3), active in AML cell lines.
- Test plan: top conc 1 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~10–500 nM.
- Notes: DMSO soluble; monitor for stress response induction.

11) Auranofin (anti‑rheumatic; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor)
- Rationale: induces oxidative stress and cell death in AML; reported activity in AML lines.
- Test plan: top conc 10 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~0.1–1 µM.
- Notes: DMSO soluble; oxidative stress assays complement viability readout.

12) Niclosamide (anthelmintic)
- Rationale: pleiotropic effects (WNT/STAT/mTOR) and reported cytotoxicity in hematologic malignancies.
- Test plan: top conc 10 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~0.5–5 µM (line dependent).
- Notes: poor solubility—prepare concentrated DMSO stock and avoid precipitation.

13) Disulfiram (alcohol‑aversion drug) ± Cu2+
- Rationale: disulfiram forms copper complexes that inhibit proteasome/ALDH and induces apoptosis in cancer cells.
- Test plan: top conc disulfiram 10 µM + 1 µM CuCl2; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50 (with copper): ~0.1–2 µM.
- Notes: requires copper for maximal activity; test disulfiram alone and with equimolar low micromolar Cu.

14) Mebendazole (anthelmintic)
- Rationale: microtubule disruption and other effects; reported AML in vitro activity.
- Test plan: top conc 10 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~0.5–5 µM.
- Notes: limited aqueous solubility—use DMSO stocks.

15) Artesunate / Dihydroartemisinin (antimalarial)
- Rationale: generates ROS and induces apoptosis in leukemia cells.
- Test plan: top conc 50 µM (or 100 µM if needed); 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~1–20 µM.
- Notes: water soluble enough; short half‑life—72 h incubation OK, but monitor kinetics.

16) Simvastatin (lipophilic statin)
- Rationale: inhibits mevalonate pathway, can induce apoptosis in AML cells and sensitize to other agents.
- Test plan: top conc 50 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~1–10 µM (often high micromolar in vitro).
- Notes: prodrug hydrolysis may be needed (active hydroxy acid form); consider pravastatin for hydrophilic testing.

17) Metformin (antidiabetic)
- Rationale: metabolic stress and AMPK activation; reported anti‑leukemia effects but requires high in vitro concentrations.
- Test plan: top conc 50 mM; 10‑point 2‑fold dilution (50 mM → ~0.1 mM). Alternatively test 0.1–50 mM range.
- Expected IC50: ~1–20 mM (cell‑culture artifact vs clinically achievable).
- Notes: effects observed at mM range—interpret cautiously.

18) Itraconazole (antifungal)
- Rationale: multiple off‑target effects (Hedgehog, angiogenesis, cholesterol trafficking) and anticancer activity reported.
- Test plan: top conc 10 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~0.5–10 µM.
- Notes: lipophilic—DMSO stocks recommended.

19) Hydroxychloroquine / Chloroquine (autophagy inhibitors)
- Rationale: block autophagy and lysosomal function, can sensitize AML to apoptosis.
- Test plan: top conc 100 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~10–50 µM (often cytostatic at these levels).
- Notes: high concentrations may be required; interpret autophagy assays in parallel.

20) Clofazimine (antileprosy)
- Rationale: accumulates in lysosomes, induces ROS and apoptosis in cancer cell models including leukemia.
- Test plan: top conc 10 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution.
- Expected IC50: ~1–5 µM.
- Notes: lipophilic—DMSO stocks; long intracellular retention may affect time course.

Practical example dilution setups
- For a compound with expected nM potency (e.g., gilteritinib): use top 1 µM; 10‑point 3‑fold dilution → 1 µM, 333 nM, 111 nM, 37 nM, 12 nM, 4 nM, 1.2 nM, 0.4 nM, 0.13 nM, 0.04 nM.
- For µM‑potency compounds (e.g., niclosamide): top 10 µM; same 3‑fold scheme.
- For high‑conc compounds (metformin): use 2‑fold 10‑point series if needed (50 mM top → 48 µM bottom).

Notes and caveats
- MOLM13 is FLT3‑ITD positive and often sensitive to FLT3 inhibitors and BCL‑2 inhibition, so include gilteritinib/quizartinib and venetoclax as positive controls.
- For repurposed non‑oncology drugs, reported in vitro IC50s vary by assay and lab; the listed expected ranges are approximate and meant to guide top concentration selection.
- Solubility and serum binding can shift apparent IC50s—monitor for precipitation and consider testing in both serum‑containing and low‑serum media if relevant.
- When testing drugs like disulfiram, always test with and without required cofactors (e.g., copper).
- For combination testing, use fixed‑ratio matrices or checkerboard designs and quantify synergy (Bliss, Loewe, ZIP).
