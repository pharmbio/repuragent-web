=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a list of 20 clinically used / late-stage drugs with published or strong preclinical evidence of activity against AML cells (including MOLM13 or closely related FLT3-ITD+ lines such as MV4-11). For each, I include:

- **Primary target / rationale**
- **Reported potency vs AML (when available)**
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay concentration range** (a 7–10 point dose–response curve in log10 steps).  
  These ranges are meant for **in vitro** assays on MOLM13 in 10% FBS, 72 h exposure (common for proliferation / viability) and should be refined using pilot experiments and literature.

Concentration units are **µM**, unless otherwise specified. Always prepare stocks in DMSO and keep final DMSO ≤0.1%.

---

## 1. Midostaurin (PKC412)
- **Type:** Multikinase inhibitor, approved for FLT3-mut+ AML.
- **Rationale:** Inhibits FLT3, KIT, PDGFR; strong activity in FLT3-ITD AML lines including MOLM13.
- **Reported potency:** FLT3-ITD AML cell IC₅₀ often ~5–50 nM.
- **Test range:** **0.0003–3 µM** (0.3 nM–3 µM).

---

## 2. Gilteritinib (ASP2215)
- **Type:** FLT3/AXL inhibitor; approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3+ AML.
- **Rationale:** Potent vs FLT3-ITD and TKD mutations; robust data in MOLM13/MV4-11.
- **Reported potency:** nM range in FLT3-mut cells (∼1–10 nM).
- **Test range:** **0.0001–1 µM** (0.1 nM–1 µM).

---

## 3. Quizartinib (AC220)
- **Type:** Highly selective FLT3 inhibitor.
- **Rationale:** Very strong anti-proliferative effects in FLT3-ITD AML lines.
- **Reported potency:** Often <1–5 nM in MV4-11/MOLM13.
- **Test range:** **0.00003–0.3 µM** (0.03 nM–300 nM).

---

## 4. Sorafenib
- **Type:** Multikinase inhibitor (RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR, FLT3).
- **Rationale:** Known off-label activity in FLT3-ITD AML; widely used and available.
- **Reported potency:** AML lines commonly 0.05–1 µM.
- **Test range:** **0.01–10 µM**.

---

## 5. Dasatinib
- **Type:** SRC/ABL family inhibitor (CML drug).
- **Rationale:** Inhibits multiple kinases relevant in AML (SRC family, c-KIT); shown to reduce viability in some AML lines.
- **Reported potency:** ∼10–100 nM in sensitive hematologic cells.
- **Test range:** **0.001–3 µM**.

---

## 6. Imatinib
- **Type:** BCR-ABL / KIT / PDGFR inhibitor.
- **Rationale:** Some AML subsets with KIT or PDGFR rearrangements respond; generally less potent vs MOLM13 but useful as a comparator.
- **Reported potency:** Often 1–10 µM in non-BCR-ABL AML.
- **Test range:** **0.1–50 µM**.

---

## 7. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)
- **Type:** BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved in AML (combo regimens).
- **Rationale:** Strong apoptosis induction in many AML lines including FLT3-ITD; synergy with hypomethylating agents.
- **Reported potency:** Frequently 1–100 nM in sensitive AML cells.
- **Test range:** **0.0003–3 µM** (0.3 nM–3 µM).

---

## 8. Glasdegib
- **Type:** SMO (Hedgehog pathway) inhibitor; approved with low-dose cytarabine in AML.
- **Rationale:** Targets leukemia stem cell–related signaling; moderate direct anti-proliferative effects.
- **Reported potency:** AML IC₅₀ often in low µM.
- **Test range:** **0.1–30 µM**.

---

## 9. Ruxolitinib
- **Type:** JAK1/2 inhibitor.
- **Rationale:** JAK/STAT activation occurs in subsets of AML; reported activity in some AML models and in FLT3-ITD/JAK-activated contexts.
- **Reported potency:** 0.1–1 µM in JAK-driven hematologic cells.
- **Test range:** **0.01–10 µM**.

---

## 10. Tofacitinib
- **Type:** JAK1/3 inhibitor (RA drug).
- **Rationale:** JAK/STAT pathway contribution to AML survival/proliferation; tofacitinib has shown activity in some leukemia models.
- **Reported potency:** Low–mid µM vs many non-JAK-addicted lines.
- **Test range:** **0.3–30 µM**.

---

## 11. Baricitinib
- **Type:** JAK1/2 inhibitor (RA drug).
- **Rationale:** Similar to ruxolitinib; repurposing interest in hematologic malignancies with hyperactive JAK/STAT.
- **Reported potency:** Typically low µM in non-JAK-addicted cells.
- **Test range:** **0.1–30 µM**.

---

## 12. Palbociclib
- **Type:** CDK4/6 inhibitor (breast cancer drug).
- **Rationale:** AML often depends on cyclin D–CDK4/6–Rb axis; palbociclib shows anti-leukemic activity in subsets of AML and synergy with other agents.
- **Reported potency:** Often 0.1–2 µM.
- **Test range:** **0.01–10 µM**.

---

## 13. Ribociclib or Abemaciclib (choose one for initial screen)
- **Type:** CDK4/6 inhibitors.
- **Rationale:** Similar to palbociclib; some data in AML and other leukemias.
- **Reported potency:** 0.05–5 µM depending on line.
- **Test range:** **0.01–10 µM**.

---

## 14. Everolimus
- **Type:** mTORC1 inhibitor.
- **Rationale:** PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway is active in AML; everolimus shows growth inhibition and differentiation in AML cells.
- **Reported potency:** Typically low–mid µM.
- **Test range:** **0.01–20 µM**.

---

## 15. Ibrutinib
- **Type:** BTK inhibitor (CLL/MCL drug).
- **Rationale:** Off-target activity on multiple kinases; BTK and related signaling implicated in some AML subsets; modest direct activity but potential repurposing interest.
- **Reported potency:** Often 1–10 µM in AML-like contexts.
- **Test range:** **0.1–30 µM**.

---

## 16. Nilotinib
- **Type:** BCR-ABL / KIT inhibitor.
- **Rationale:** More potent KIT inhibition than imatinib; shows antiproliferative effects in KIT+ AML and has some activity in FLT3-ITD models.
- **Reported potency:** Often low µM in non-BCR-ABL AML lines.
- **Test range:** **0.01–20 µM**.

---

## 17. Panobinostat
- **Type:** Pan–HDAC inhibitor.
- **Rationale:** Epigenetic dysregulation is central in AML; panobinostat has strong anti-proliferative and pro-apoptotic effects in AML lines, including FLT3-ITD.
- **Reported potency:** Sub-µM (∼10–100 nM) in many AML cells.
- **Test range:** **0.0003–3 µM**.

---

## 18. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- **Type:** HDAC inhibitor (approved in CTCL).
- **Rationale:** Broad AML activity and frequently used in preclinical AML combinations.
- **Reported potency:** Typically 0.5–5 µM.
- **Test range:** **0.1–20 µM**.

---

## 19. Bortezomib
- **Type:** Proteasome inhibitor (multiple myeloma).
- **Rationale:** Ubiquitin–proteasome pathway critical for AML survival; bortezomib shows strong apoptosis induction in AML lines.
- **Reported potency:** 5–50 nM in many hematologic cells.
- **Test range:** **0.00001–1 µM** (0.01 nM–1 µM).

---

## 20. Carfilzomib
- **Type:** Irreversible proteasome inhibitor.
- **Rationale:** Similar to bortezomib; potent activity in AML cell lines reported.
- **Reported potency:** ∼1–20 nM.
- **Test range:** **0.00001–0.3 µM**.

### Practical notes for IC₅₀ assays in MOLM13

- **Assay duration:** 72 h is standard for proliferation/viability (CellTiter-Glo, MTS, resazurin), with 48 h as a secondary timepoint if you want more immediate effects.
- **Seeding density:** Typically 0.5–1.5 × 10⁵ cells/mL in 96-well plates; ensure cells remain in log-phase and below overgrowth.
- **Controls:**  
  - Vehicle (0.1% DMSO).  
  - Positive killing control (e.g., staurosporine 0.5–1 µM).
- **Curve design:** 7–10 concentrations in 3–5-fold serial dilutions across suggested ranges; refine after first run to bracket the IC₅₀ tightly.
- **Replicates:** At least triplicate wells per condition; perform ≥3 independent experiments for robust IC₅₀ estimation.
