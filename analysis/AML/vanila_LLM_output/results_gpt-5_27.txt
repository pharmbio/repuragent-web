=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 approved drugs from other indications with published anti-AML activity, including activity in the FLT3-ITD–positive AML line MOLM-13. For each, I list an approximate reported cellular IC50 (viability/proliferation) in MOLM-13 or closely related FLT3-ITD AML lines (e.g., MV4-11). Potencies vary with assay format, serum, and exposure time; use the original papers and your institutional SOPs to choose exact test ranges.

FLT3/multikinase inhibitors (strong activity in MOLM-13)
1) Ponatinib (CML/Ph+ ALL; pan-ABL/FLT3 inhibitor): IC50 ~1–10 nM in MOLM-13/MV4-11.
2) Sorafenib (RCC/HCC; multikinase, FLT3-ITD active): IC50 ~1–10 nM in MOLM-13/MV4-11.
3) Sunitinib (RCC; multikinase/FLT3): IC50 ~5–20 nM in FLT3-ITD AML lines, including MOLM-13.
4) Cabozantinib (RCC/thyroid; MET/VEGFR, also inhibits FLT3): IC50 ~5–30 nM in MOLM-13/MV4-11.
5) Regorafenib (CRC/GIST/HCC; multikinase, FLT3): IC50 ~30–100 nM in MOLM-13/MV4-11.
6) Pazopanib (RCC/STS; multikinase, some FLT3 activity): IC50 ~0.1–0.3 μM in MOLM-13/MV4-11.
7) Dasatinib (CML/Ph+ ALL; SRC/ABL, some FLT3): IC50 ~50–200 nM in MOLM-13 (context-dependent).
8) Nilotinib (CML; ABL/KIT, weaker FLT3): IC50 ~1–5 μM in MOLM-13.

Non-FLT3 targeted oncology drugs
9) Omacetaxine mepesuccinate (CML; protein translation inhibitor): IC50 ~2–20 nM in MOLM-13.
10) Bortezomib (MM/MCL; proteasome inhibitor): IC50 ~5–50 nM in MOLM-13.
11) Carfilzomib (MM; proteasome inhibitor): IC50 ~10–50 nM in MOLM-13.
12) Selinexor (MM; XPO1 inhibitor): IC50 ~0.1–0.5 μM in MOLM-13.
13) Ibrutinib (CLL/MCL; BTK inhibitor with off-targets): IC50 ~1–5 μM in MOLM-13 (variable).

Repurposed anti-infectives and other non-oncology agents
14) Tigecycline (antibiotic; mitochondrial translation): IC50 ~1–5 μM in MOLM-13.
15) Niclosamide (anthelmintic; OXPHOS/Wnt signaling): IC50 ~0.2–1 μM in MOLM-13.
16) Auranofin (anti-rheumatic; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor): IC50 ~0.5–2 μM in MOLM-13.
17) Clofazimine (antimycobacterial; Wnt/lysosome effects): IC50 ~0.1–0.5 μM in AML lines incl. MOLM-13.
18) Mefloquine (antimalarial; lysosomal disruption): IC50 ~2–8 μM in MOLM-13.
19) Artesunate/dihydroartemisinin (antimalarial; ROS/Fe-dependent): IC50 ~1–5 μM in MOLM-13.
20) Pimozide (antipsychotic; STAT5 inhibition): IC50 ~1–5 μM in MOLM-13. Thioridazine (antipsychotic) is similar with IC50 ~5–15 μM.

Notes
- MOLM-13 is FLT3-ITD–driven; FLT3-active TKIs (ponatinib, sorafenib, sunitinib, cabozantinib, regorafenib) are typically the most potent single agents in this line.
- Reported IC50 values come from peer-reviewed studies using standard cell viability/proliferation readouts; expect shifts based on serum, exposure time, and endpoint.
- Check original publications and product datasheets for details and verify identity/mycoplasma status of MOLM-13 before experiments.
