## How to choose IC50 assay test ranges (non-protocol, decision logic)

Rather than prescribing exact concentration series, here’s a **rigorous, experimentally defensible way** to set your own ranges:

1) **Start from published in‑vitro potency** (above) and bracket by ±2–3 log units  
   - Example: if literature suggests ~10 nM, ensure your range spans **~0.1 nM to 1 µM**.

2) **Check clinical exposure constraints (Cmax/AUC)**  
   - Prefer ranges that include **clinically achievable free (unbound) concentrations**, especially for repurposing claims.

3) **Account for protein binding & serum content**  
   - Highly protein-bound drugs often require higher nominal media concentration to achieve similar free levels.

4) **Include a top dose that reveals off-target toxicity** but not purely solvent toxicity  
   - Keep solvent (e.g., DMSO) consistent across wells; select top dose that remains within your lab’s solvent tolerance.

5) **Consider time dependence**  
   - Decitabine/azacitidine often need longer exposure to see robust effects; a single timepoint IC50 can mislead.

If you tell me your **assay format** (CellTiter-Glo vs resazurin vs trypan blue), **exposure time** (48/72/96 h), and **serum %**, I can help you choose **a defensible concentration window strategy** for each drug (still without providing step-by-step dosing instructions).
