=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing, non-AML–approved drugs with published activity against AML cell lines (many with data in FLT3-ITD models such as MOLM13 and/or MV4-11). For each, I list a brief rationale and an experimentally testable concentration window suitable for deriving an IC50 in a 72-hour viability assay (e.g., CellTiter-Glo) using 8–12 serial dilutions. Unless noted, dissolve in DMSO and keep final DMSO ≤0.1–0.2%.

FLT3/kinase-targeting oncology drugs (repurposing within oncology)
1) Sorafenib (multi-kinase; inhibits FLT3-ITD signaling; strong activity in MOLM13/MV4-11)
   - Suggested range: 0.5–500 nM (e.g., 10-point, 3-fold dilution starting at 500 nM)

2) Ponatinib (ABL/FLT3; potent vs FLT3-ITD; robust MOLM13 activity)
   - Suggested range: 0.1–200 nM

3) Sunitinib (multi-kinase including FLT3; active in FLT3-ITD AML lines)
   - Suggested range: 1–3000 nM

4) Dasatinib (Src/Abl; disrupts FLT3-ITD downstream signaling; AML cell line activity reported)
   - Suggested range: 0.5–1000 nM

Proteostasis/export inhibitors (approved for other cancers)
5) Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor; nanomolar cytotoxicity in AML including MOLM13)
   - Suggested range: 0.5–100 nM

6) Carfilzomib (proteasome inhibitor; AML cell line activity)
   - Suggested range: 1–200 nM

7) Selinexor (XPO1 inhibitor; AML lines, including FLT3-ITD, often sub-µM sensitive)
   - Suggested range: 5–2000 nM

Epigenetic modulators (approved for other cancers)
8) Panobinostat (pan-HDAC inhibitor; potent sub-µM activity in AML lines)
   - Suggested range: 2–200 nM

9) Vorinostat/SAHA (HDAC inhibitor; induces apoptosis/differentiation in AML lines)
   - Suggested range: 0.3–10 µM

Mitochondrial/metabolic and lysosomal disruptors (non-oncology approvals)
10) Tigecycline (antibiotic; inhibits mitochondrial translation; kills AML stem/progenitor cells)
    - Suggested range: 0.2–20 µM

11) Mefloquine (antimalarial; lysosomotropic; reported AML selectivity including LSCs)
    - Suggested range: 0.5–20 µM

12) Niclosamide (anthelmintic; uncouples OXPHOS/Wnt; AML line activity)
    - Suggested range: 0.05–5 µM

13) Simvastatin (use the active hydroxy-acid form; mevalonate pathway blockade; AML antiproliferative)
    - Suggested range: 0.1–10 µM
    - Note: test mevalonate rescue (50–100 µM) as a specificity control.

Redox and proteostasis disruptors (non-oncology approvals)
14) Auranofin (anti-rheumatic; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor; potent in AML including FLT3-ITD)
    - Suggested range: 50–2000 nM

15) Disulfiram + copper (ALDH inhibitor; forms Cu–DSF complex targeting proteostasis/NPL4; AML active)
    - Suggested range: 10–1000 nM disulfiram at a fixed 0.5–2 µM CuCl2 (optimize copper; include copper-alone controls)

Ion channel/calmodulin/STAT pathway modulators (non-oncology approvals)
16) Pimozide (antipsychotic; STAT5 and calmodulin modulation; reported activity in FLT3-ITD AML)
    - Suggested range: 0.5–10 µM

17) Thioridazine (antipsychotic; functionally targets AML stem-like cells; antiproliferative)
    - Suggested range: 0.5–10 µM

Protein synthesis/mTOR axis
18) Omacetaxine (homoharringtonine; protein synthesis inhibitor; approved in CML; AML cell line cytotoxicity)
    - Suggested range: 1–100 nM

19) Temsirolimus (mTORC1 inhibitor; cytostatic/antiproliferative in AML, sometimes pro-apoptotic at higher exposures or in combinations)
    - Suggested range: 10–1000 nM

Oxidative stress inducers/antimalarials
20) Dihydroartemisinin (antimalarial; ROS generation/ER stress; AML line activity)
    - Suggested range: 0.5–20 µM

Practical assay notes
- Use half-log or 3-fold serial dilutions spanning the ranges above; 72-hour exposure commonly gives robust IC50s for MOLM13.
- Keep vehicle controls matched; maintain DMSO ≤0.1–0.2%.
- MOLM13 are FLT3-ITD–driven; expect greatest potency from FLT3-directed TKIs (sorafenib, ponatinib, sunitinib, dasatinib), proteasome inhibitors, and some redox/proteostasis agents.
- For disulfiram, include copper; for simvastatin, use the hydroxy-acid form; serum protein binding may shift apparent potency (consider 0–10% FBS comparisons).
- Confirm hits with an orthogonal readout (e.g., Annexin V/PI or caspase activation) and, if possible, with a second FLT3-ITD line (MV4-11) and a FLT3-wildtype AML line to gauge selectivity.

These concentration windows are based on published AML cell line sensitivities (including MOLM13/MV4-11 where available) and should allow you to capture full sigmoidal dose–response curves for IC50 fitting.
