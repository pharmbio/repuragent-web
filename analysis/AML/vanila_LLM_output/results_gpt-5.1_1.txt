=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 existing (non‑AML) drugs with published or strong preclinical rationale for repurposing in AML, including MOLM‑13 where data are available. For each, I list:

- **Putative mechanism in AML**
- **Reported IC₅₀ range** (preferentially MOLM‑13 or similar FLT3‑ITD AML cells; when not available, I note the cell type or give a typical in‑vitro range from leukemia models)
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay range** (a practical concentration series to test in MOLM‑13 proliferation/viability assays such as CellTiter‑Glo or MTT)

Notes for assay design at the end.

---

## 1. Sorafenib (multi‑kinase inhibitor; approved for HCC, RCC)

- **Mechanism**: Potent FLT3‑ITD inhibitor; also targets RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR; substantial activity in FLT3‑mutant AML.
- **Reported IC₅₀**:  
  - FLT3‑ITD AML cells: ~1–10 nM for biochemical FLT3 inhibition;  
  - Cellular antiproliferative IC₅₀ often ~10–100 nM (MOLM‑13, MV4‑11).
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.5 nM – 5 µM** (10‑point half‑log dilution; e.g., 0.5, 1, 3, 10, 30, 100, 300, 1000, 3000, 5000 nM).

---

## 2. Sunitinib (multi‑kinase inhibitor; RCC, GIST)

- **Mechanism**: Inhibits FLT3, KIT, VEGFR, PDGFR; reported activity in FLT3‑mutant AML lines.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: Antiproliferative IC₅₀ in FLT3‑ITD cells generally **~20–200 nM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 10 µM**.

---

## 3. Dasatinib (BCR‑ABL/SRC inhibitor; CML, Ph+ ALL)

- **Mechanism**: Inhibits SRC family kinases, ABL, KIT; can impair AML cell proliferation and leukemic stem cell functions.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML cell lines often **~10–100 nM** for viability/proliferation.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 5 µM**.

---

## 4. Nilotinib (BCR‑ABL inhibitor; CML)

- **Mechanism**: Inhibits BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR; some activity in KIT/FLT3‑driven AML models.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML cell lines typically **~0.1–3 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **10 nM – 30 µM**.

---

## 5. Imatinib (BCR‑ABL/KIT inhibitor; CML, GIST)

- **Mechanism**: Inhibits BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR; active mainly in KIT‑dependent AML subsets.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML lines (non‑BCR‑ABL) usually **1–10 µM** for growth inhibition.
- **Suggested assay range**: **100 nM – 50 µM**.

---

## 6. Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2 inhibitor; myelofibrosis, PV)

- **Mechanism**: Inhibits JAK‑STAT signaling; some AML (including FLT3‑ITD) display JAK‑STAT dependence; synergy noted with FLT3 inhibitors.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: Leukemia cell lines **~0.2–5 µM** for growth/STAT inhibition.
- **Suggested assay range**: **50 nM – 20 µM**.

---

## 7. Tofacitinib (JAK inhibitor; RA, PsA)

- **Mechanism**: JAK1/3 inhibition; can reduce survival of some leukemia/lymphoid cells dependent on cytokine/JAK signaling.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: Leukemia models often **1–10 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **100 nM – 50 µM**.

---

## 8. Ibrutinib (BTK inhibitor; CLL, MCL)

- **Mechanism**: BTK blockade; in AML, BTK and related pathways support survival and stemness in some subtypes; preclinical AML data show modest direct cytotoxicity and synergy with other agents.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML cell lines often **~2–10 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **100 nM – 50 µM**.

---

## 9. Venetoclax (BCL‑2 inhibitor; CLL, AML – but including here as repurposed from CLL)

- **Mechanism**: Selective BCL‑2 inhibition; MOLM‑13 is typically BCL‑2‑dependent and sensitive.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: In FLT3‑ITD/BCL‑2–high AML (MOLM‑13, MV4‑11), **~1–100 nM** for apoptosis/viability.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 nM – 1 µM**.

---

## 10. Navitoclax / ABT‑263 (BCL‑2/BCL‑XL inhibitor; previously in oncology trials)

- **Mechanism**: Inhibits BCL‑2, BCL‑XL, BCL‑w; strong preclinical activity in AML.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML cell lines, including MOLM‑13, generally **~10–200 nM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 5 µM**.

---

## 11. Disulfiram (acetaldehyde dehydrogenase inhibitor; alcohol dependence)

- **Mechanism**: Targets ALDH, NF‑κB, and, with copper, inhibits proteasome and various oncogenic pathways; shown to kill AML stem/progenitor cells.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML cell lines often **0.1–2 µM** in presence of physiologic copper; may be higher without copper supplementation.
- **Suggested assay range**: **10 nM – 20 µM**, ideally with **1–2 µM Cu²⁺** (e.g., CuCl₂) co‑treatment.

---

## 12. Metformin (biguanide; type 2 diabetes)

- **Mechanism**: Mitochondrial complex I inhibition, AMPK activation; preferential toxicity to leukemia stem cells and OXPHOS‑dependent AML.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: Leukemia in vitro often **~1–10 mM** (high, but achievable in culture).
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 mM – 40 mM**.

---

## 13. Valproic acid (HDAC inhibitor; epilepsy, bipolar disorder)

- **Mechanism**: Weak HDAC inhibitor at pharmacologic levels; induces differentiation and apoptosis in AML blasts; used clinically in AML combination trials.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML cell lines **~0.5–5 mM** for antiproliferative effects.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 mM – 10 mM**.

---

## 14. Vorinostat / SAHA (HDAC inhibitor; CTCL)

- **Mechanism**: Broad class I/II HDAC inhibition; robust induction of apoptosis/differentiation in AML cell lines.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: MOLM‑13 and similar AML lines commonly **~0.5–2 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **50 nM – 20 µM**.

---

## 15. Panobinostat (HDAC inhibitor; multiple myeloma)

- **Mechanism**: Potent pan‑HDAC inhibitor; strong cytotoxicity in AML preclinically.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML lines generally **~5–50 nM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 nM – 1 µM**.

---

## 16. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide (IMiDs; myeloma, MDS with del(5q))

- **Mechanism**: Cereblon‑mediated degradation of IKZF1/3; immunomodulatory and direct anti‑leukemic effects; some activity in specific AML subsets.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML lines often **~5–50 µM** (lenalidomide somewhat more potent than thalidomide).
- **Suggested assay range**:  
  - **Lenalidomide**: **0.5 µM – 100 µM**  
  - **Thalidomide**: **1 µM – 200 µM**

---

## 17. Chloroquine / Hydroxychloroquine (antimalarial; autophagy inhibitor)

- **Mechanism**: Inhibits autophagosome–lysosome fusion; AML cells (especially FLT3‑ITD) may rely on autophagy; strong synergy with TKIs and BCL‑2 inhibitors.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML lines often **~5–20 µM** for viability.
- **Suggested assay range**: **1 µM – 80 µM**.

---

## 18. Atorvastatin (HMG‑CoA reductase inhibitor; hyperlipidemia)

- **Mechanism**: Inhibits mevalonate pathway; affects prenylation and membrane localization of RAS and other small GTPases; antiproliferative in AML models.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML cell lines generally **~1–10 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 µM – 50 µM**.

---

## 19. Artesunate (antimalarial artemisinin derivative)

- **Mechanism**: ROS generation, interference with iron metabolism; several reports of strong cytotoxicity in AML cells, including FLT3‑ITD.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML lines commonly **~1–10 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **0.1 µM – 50 µM**.

---

## 20. Niclosamide (anti‑helminthic)

- **Mechanism**: Mitochondrial uncoupling; inhibits Wnt/β‑catenin, STAT3, NF‑κB; reported strong antiproliferative activity in various leukemias including AML.
- **Reported IC₅₀**: AML cell lines typically **~0.2–2 µM**.
- **Suggested assay range**: **10 nM – 20 µM**.

---

## Practical notes for IC₅₀ assays in MOLM‑13

1. **General concentration strategy**  
   - Use ~8–12 concentrations in **half‑log or log₁₀ steps**, covering at least 2–3 orders of magnitude around the expected IC₅₀.  
   - Maintain constant vehicle (e.g., 0.1% DMSO) across all wells.

2. **Exposure time**  
   - 48–72 h drug exposure is typical for AML proliferation/viability assays.  
   - MOLM‑13 doubles ~24 h; 72 h often provides clear separation.

3. **Readouts**  
   - Use ATP‑based viability (CellTiter‑Glo), resazurin, or MTT; optionally Annexin V/PI for apoptosis.

4. **Controls**  
   - Positive control: a known FLT3 inhibitor active in MOLM‑13 (e.g., sorafenib, midostaurin, or gilteritinib if available).  
   - Negative control: vehicle only.

5. **Solubility**  
   - Many of these compounds are hydrophobic; pre‑dissolve in DMSO at 10–20 mM; confirm absence of precipitation in media at highest test concentration.
