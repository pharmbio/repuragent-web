## Practical IC₅₀ assay setup (MOLM‑13 focused)
If you want, I can tailor this to your exact plate format and readout.

**Typical conditions**
- Cells: MOLM‑13 in log phase, viability >90%
- Plate: 96-well (white for luminescence)
- Seeding: ~5,000–15,000 cells/well (optimize so control wells remain in exponential growth for 72 h)
- Exposure: **72 h** (common for MOLM‑13 drug response curves)
- Readout: CellTiter-Glo (ATP) or Resazurin

**Dose-response design**
- Use **10–12 concentrations**, 1:3 dilution series  
- Keep **DMSO constant** across wells (commonly 0.1% max; some cells tolerate 0.2%).
- Include controls: vehicle only, and at least one strong cytotoxic control (e.g., staurosporine or doxorubicin) for assay QC.

**Example 10-point 1:3 series (starting at 10 µM)**

## A few “gotchas” that strongly affect apparent IC₅₀
- **Serum binding** (e.g., venetoclax/navitoclax): IC₅₀ can shift dramatically with FBS%.
- **Cell density**: higher seeding can make drugs appear weaker.
- **Assay duration**: cytostatic agents (mTOR inhibitors) may need 96 h to show clearer effects.
- **Compound adsorption/instability**: proteasome inhibitors and hydrophobic TKIs can be sensitive to handling.
