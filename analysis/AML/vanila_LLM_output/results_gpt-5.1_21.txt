=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a focused list of 20 existing, non‑chemotherapy drugs (or drugs already in/near clinical use) with evidence or strong rationale for repurposing in AML, especially FLT3‑ITD–positive lines such as MOLM‑13. For each, I give:

- **Primary mechanism / rationale**
- **Suggested IC₅₀ assay test range** (final concentrations in culture medium, e.g., RPMI + 10% FBS)
- Notes on MOLM‑13 or AML data when available

For small‑molecule drugs, a **10‑point half‑log dilution** (or similar) within the suggested range is typically appropriate. Always verify solubility and vehicle (usually DMSO ≤0.1% v/v).

---

## 1. Midostaurin (PKC412)  
- **Mechanism:** Multikinase inhibitor; FLT3, PKC, KIT, VEGFR. FDA‑approved for FLT3‑mut AML.  
- **Suggested range:** **0.1 nM – 5 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Active against FLT3‑ITD AML; MOLM‑13 is a standard sensitive model. Expect IC₅₀ in the low‑ to mid‑nanomolar range.

---

## 2. Sorafenib  
- **Mechanism:** RAF/VEGFR/PDGFR/FLT3 inhibitor; approved for hepatocellular and renal carcinoma.  
- **Suggested range:** **1 nM – 10 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Strong preclinical data in FLT3‑ITD AML, including MOLM‑13; typical IC₅₀ in the low‑hundreds of nM.

---

## 3. Ponatinib  
- **Mechanism:** Pan‑tyrosine kinase inhibitor (BCR‑ABL, FLT3, KIT, FGFR, etc.); approved for CML/Ph+ ALL.  
- **Suggested range:** **0.1 nM – 2 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Potent against FLT3‑ITD; MOLM‑13 often shows IC₅₀ in the low‑nanomolar range.

---

## 4. Dasatinib  
- **Mechanism:** Src/ABL family inhibitor; CML/ALL drug. Also inhibits KIT, some FLT3 signaling.  
- **Suggested range:** **1 nM – 5 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Reported anti‑AML activity, especially in some kinase‑driven contexts; MOLM‑13 viability often reduced in 10–100 nM range.

---

## 5. Nintedanib  
- **Mechanism:** Triple angiokinase inhibitor (VEGFR/FGFR/PDGFR); approved for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis and NSCLC.  
- **Suggested range:** **1 nM – 10 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Shown to impair AML blast proliferation and angiocrine support; typical AML IC₅₀ in the sub‑ to low‑µM range.

---

## 6. Venetoclax (ABT‑199)  
- **Mechanism:** BCL‑2 inhibitor; approved in AML combos, CLL.  
- **Suggested range:** **0.1 nM – 2 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Very common AML model drug; MOLM‑13 often highly sensitive, IC₅₀ ≈ 1–50 nM.

---

## 7. Navitoclax (ABT‑263)  
- **Mechanism:** BCL‑2/BCL‑XL/BCL‑W inhibitor; clinical‑stage molecule.  
- **Suggested range:** **1 nM – 5 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Broad anti‑leukemic activity; IC₅₀ in AML lines typically tens of nM to low‑µM.

---

## 8. Ruxolitinib  
- **Mechanism:** JAK1/2 inhibitor; approved for myelofibrosis, PV, GVHD.  
- **Suggested range:** **1 nM – 10 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Synergistic with FLT3/BCL2 inhibitors and cytokine‑driven AML; IC₅₀ often in mid‑nM to low‑µM.

---

## 9. Ibrutinib  
- **Mechanism:** BTK inhibitor; FDA‑approved for multiple B‑cell malignancies. Some off‑target ITK, TEC, EGFR.  
- **Suggested range:** **10 nM – 20 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Limited but positive reports of AML activity and signaling interference; expect IC₅₀ in high‑nM to µM.

---

## 10. Auranofin  
- **Mechanism:** Oral gold complex; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor; approved for rheumatoid arthritis. Induces oxidative stress.  
- **Suggested range:** **5 nM – 10 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Preclinical AML data show apoptosis induction; AML IC₅₀ commonly ~0.1–2 µM.

---

## 11. Disulfiram (± copper)  
- **Mechanism:** ALDH inhibitor; forms Cu‑complexes inhibiting proteasome/NPL4; alcoholism treatment.  
- **Suggested range:**  
  - **Disulfiram alone:** **50 nM – 5 µM**  
  - **With Cu²⁺:** 0.5–2 µM DSF + 0.5–2 µM CuCl₂ (1:1–1:2 molar)  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Robust literature in AML; MOLM‑13 typically sensitive in low‑µM range, especially with copper.

---

## 12. Metformin  
- **Mechanism:** AMPK activator; complex I inhibitor; diabetes drug.  
- **Suggested range:** **0.1 mM – 20 mM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Several AML lines respond at 1–10 mM; use as metabolic stressor or combo agent.

---

## 13. Niclosamide  
- **Mechanism:** Anti‑helminthic; uncouples oxidative phosphorylation; modulates Wnt/STAT3/mTOR.  
- **Suggested range:** **50 nM – 10 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Reported anti‑AML activity via Wnt and mitochondrial disruption; IC₅₀ usually sub‑ to low‑µM.

---

## 14. Mebendazole  
- **Mechanism:** Anti‑helminthic; tubulin polymerization inhibitor, hedgehog pathway modulator.  
- **Suggested range:** **50 nM – 20 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Broad anti‑cancer data; AML lines generally respond around 1–10 µM.

---

## 15. Clofazimine  
- **Mechanism:** Leprosy drug; redox‑cycling, ROS generation, membrane effects.  
- **Suggested range:** **10 nM – 10 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Reported to induce apoptosis and inhibit proliferation in leukemia models; expect IC₅₀ in sub‑ to low‑µM.

---

## 16. Chloroquine / Hydroxychloroquine  
- **Mechanism:** Lysosomotropic autophagy inhibitor; antimalarial/anti‑rheumatic.  
- **Suggested range:** **0.5 µM – 50 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Often used to block autophagy in AML and tested in combos; single‑agent IC₅₀ usually 10–30 µM.

---

## 17. Bortezomib  
- **Mechanism:** 26S proteasome inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma and mantle cell lymphoma.  
- **Suggested range:** **0.5 nM – 500 nM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Well‑documented anti‑AML effect; most AML lines (incl. MOLM‑13) show IC₅₀ ~5–50 nM.

---

## 18. Carfilzomib  
- **Mechanism:** Irreversible proteasome inhibitor; multiple myeloma drug.  
- **Suggested range:** **0.5 nM – 500 nM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Potent in AML preclinical models; IC₅₀ usually in low‑ to mid‑nM.

---

## 19. Panobinostat  
- **Mechanism:** Pan‑HDAC inhibitor; approved for multiple myeloma.  
- **Suggested range:** **0.5 nM – 1 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Strong pro‑apoptotic effect in AML; typical IC₅₀ for AML lines in 5–100 nM range.

---

## 20. Vorinostat (SAHA)  
- **Mechanism:** HDAC inhibitor; approved for CTCL.  
- **Suggested range:** **10 nM – 10 µM**  
- **AML/MOLM‑13:** Extensively tested in AML; IC₅₀ usually ~0.5–5 µM in cell lines, lower in combinations.

---

### Practical IC₅₀ Assay Notes (for MOLM‑13)

- **Cell density:** 2–5 × 10⁴ cells/well in 96‑well plates in RPMI‑1640 + 10% FBS (or your standard medium).  
- **Exposure time:** 48–72 h is common for proliferation/viability readouts (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, resazurin).  
- **Vehicle:** Keep DMSO or ethanol at constant final concentration (≤0.1%) across all wells, including controls.  
- **Controls:**  
  - Positive control for cytotoxicity (e.g., high‑dose bortezomib or staurosporine).  
  - Untreated and vehicle controls.  
- **Curve design:** 8–10 serial dilutions (half‑log or 3‑fold) spanning the ranges above should allow robust IC₅₀ fitting.
