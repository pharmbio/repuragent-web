## Notes to make these ranges actually “IC₅₀-testable” in MOLM‑13

**Assay timing**
- Many targeted inhibitors show clearer separation at **72 h** in MOLM‑13.
- Hypomethylating agents (azacitidine/decitabine) may require **96–120 h** or repeated dosing for maximal effect; still usable at 72 h but expect flatter curves.

**Solubility / vehicle constraints**
- Keep **DMSO ≤0.1%** (ideally ≤0.05%) across the plate.
- **Niclosamide** and some HDAC inhibitors can precipitate—confirm visually and/or via nephelometry.

**Practical plate design**
- Use a **top concentration** that’s high enough to reach a plateau but not so high it creates nonspecific toxicity:
  - For “strong” kinase/HDAC/proteasome inhibitors: top at **0.5–10 µM** is usually plenty.
  - For weak metabolic drugs (metformin, valproate): you need **mM** windows.

**Controls**
- Positive kill control: e.g., **staurosporine (0.01–1 µM)** or **doxorubicin (0.001–1 µM)**.
- Include **cell-free wells** for background subtraction.
