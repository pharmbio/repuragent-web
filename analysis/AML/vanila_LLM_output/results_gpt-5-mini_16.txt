Top 20 repurposing candidates for AML (rationale + qualitative potency)
1) Venetoclax (BCL‑2 inhibitor)
   - Rationale: Proven single‑agent and combination activity in AML; clinically approved combinations with hypomethylating agents. Effective in BCL‑2 dependent AML models.
   - Reported potency: typically high (nanomolar) in sensitive AML models.

2) Gilteritinib (FLT3 inhibitor)
   - Rationale: Potent, clinically approved FLT3 inhibitor for FLT3‑mutant AML; active against FLT3‑ITD models.
   - Reported potency: nanomolar against FLT3‑driven lines.

3) Quizartinib (FLT3 inhibitor)
   - Rationale: Highly selective FLT3 inhibitor with strong activity in FLT3‑ITD AML models.
   - Reported potency: nanomolar in FLT3‑ITD cell lines.

4) Midostaurin (multikinase/FLT3 inhibitor)
   - Rationale: Approved in combination therapy for FLT3‑mutant AML; activity in FLT3‑ITD models.
   - Reported potency: nanomolar to low micromolar depending on context.

5) Crenolanib (FLT3 inhibitor)
   - Rationale: Potent against both ITD and certain TKD FLT3 mutants; active in preclinical AML models.
   - Reported potency: nanomolar in many studies.

6) Ponatinib (multi‑kinase inhibitor)
   - Rationale: Broad kinase inhibition including FLT3; preclinical activity in FLT3‑ITD AML.
   - Reported potency: nanomolar to low micromolar.

7) Selinexor (XPO1/CRM1 inhibitor)
   - Rationale: Nuclear export inhibitor with preclinical and clinical AML activity; induces apoptosis across AML models.
   - Reported potency: low nanomolar to micromolar (variable across cell lines).

8) Pinometostat / EPZ‑5676 (DOT1L inhibitor)
   - Rationale: Targets DOT1L methyltransferase; rationale strongest in MLL‑rearranged AML (MOLM‑13 is MLL‑rearranged).
   - Reported potency: active at low micromolar to nanomolar ranges in MLL‑rearranged models.

9) S63845 (MCL‑1 inhibitor; investigational)
   - Rationale: Targets MCL‑1, which contributes to venetoclax resistance; potent single‑agent activity in AML preclinical models.
   - Reported potency: nanomolar in sensitive AML lines.

10) Panobinostat (pan‑HDAC inhibitor)
    - Rationale: HDAC inhibition induces differentiation/apoptosis in AML; investigated in AML trials and combinations.
    - Reported potency: micromolar in many AML lines.

11) Vorinostat / SAHA (HDAC inhibitor)
    - Rationale: Clinically used HDAC inhibitor with preclinical AML activity and combination potential.
    - Reported potency: micromolar.

12) Auranofin (antirheumatic; thioredoxin reductase inhibitor)
    - Rationale: Repurposing candidate with pro‑oxidant and proteostasis effects; reported cytotoxicity in AML lines in screens.
    - Reported potency: low micromolar in many reports.

13) Niclosamide (antihelminthic)
    - Rationale: Identified in drug repurposing screens for anticancer effects (Wnt/STAT/mTOR modulation); reported AML cell inhibition.
    - Reported potency: micromolar (varies by cell line).

14) Disulfiram (antialcoholism drug; often assessed with copper)
    - Rationale: Proteasome/aldehyde dehydrogenase and other effects; reported AML cytotoxicity in preclinical studies (enhanced with copper).
    - Reported potency: micromolar (condition dependent).

15) Itraconazole (antifungal)
    - Rationale: Hedgehog and mTOR pathway modulation; reported anticancer activity in some hematologic models and screens.
    - Reported potency: micromolar.

16) Mebendazole (anthelmintic)
    - Rationale: Microtubule disruptor with repurposing reports showing activity in various cancers including hematologic models.
    - Reported potency: micromolar.

17) Statins (e.g., simvastatin)
    - Rationale: Mevalonate pathway inhibition can impair AML growth and sensitize cells to other agents in preclinical studies.
    - Reported potency: micromolar (often higher than targeted kinase inhibitors).

18) Metformin (antidiabetic)
    - Rationale: Alters cellular metabolism and mTOR/AMPK pathways; reported growth inhibitory effects in AML models (often at higher concentrations).
    - Reported potency: typically higher (micromolar to millimolar ranges in vitro).

19) Sertraline (SSRI antidepressant)
    - Rationale: Identified in drug screens with reported cytotoxicity in AML lines via lysosomal/mitochondrial effects.
    - Reported potency: micromolar.

20) Clofazimine (anti‑leprosy)
    - Rationale: Immunomodulatory and anticancer activity reported in screens and preclinical hematologic models.
    - Reported potency: micromolar.
