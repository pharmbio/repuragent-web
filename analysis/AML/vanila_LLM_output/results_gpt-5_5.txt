=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below are 20 approved, non-AML drugs with published anti-AML activity (many specifically reported in FLT3-ITD lines such as MOLM-13/MV4-11). For each, I list a concise rationale and a concentration window that typically brackets reported cellular IC50/GI50 values and is suitable as a first-pass range when determining an IC50 in MOLM-13. Actual potency can vary with assay format and conditions; verify solubility and adjust as needed.

- Sorafenib (multi-kinase; FLT3-ITD active)
  Suggested test range: 0.1–100 nM
  Note: MOLM-13/MV4-11 often show low-nM sensitivity.

- Ponatinib (multi-kinase; FLT3-ITD active)
  Suggested test range: 0.5–200 nM
  Note: Frequently single-digit to tens of nM in FLT3-ITD AML cells.

- Cabozantinib (multi-kinase; FLT3 inhibition reported)
  Suggested test range: 0.5–500 nM
  Note: Low-nM activity reported in FLT3-ITD AML lines.

- Sunitinib (multi-kinase; FLT3 inhibition)
  Suggested test range: 1–5,000 nM
  Note: Typically tens to low hundreds of nM in FLT3-ITD AML.

- Regorafenib (multi-kinase; FLT3 inhibition)
  Suggested test range: 1–5,000 nM
  Note: Often low- to mid-nM activity versus FLT3-ITD AML cells.

- Dasatinib (SRC/ABL family; reported AML activity)
  Suggested test range: 10–5,000 nM
  Note: Activity reported in subsets of AML lines, including MLL- or SRC-dependent contexts.

- Trametinib (MEK1/2 inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 1–1,000 nM
  Note: RAS/MAPK-driven AML models are more responsive; may be cytostatic.

- Panobinostat (HDAC inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 0.5–100 nM
  Note: Broad AML potency commonly in low-nM.

- Vorinostat/SAHA (HDAC inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 0.1–20 μM
  Note: AML lines typically respond in sub- to low-μM.

- Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 0.5–100 nM
  Note: AML cell viability often inhibited at low-nM.

- Selinexor (XPO1 inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 10–5,000 nM
  Note: Many AML lines show IC50 in tens–hundreds of nM.

- Auranofin (thioredoxin reductase inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 0.1–5 μM
  Note: Reported sub- to low-μM activity in AML (incl. FLT3-ITD models).

- Tigecycline (mitochondrial translation inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 0.1–50 μM
  Note: Targets leukemia stem/OXPHOS dependence; low-μM in AML lines.

- Atovaquone (ETC inhibitor; OXPHOS)
  Suggested test range: 0.1–50 μM
  Note: Low- to mid-μM growth inhibition in AML cells, including FLT3-ITD.

- Nelfinavir (HIV protease inhibitor; ER stress/PI3K-AKT effects)
  Suggested test range: 0.2–50 μM
  Note: Typically low- to mid-μM in AML.

- Niclosamide (anthelmintic; Wnt/STAT3/OXPHOS)
  Suggested test range: 0.01–10 μM
  Note: Often sub-μM in leukemia cells; watch solubility.

- Disulfiram (ALDH inhibitor; proteasome/copper-dependent oxidative stress)
  Suggested test range: 0.01–10 μM
  Note: AML activity commonly sub- to low-μM in vitro.

- Leflunomide (DHODH inhibitor prodrug)
  Suggested test range: 1–300 μM
  Note: Differentiation/anti-proliferative effects; AML cell IC50s often in tens of μM.

- Pitavastatin (HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 0.1–50 μM
  Note: Statins show low-μM AML cytotoxicity via mevalonate-pathway dependence.

- Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2 inhibitor)
  Suggested test range: 0.1–50 μM
  Note: Stronger effects in JAK/STAT–dependent contexts; single-agent potency in MOLM-13 may be modest but useful in combinations.

Notes
- MOLM-13 is FLT3-ITD–driven; FLT3-active TKIs (sorafenib, ponatinib, cabozantinib, sunitinib, regorafenib) are often among the most potent single agents.
- The suggested ranges span roughly 0.1× to 100× typical reported cellular IC50s to capture full dose–response curves in MOLM-13–like systems.
- Assay results depend on medium, serum, exposure time, and readout; confirm compound solubility and stability in your conditions and include an internal positive control known to kill MOLM-13 (for example, a potent FLT3 inhibitor).
