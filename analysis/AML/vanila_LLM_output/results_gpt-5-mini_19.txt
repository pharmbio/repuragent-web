Top 20 repurposing candidates for AML (especially relevant to FLT3‑ITD MOLM13)

1. Gilteritinib
- Mechanism: potent FLT3/AXL inhibitor (approved for relapsed/refractory FLT3‑mutated AML).
- Rationale: clinical and preclinical activity against FLT3‑ITD AML; MOLM13 is commonly sensitive to FLT3 inhibition.

2. Quizartinib
- Mechanism: selective FLT3 inhibitor (FLT3‑ITD potency).
- Rationale: potent preclinical activity in FLT3‑ITD models and clinical trials in FLT3‑ITD AML.

3. Midostaurin
- Mechanism: multi‑kinase / first‑generation FLT3 inhibitor.
- Rationale: approved in combination with chemotherapy for newly diagnosed FLT3‑mutant AML; active against FLT3‑ITD in vitro.

4. Sorafenib
- Mechanism: multi‑kinase inhibitor (including FLT3).
- Rationale: off‑label/clinical reports and in vitro studies show activity against FLT3‑mutant AML cell lines.

5. Ponatinib
- Mechanism: multi‑kinase inhibitor with FLT3 activity.
- Rationale: preclinical activity against FLT3‑ITD and certain resistant FLT3 mutants.

6. Venetoclax
- Mechanism: BCL‑2 inhibitor.
- Rationale: strong clinical activity in AML in combination regimens; many AML cell lines (including FLT3‑mutant) are sensitive to BCL‑2 inhibition.

7. Alvocidib (flavopiridol)
- Mechanism: CDK9/CDK inhibitor (transcriptional repression via MCL‑1 reduction).
- Rationale: preclinical and clinical studies in AML show single‑agent and combination activity; synergizes with BCL‑2 inhibitors in some models.

8. Volasertib
- Mechanism: PLK1 inhibitor.
- Rationale: preclinical activity in AML and clinical development; PLK1 is a validated mitotic target in AML.

9. Panobinostat
- Mechanism: pan‑HDAC inhibitor.
- Rationale: HDAC inhibitors show cytotoxicity and can sensitize AML cells to other agents in vitro and clinical trials.

10. Vorinostat (SAHA)
- Mechanism: HDAC inhibitor.
- Rationale: preclinical AML activity; explored in combination strategies.

11. Decitabine / Azacitidine
- Mechanism: DNA hypomethylating agents.
- Rationale: standard therapies in AML and MDS; useful backbone for combination repurposing strategies.

12. Auranofin
- Mechanism: thioredoxin reductase inhibitor / redox poison.
- Rationale: reported preclinical AML cytotoxicity and early clinical interest in hematologic malignancies.

13. Disulfiram (with copper)
- Mechanism: multiple proposed mechanisms (aldehyde dehydrogenase inhibition, proteasome inhibition with Cu).
- Rationale: reported in vitro anticancer activity across tumor types including leukemia models.

14. Niclosamide
- Mechanism: uncoupler / Wnt/STAT/mitochondrial effects depending on context.
- Rationale: broad reported anticancer activity in preclinical studies; some AML models sensitive.

15. Mebendazole
- Mechanism: microtubule‑binding antiparasitic.
- Rationale: repurposed as anticancer agent in preclinical leukemia models (disrupts microtubules, induces apoptosis).

16. Metformin
- Mechanism: mitochondrial complex I inhibitor / AMPK activator.
- Rationale: metabolic modulation can impair leukemia cell survival and sensitize to other agents in vitro.

17. Simvastatin (and other statins)
- Mechanism: HMG‑CoA reductase inhibitor (mevalonate pathway).
- Rationale: proapoptotic/antiproliferative effects reported in AML preclinical studies and explored in combination.

18. Doxycycline
- Mechanism: mitochondrial translation inhibitor (at higher concentrations relevant to mitochondria).
- Rationale: reported to target leukemia stem cell–like compartments and mitochondrial function in AML models.

19. Ibrutinib
- Mechanism: BTK/TEC family kinase inhibitor.
- Rationale: some preclinical reports of activity in AML subsets and as part of combination strategies; variable single‑agent activity.

20. Ruxolitinib
- Mechanism: JAK1/2 inhibitor.
- Rationale: activity in some myeloproliferative and AML contexts, particularly with JAK pathway activation or cooperating mutations.

Notes and practical considerations
- Many of the most potent effects in MOLM13 and related FLT3‑ITD lines are seen with FLT3 inhibitors (gilteritinib, quizartinib, midostaurin, sorafenib, ponatinib). BCL‑2 inhibition (venetoclax) and CDK9 inhibition (alvocidib) are also well‑supported strategies in AML.
- Several of the listed agents are approved cancer drugs (gilteritinib, midostaurin, venetoclax), while others are non‑oncology drugs being investigated for anticancer activity (disulfiram, niclosamide, mebendazole, metformin, statins).
- Published potency (IC50) values vary by assay format (cell viability vs. proliferation vs. apoptosis), exposure time, medium, and cell line passage; published IC50s for a given drug can range across orders of magnitude between studies.
