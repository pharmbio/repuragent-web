=== AML Drug Repurposing Suggestions ===

Prompt:
Suggest top 20 existing drugs that could be repurposed for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) treatment 
and provide experimentally testable concentrations for an IC50 assay. 
The drug should inhibit the proliferation of AML cell lines, particularly MOLM13.


==================================================

Results:

Below is a **shortlist of 20 clinically used drugs** with literature support or strong rationale for **repurposing in AML (especially FLT3-ITD+ lines like MOLM‑13)**, plus **practical IC₅₀ assay ranges**.

**Important notes (read first):**  
- Start with a **10‑point or 8‑point half‑log dilution series** centered around reported IC₅₀ or the mid‑range below.  
- Final **DMSO ≤0.1–0.2%**.  
- For MOLM13 or other AML cell lines, 72 h exposure is common for proliferation/viability IC₅₀ (e.g., CellTiter‑Glo, MTS, resazurin).  
- Concentration ranges below are **starting suggestions** based on in vitro literature in leukemia and other cancers; always do a pilot to adjust.

---

## 1. Sorafenib  
- Class: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, RAF, VEGFR, PDGFR).  
- Rationale: Known activity in FLT3‑ITD AML; MOLM‑13 is FLT3‑ITD+.  
- Suggested test range (MOLM13): **0.5 nM – 5 µM** (e.g., 0.5, 1, 2, 5, 10, 50, 100, 500, 1000, 5000 nM).  

## 2. Sunitinib  
- Class: Multikinase inhibitor (FLT3, KIT, PDGFR, VEGFR).  
- Rationale: Inhibits FLT3; shown anti‑proliferative in AML lines.  
- Range: **1 nM – 10 µM**.  

## 3. Dasatinib  
- Class: BCR‑ABL/Src family TKI.  
- Rationale: SRC and related kinases relevant to AML survival signaling.  
- Range: **0.5 nM – 5 µM**.  

## 4. Nilotinib  
- Class: BCR‑ABL TKI.  
- Rationale: Off‑target inhibition of KIT/PDGFR; some AML data.  
- Range: **1 nM – 10 µM**.  

## 5. Imatinib  
- Class: BCR‑ABL, KIT, PDGFR TKI.  
- Rationale: Activity in KIT‑mutated myeloid disease; partial effects in some AML lines.  
- Range: **10 nM – 20 µM**.  

## 6. Crizotinib  
- Class: ALK/MET/ROS1 inhibitor.  
- Rationale: MET/ALK pathways can support AML survival; some preclinical AML data.  
- Range: **10 nM – 20 µM**.  

## 7. Ibrutinib  
- Class: BTK inhibitor.  
- Rationale: BTK and B‑cell receptor–like signaling implicated in some myeloid contexts; emerging AML data.  
- Range: **10 nM – 20 µM**.  

## 8. Venetoclax  
- Class: BCL‑2 inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Approved for AML (but originally CLL); strong activity in many AML lines, including MOLM13.  
- Range: **0.1 nM – 1 µM** (MOLM13 is often very sensitive).  

## 9. Ruxolitinib  
- Class: JAK1/2 inhibitor.  
- Rationale: JAK/STAT signaling important in subsets of AML, especially with cytokine‑driven growth.  
- Range: **10 nM – 20 µM**.  

## 10. Tofacitinib  
- Class: JAK inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Similar rationale as ruxolitinib for cytokine/JAK‑STAT dependence.  
- Range: **10 nM – 20 µM**.  

## 11. Disulfiram (± copper)  
- Class: ALDH inhibitor; proteasome/NPL4 modulator (repurposed anticancer candidate).  
- Rationale: ALDH and proteostasis relevant in AML stem/progenitor cells. Activity often enhanced with Cu²⁺.  
- Range: **50 nM – 10 µM** disulfiram; if using copper, add **1–2 µM CuCl₂**.  

## 12. Metformin  
- Class: Biguanide antidiabetic.  
- Rationale: AMPK activation, mTOR modulation; reported anti‑leukemic effects at higher in vitro doses.  
- Range: **0.1 mM – 20 mM** (wide, because IC₅₀ often in the low‑mM region).  

## 13. Chloroquine / Hydroxychloroquine  
- Class: Autophagy inhibitor, antimalarial.  
- Rationale: AML cells can depend on autophagy; CQ/HQ sensitize to other drugs and have direct effects at higher doses.  
- Range: **0.5 µM – 50 µM**.  

## 14. propranolol  
- Class: Non‑selective β‑blocker.  
- Rationale: Adrenergic signaling affects leukemia niche and cell survival; some direct antiproliferative data in hematologic malignancies.  
- Range: **1 µM – 100 µM**.  

## 15. Fluvastatin (or simvastatin/atorvastatin as alternatives)  
- Class: HMG‑CoA reductase inhibitor (statin).  
- Rationale: Mevalonate pathway crucial for leukemic cell survival; statins show AML cytotoxicity.  
- Range (fluvastatin): **0.1 µM – 50 µM**.  
  - For simvastatin or atorvastatin: **0.01 µM – 10 µM** is often sufficient.  

## 16. Thalidomide / Lenalidomide  
- Class: Immunomodulatory drugs (IMiDs).  
- Rationale: Cereblon‑mediated degradation of transcription factors and microenvironment modulation; some direct AML line effects.  
- Range:  
  - Thalidomide: **1 µM – 100 µM**  
  - Lenalidomide: **0.1 µM – 50 µM**  

## 17. Auranofin  
- Class: Gold compound, anti‑rheumatic.  
- Rationale: Inhibits thioredoxin reductase; induces ROS and apoptosis in AML cells.  
- Range: **5 nM – 10 µM**.  

## 18. Mebendazole  
- Class: Anthelmintic (microtubule disruptor).  
- Rationale: Tubulin disruption, hedgehog pathway effects; preclinical AML and other cancer data.  
- Range: **0.1 µM – 20 µM**.  

## 19. Niclosamide  
- Class: Anthelmintic; mitochondrial uncoupler, Wnt/STAT3 inhibitor.  
- Rationale: Wnt and STAT3 active in AML; niclosamide has broad anti‑cancer activity.  
- Range: **0.1 µM – 10 µM**.  

## 20. Clofazimine  
- Class: Leprosy drug; affects mitochondrial function, ROS, and ion channels.  
- Rationale: Shown anti‑leukemic activity in preclinical models; targets oxidative metabolism.  
- Range: **0.05 µM – 10 µM**.  

---

### Minimal experimental design suggestions

- **Cells**: MOLM‑13 (plus at least one other AML line for comparison, e.g., OCI‑AML3).  
- **Density**: 3–5×10³ cells/well in 96‑well plates.  
- **Assay time**: 72 h for IC₅₀; include 24 and 48 h for kinetic insight if possible.  
- **Readout**: ATP‑based (CellTiter‑Glo) or metabolic (MTS/WST‑1).  
- **Controls**:  
  - Vehicle (0.1% DMSO)  
  - Positive control: known active AML drug at effective dose (e.g., mid‑nM venetoclax or low‑nM sorafenib).  

If you tell me what you already have in your chemical library and whether you’re aiming more at single‑agent activity or combination testing (e.g., with cytarabine/venetoclax), I can narrow this list to a prioritized panel and give you exact dilution plates.
